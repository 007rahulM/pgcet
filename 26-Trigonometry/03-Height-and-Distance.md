# Height and Distance (Trigonometric Applications)

> Appears in **1–2 questions** per PGCET paper. Always involves a right triangle with an angle of elevation or depression.

---

## PART 1 — Key Terms

```
Angle of Elevation: The angle above the horizontal when looking UP at an object.
Angle of Depression: The angle below the horizontal when looking DOWN at an object.
```

```
         Object (top of tower)
            /|
           / |  Height
          /  |
         /θ  |
---------/---+--------
      Observer    
      ←  distance →
```

**tan θ = Height / Distance** (from the right triangle)

---

## PART 2 — The THREE Formulas to Remember

```
sin θ = Opposite / Hypotenuse = Height / Line-of-sight
cos θ = Adjacent / Hypotenuse = Distance / Line-of-sight
tan θ = Opposite / Adjacent  = Height / Distance
```

> **For HEIGHT & DISTANCE problems: Use TAN most of the time!**

---

## PART 3 — Standard Solved Examples

### Example 1 (from 2024 paper): Pole and Shadow
**A pole is 2√3 metres high. Its shadow is 2 metres long. Angle of elevation of sun?**

```
        Pole top
         |\ 
    2√3  |  \  
         |   \  Line of sight
         |    \
         |   θ \
---------+------→
         2 m
```

- tan θ = height/shadow = 2√3/2 = √3
- θ = tan⁻¹(√3) = **60°**

### Example 2 (from 2025 paper): Ladder Against a Wall
**A ladder makes 60° angle with the ground. Foot is 4.6 m from the wall. Length of ladder?**

```
      wall
       |\ 
       |  \
       |    \ ladder (L)
       |      \
       |    60°\
-------+--------
       ← 4.6m →
```

- cos 60° = adjacent/hypotenuse = 4.6/L
- 1/2 = 4.6/L
- L = 4.6 × 2 = **9.2 m**

> 💡 **Use cos when you have the adjacent (horizontal) side and want the hypotenuse (ladder).**

### Example 3: Tower and Distance
**From 30 m away, the angle of elevation of a tower is 30°. Height of tower?**
- tan 30° = height/distance
- 1/√3 = h/30
- h = 30/√3 = **10√3 m**

---

## PART 4 — Two-Observation Problems

**When observed from two different distances:**

If angle changes from α to β as you move closer by d metres:

```
Height = d × tan α × tan β / (tan β − tan α)
```

---

## PART 5 — Key Angle Values for Height & Distance

| θ | sin θ | cos θ | tan θ |
|---|-------|-------|-------|
| 30° | 1/2 | √3/2 | 1/√3 ≈ 0.577 |
| 45° | 1/√2 | 1/√2 | 1 |
| 60° | √3/2 | 1/2 | √3 ≈ 1.73 |

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Method

1. **Draw the right triangle** (always!)
2. **Label:** angle θ, opposite side, adjacent side, hypotenuse
3. **Choose the right formula:**
   - Height/shadow → use **tan**
   - Ladder/hypotenuse + ground distance → use **cos**
   - Ladder/hypotenuse + height → use **sin**
4. **Solve the equation**

## 📝 Practice Problems

**Q1.** A ladder leans against a wall. Angle with ground = 60°. Foot is 4.6 m from wall. Length of ladder?
- (A) 2.3 m  (B) 4.6 m  (C) 7.8 m  (D) 9.2 m

**Q2.** A pole is 2√3 m high. Shadow is 2 m. Angle of elevation of sun?
- (A) 30°  (B) 45°  (C) 60°  (D) 90°

**Q3.** From a point 20 m from base of a tower, angle of elevation is 30°. Height of tower?
- (A) 10√3 m  (B) 20/√3 m  (C) 10 m  (D) 20√3 m

**Q4.** If angle of elevation = 45°, height = 50 m. Distance from base = ?
- (A) 25 m  (B) 50 m  (C) 50√2 m  (D) 100 m

---

## ✔️ Answers

| Q | A | Reason |
|---|---|--------|
| Q1 | (D) 9.2 m | cos60° = 4.6/L → L = 4.6/0.5 = 9.2 |
| Q2 | (C) 60° | tan θ = 2√3/2 = √3 → θ = 60° |
| Q3 | (B) 20/√3 | tan30° = h/20 → h = 20×(1/√3) = 20/√3 = 20√3/3 ≈ 11.5 m |
| Q4 | (B) 50 m | tan45° = 1 = 50/d → d = 50 m |
