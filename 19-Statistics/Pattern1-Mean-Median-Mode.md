# Pattern 1: Mean, Median, and Mode

## 🔍 When This Comes Up

- "Find the average / mean of..."
- "Find the middle value (median) of..."
- "Which value appears most (mode)?"
- "Central tendency" questions

---

## 📐 Measures of Central Tendency

### 1. Mean (Average)
> **Mean = Sum of all values / Number of values**

**Example:** Find mean of 4, 7, 2, 9, 8
- Sum = 30, Count = 5
- Mean = 30/5 = **6** ✅

**Shortcut for AP:** If values are in arithmetic progression, **mean = middle value**
- Example: 10, 20, 30, 40, 50 → Mean = 30 (middle value) ✅

---

### 2. Median (Middle Value)
> **Median = Middle value when data is sorted**

**For ODD number of values:**
- Sort the data
- Median = value at position **(n+1)/2**

**For EVEN number of values:**
- Sort the data
- Median = average of values at positions **n/2** and **(n/2)+1**

**Example 1 (odd, n=5):** Find median of 3, 7, 1, 9, 5
- Sorted: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9
- Position = (5+1)/2 = 3rd value = **5** ✅

**Example 2 (even, n=4):** Find median of 2, 8, 4, 6
- Sorted: 2, 4, 6, 8
- Positions 2 and 3: values 4 and 6
- Median = (4+6)/2 = **5** ✅

---

### 3. Mode (Most Frequent)
> **Mode = Value that appears most often**

**Example:** 3, 5, 7, 3, 8, 3, 9, 5
- 3 appears 3 times (most)
- Mode = **3** ✅

**Special cases:**
- **No mode:** If all values appear equally often
- **Bimodal:** Two values appear most (two modes)

---

## 📐 Relationship Between Mean, Median, Mode

For symmetric distributions (like normal distribution):
> **Mean = Median = Mode**

For skewed data (approximate):
> **Mode ≈ 3 × Median − 2 × Mean** (Karl Pearson's empirical relation)

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Finding missing value from mean)

**Problem:** The mean of 5 numbers is 10. If one number is added, new mean = 11. Find the new number.

- Old sum = 5 × 10 = 50
- New sum = 6 × 11 = 66
- New number = 66 − 50 = **16** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Split mean)

**Problem:** Mean of 20 items = 40. Mean of first 10 = 35. Find mean of last 10.

- Total sum = 20 × 40 = 800
- First 10 sum = 10 × 35 = 350
- Last 10 sum = 800 − 350 = 450
- Mean of last 10 = 450/10 = **45** ✅

---

### Example 3 (All three measures)

**Dataset:** 4, 2, 4, 5, 2, 4, 7, 2, 4

- Sort: 2, 2, 2, 4, 4, 4, 4, 5, 7
- **Mean** = (4+2+4+5+2+4+7+2+4)/9 = 34/9 ≈ 3.78
- **Median** = middle value (n=9, position=5) = **4**
- **Mode** = 4 (appears 4 times) = **4** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcuts

| Measure | Quick Method |
|---------|-------------|
| Mean | Sum ÷ Count |
| Median (odd n) | Sort, take middle |
| Median (even n) | Sort, average of 2 middles |
| Mode | Most frequent value |

**Key Properties:**
- Adding constant c to all values: Mean increases by c, Median increases by c, Mode increases by c
- Adding constant does NOT change SD (spread)
- Multiplying all by k: Mean multiplied by k
