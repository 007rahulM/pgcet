## PART 1 — SKEWNESS

**Skewness** measures the **asymmetry** of a distribution around its mean.

### Three Types of Distributions

| Distribution | Relationship | Shape |
|-------------|-------------|-------|
| **Symmetrical (Normal)** | Mean = Median = Mode | Bell curve, balanced |
| **Positively Skewed** | Mean > Median > Mode | Long right tail |
| **Negatively Skewed** | Mean < Median < Mode | Long left tail |

### Memory Trick:
> **Positive skew** = tail extends to the **right** (positive side)
> **Negative skew** = tail extends to the **left** (negative side)

---

### Quick Rule: Pearson's Skewness Formula

```
Skewness = (Mean − Mode) / Standard Deviation
```

Or alternatively:
```
Skewness = 3(Mean − Median) / Standard Deviation
```

- **Positive value** → Positively skewed
- **Negative value** → Negatively skewed
- **Zero** → Symmetrical

---

## 📐 Worked Example (from PGCET 2025)

**Q:** A frequency distribution of variable x with mean 45 is a skewed distribution.

> If Mean > Mode → **Positively skewed** distribution

This type of question asks you to identify the type of distribution given the relationship between mean, median, and mode.

**Key pattern:**
- Mean > Median > Mode → **Positively skewed**
- Mean < Median < Mode → **Negatively skewed**
- Mean = Median = Mode → **Symmetrical**

## PART 2 — KURTOSIS

**Kurtosis** measures the **peakedness** (how peaked or flat) a distribution is compared to a normal distribution.

| Type | Name | Shape |
|------|------|-------|
| Normal kurtosis (= 3) | **Mesokurtic** | Normal bell curve |
| High kurtosis (> 3) | **Leptokurtic** | Very peaked, heavy tails |
| Low kurtosis (< 3) | **Platykurtic** | Flat, thin tails |

### Memory Trick:
- **Lepto**kurtic = **Slender** (tall, thin peak) — "Lepto" means slender
- **Platy**kurtic = **Flat** — "Platy" means flat/broad
- **Meso**kurtic = **Middle** — normal distribution

## 📝 Practice Questions

**Q1.** In a distribution, Mean = 50, Median = 45, Mode = 35. The distribution is:
- (A) Positively skewed  (B) Negatively skewed  (C) Symmetrical  (D) Mesokurtic

**Answer: (A) Positively skewed** (Mean > Median > Mode)

---

**Q2.** If Mean = Mode = Median, the distribution is:
- (A) Leptokurtic  (B) Platykurtic  (C) Symmetrical  **(C) Symmetrical**

---

**Q3.** A distribution where the tail is on the left side is called:
- (A) Positively skewed  **(B) Negatively skewed**  (C) Normal  (D) Leptokurtic

---

**Q4.** A distribution more peaked than the normal distribution is called:
- (A) Platykurtic  **(B) Leptokurtic**  (C) Mesokurtic  (D) Symmetrical

---

**Q5.** Pearson's coefficient of skewness for a data set is −0.5. This means:
- (A) Positively skewed  **(B) Negatively skewed**  (C) Symmetrical  (D) Bimodal

## ⚡ Quick Summary Table

| Term | Key Feature | PGCET Clue |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| Positively skewed | Mean > Median > Mode | Right tail longer |
| Negatively skewed | Mean < Median < Mode | Left tail longer |
| Symmetrical | Mean = Median = Mode | Perfect bell shape |
| Mesokurtic | Kurtosis = 3 (normal) | Normal distribution |
| Leptokurtic | Kurtosis > 3 | More peaked than normal |
| Platykurtic | Kurtosis < 3 | Flatter than normal |
