# Statistics — Mean, Median, Mode Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** Find the mean of 15, 25, 35, 45, 55.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 5 numbers: 15, 25, 35, 45, 55
- Find: Mean (average)

**💡 What I'll use:** Mean = Sum of all values / Number of values

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Sum = 15 + 25 + 35 + 45 + 55 = 175

Step 2: n = 5

Step 3: Mean = 175 / 5 = **35**

**✅ Answer: 35**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** Find the median of 7, 3, 11, 9, 2, 8, 6.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 7 numbers (unsorted)
- Find: Median (middle value)

**💡 What I'll use:** Sort the data, then pick middle value. For n values, median = ((n+1)/2)th value.

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Sort in ascending order: 2, 3, 6, **7**, 8, 9, 11

Step 2: n = 7 (odd), so median position = (7+1)/2 = 4th value

Step 3: The 4th value is **7**

**✅ Answer: 7**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** Find the mode of 4, 2, 4, 5, 2, 4, 7, 2, 4.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 9 numbers
- Find: Mode (most frequently occurring value)

**💡 What I'll use:** Count frequency of each value; mode = value with highest frequency.

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Sort: 2, 2, 2, 4, 4, 4, 4, 5, 7

Step 2: Count frequencies:
- 2 appears: 3 times
- 4 appears: 4 times
- 5 appears: 1 time
- 7 appears: 1 time

Step 3: 4 has the highest frequency (4 times)

**✅ Answer: Mode = 4**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** The mean of 5 numbers is 10. If a new number is added and the new mean becomes 11, find the new number.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Mean of 5 numbers = 10; after adding one number, mean of 6 = 11
- Find: The new number added

**💡 What I'll use:** Sum = Mean × n; New number = New sum − Old sum

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Old sum = 10 × 5 = 50

Step 2: New sum (6 numbers, mean 11) = 11 × 6 = 66

Step 3: New number = 66 − 50 = **16**

**✅ Answer: 16**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** The mean of 20 numbers is 40. The mean of the first 10 numbers is 35. Find the mean of the last 10 numbers.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Total 20 numbers, overall mean = 40; first 10 mean = 35
- Find: Mean of last 10 numbers

**💡 What I'll use:** Total sum = sum of first 10 + sum of last 10

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Total sum = 40 × 20 = 800

Step 2: Sum of first 10 = 35 × 10 = 350

Step 3: Sum of last 10 = 800 − 350 = 450

Step 4: Mean of last 10 = 450 / 10 = **45**

**✅ Answer: 45**

### Q6

**❓ Question:** Find the median of 12, 7, 19, 3, 15, 9, 21, 5.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 8 numbers (even count)
- Find: Median

**💡 What I'll use:** For even n, median = average of (n/2)th and (n/2 + 1)th values after sorting.

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Sort: 3, 5, 7, **9, 12**, 15, 19, 21

Step 2: n = 8, so median = average of 4th and 5th values

Step 3: 4th = 9, 5th = 12

Step 4: Median = (9 + 12) / 2 = 21 / 2 = **10.5**

**✅ Answer: 10.5**

### Q7

**❓ Question:** Class A has 30 students with mean score 70. Class B has 20 students with mean score 80. Find the combined mean.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Class A: n=30, mean=70; Class B: n=20, mean=80
- Find: Combined mean of all 50 students

**💡 What I'll use:** Combined mean = (Sum A + Sum B) / (nA + nB)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Sum A = 70 × 30 = 2100

Step 2: Sum B = 80 × 20 = 1600

Step 3: Total sum = 2100 + 1600 = 3700

Step 4: Total students = 30 + 20 = 50

Step 5: Combined mean = 3700 / 50 = **74**

**✅ Answer: 74**
