# Pattern 1: Opposite Face Problems

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

- "On a standard die, what face is opposite to 3?"
- "Opposite faces of a die sum to 7. Which face is opposite to 5?"
- "A die is rolled; the top face shows 2. What is on the bottom?"

---

## 🧠 The Golden Rule

> **On a standard die, opposite faces always sum to 7.**
> Pairs: **(1, 6)**, **(2, 5)**, **(3, 4)**

So:
- Opposite of 1 → 6
- Opposite of 2 → 5
- Opposite of 3 → 4
- Opposite of 4 → 3
- Opposite of 5 → 2
- Opposite of 6 → 1

---

## 🎲 Die Face Layout (Standard)

```
        ┌───┐
        │ 5 │     ← top
    ┌───┼───┼───┬───┐
    │ 1 │ 2 │ 3 │ 4 │   ← middle strip (unfolded)
    └───┼───┼───┴───┘
        │ 6 │     ← bottom (wait — standard: 1 opp 6, 2 opp 5, 3 opp 4)
        └───┘
```

Unfolded standard die (cross shape):
```
        [2]
    [1] [5] [6] [3]
        [4]
```
Here 5 is opposite 4's partner — use the **sum-to-7 rule** directly for a standard die.

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**❓ Question:** On a standard die, opposite faces sum to 7. Which face is opposite to 4?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Standard die; opposite faces sum to 7
- Find: The face opposite to 4

**💡 What I'll use:** Sum-to-7 rule: opposite of 4 = 7 − 4 = 3

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Apply the rule
Opposite of 4 = 7 − 4 = **3**

**✅ Answer: 3**

---

### Example 2

**❓ Question:** A die is thrown. The top face shows 6. What is the number on the bottom face?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Top face = 6; standard die
- Find: Bottom face value

**💡 What I'll use:** Top and bottom are opposite faces → sum = 7

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Bottom = 7 − top = 7 − 6 = **1**

**✅ Answer: 1**

---

### Example 3

**❓ Question:** In a standard die, which two faces are opposite to each other?
- (A) 1 and 5  (B) 2 and 4  (C) 3 and 4  (D) 4 and 3

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Standard die
- Find: A correct opposite pair

**💡 What I'll use:** Sum-to-7 rule: valid pairs are (1,6), (2,5), (3,4)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Check each option
- (A) 1+5 = 6 ≠ 7 ✗
- (B) 2+4 = 6 ≠ 7 ✗
- (C) 3+4 = 7 ✓
- (D) Same as (C)

**✅ Answer: (C) 3 and 4**

---

### Example 4

**❓ Question:** A die is rolled. Sum of the top and bottom faces is always:
- (A) 5  (B) 6  (C) 7  (D) 8

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Standard die
- Find: Constant sum of top and bottom (which are opposite) faces

**💡 What I'll use:** Opposite faces of a standard die sum to 7

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Opposite-face sum on standard die = 7 (always)

**✅ Answer: (C) 7**

---

### Example 5

**❓ Question:** A die shows 1 on top and 2 facing you (front). Which number is at the back (opposite to front)?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Top = 1, Front = 2
- Find: The face opposite to front (= back face)

**💡 What I'll use:** Opposite of 2 = 7 − 2 = 5

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Back = opposite of front = 7 − 2 = **5**

**✅ Answer: 5**

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Strategy

1. Standard die? → Use **opposite = 7 − face**.
2. Non-standard die? → Use the views given (see Pattern 2).
3. Three pairs always: **(1,6), (2,5), (3,4)**. Memorise once, use forever.

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. On a standard die, what face is opposite to 2?

2. A die shows 5 on top. What is on the bottom?

3. Which pair is a valid opposite pair on a standard die?
   - (A) 1 and 4  (B) 2 and 5  (C) 3 and 6  (D) 1 and 3

4. A die is thrown. Top = 3, Front = 1. What is the number at the back?

5. The sum of three pairs of opposite faces of a die is:
   - (A) 18  (B) 21  (C) 14  (D) 7

6. On a standard die, which face is opposite to the face showing 1?

7. A die is placed with 6 on top. The bottom face shows:
   - (A) 2  (B) 1  (C) 3  (D) 4

8. If front of a die shows 4 and left shows 5, what is the right face?
   (Hint: Left (5) and right are opposite)
