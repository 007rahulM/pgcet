## 📋 Exam Overview

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Exam Name** | KEA PGCET (MCA) — Post Graduate Common Entrance Test |
| **Conducting Body** | Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) |
| **Total Questions** | 100 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) |
| **Total Marks** | 100 marks (1 mark each) |
| **Time Limit** | 90 minutes |
| **Negative Marking** | No negative marking |
| **Language** | English |
| **Mode** | Online (Computer Based Test) |

> **Time per question:** 90 min ÷ 100 questions = **54 seconds per question**
> Since there's no negative marking — **attempt every question!**

## ⚡ Exam Day Strategy

### Time Allocation
| Section | Questions | Time |
|---------|-----------|------|
| Easy questions (first pass) | ~60 | 40 min |
| Medium questions | ~30 | 35 min |
| Hard/guessing | ~10 | 15 min |
| Review | — | 5 min |

### Rules to Follow
1. **Skip hard questions first time** — come back later
2. **Answer ALL questions** — no negative marking!
3. **Attempt Computer Awareness first** — usually straightforward
4. **Check units** in math problems (km/h vs m/s, etc.)
5. **Eliminate 2 wrong options** before guessing MCQ

## 🔑 Key Insights from Previous Year Papers

1. **Computer Awareness has grown** — from ~20% to ~30% of the paper
2. **Binary/Hexadecimal conversions** appear every year (2–3 Qs)
3. **Time & Work** and **Profit & Loss** are consistent (2–3 Qs each)
4. **Data Structures** (especially Stack, Queue, Tree) asked frequently
5. **SQL queries** (SELECT, WHERE, ORDER BY) appear in DBMS section
6. **Series questions** have increased in recent years
7. **Reading comprehension** passage is always 1 paragraph with 3–4 questions
8. **Probability** and **P&C** together account for 4–6 questions
9. **Mathematical Logic** (truth tables, tautology) is a scoring topic
10. **Direction Sense** and **Blood Relations** are always present
