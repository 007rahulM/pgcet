# Pattern 2: One Leaves — Work Together, Then One Person Leaves — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** A can finish in 12 days, B in 18 days. They work together for 4 days, then A leaves. How many more days for B to finish?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A takes 12 days alone, B takes 18 days alone; they work together for 4 days, then A stops
- Find: Extra days B needs to finish the remaining work alone

**💡 What I'll use:** 6-Step LCM Method — find work done together, subtract from total, divide remaining by B's efficiency

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Total Work = LCM(12, 18) = 36 units

Step 2: Find efficiencies
- A = 36 ÷ 12 = 3 units/day
- B = 36 ÷ 18 = 2 units/day

Step 3: Combined efficiency = 3 + 2 = 5 units/day

Step 4: Work done together in 4 days = 5 × 4 = 20 units

Step 5: Remaining work = 36 − 20 = 16 units

Step 6: B alone finishes remaining = 16 ÷ 2 = 8 days

**✅ Answer: 8 days**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** A and B together finish in 8 days. A alone takes 12 days. They work together for 4 days, then A leaves. How many more days does B need to finish?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Together = 8 days, A alone = 12 days; they work together for 4 days, then A stops
- Find: B alone finishes the remaining work — how many more days?

**💡 What I'll use:** First find B alone using the reverse formula, then apply the 6-Step LCM Method

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find B's solo time
- B alone = (A × Together) / (A − Together) = (12 × 8) / (12 − 8) = 96 / 4 = 24 days

Step 2: Total Work = LCM(12, 24) = 24 units

Step 3: Find efficiencies
- A = 24 ÷ 12 = 2 units/day
- B = 24 ÷ 24 = 1 unit/day
- Combined = 24 ÷ 8 = 3 units/day ✓ (matches 2 + 1)

Step 4: Work done together in 4 days = 3 × 4 = 12 units

Step 5: Remaining work = 24 − 12 = 12 units

Step 6: B alone finishes remaining = 12 ÷ 1 = 12 days

**✅ Answer: 12 days**
