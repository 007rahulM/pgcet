# Pattern 4: Efficiency Ratios — "A is Twice as Efficient as B"

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for:
- "A is **twice** as efficient as B"
- "A and B work in the **ratio 3:4**"
- "A can do in **half the time** of B"

---

## 🧠 The Concept (Why This Works)

**Efficiency and Time are OPPOSITES (Inversely Proportional):**

> If A is **faster** (more efficient), A takes **less time**.
> If A is **slower** (less efficient), A takes **more time**.

**Rule:**
> If efficiency ratio = a : b, then time ratio = b : a

### Example:
- A is twice as efficient as B → Efficiency ratio = 2 : 1
- Time ratio = 1 : 2 → A takes half the time B takes

---

## 📐 Key Rules

| Given | Conclusion |
|-------|-----------|
| A twice as efficient as B | A's time = B's time ÷ 2 |
| A thrice as efficient as B | A's time = B's time ÷ 3 |
| Efficiency A:B = 3:4 | Time A:B = 4:3 |
| Efficiency A:B = m:n | Time A:B = n:m |

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**Problem:** A is twice as efficient as B. B alone takes 18 days. How long if they work together?

**Step 1 — Find A's time:**
- A is twice as efficient → A takes half the time of B
- B = 18 days → A = 18 ÷ 2 = **9 days**

**Step 2 — Together formula:**
- (9 × 18) / (9 + 18) = 162 / 27 = **6 days** ✅

---

### Example 2

**Problem:** A and B have efficiency ratio 3:2. Together they finish in 10 days. How long does A take alone?

**Step 1 — Assign units to efficiency:**
- A's efficiency = 3k units/day, B's efficiency = 2k units/day
- Combined = 5k units/day

**Step 2 — Total work:**
- Together in 10 days → Total work = 10 × 5k = 50k units

**Step 3 — A alone:**
- A's time = 50k ÷ 3k = **50/3 ≈ 16.67 days** ✅

---

### Example 3

**Problem:** A is 3 times as efficient as B. Together they finish in 12 days. How long does B take alone?

**Step 1:**
- Let B's efficiency = 1 unit/day → A's = 3 units/day
- Combined = 4 units/day

**Step 2:**
- Total work = 4 × 12 = 48 units

**Step 3:**
- B alone = 48 ÷ 1 = **48 days** ✅

---

### Example 4 (From KEA-style papers)

**Problem:** A does a piece of work in 8 days that B does in 12 days. In how many days can A do the work that B does in 6 days?

**Step 1 — How much does B do in 6 days?**
- B's rate = 1/12 per day
- B in 6 days = 6/12 = 1/2 of the work

**Step 2 — A takes how long to do 1/2 the work?**
- A's rate = 1/8 per day
- Time = (1/2) ÷ (1/8) = **4 days** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

1. If A is **n times** as efficient as B:
   - A's time = B's time ÷ n
   
2. If efficiency ratio = a:b:
   - Time ratio = b:a (just flip it!)
   
3. For together time when you know individual times:
   - Use formula: (X × Y) / (X + Y)

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. A is twice as efficient as B. B alone takes 24 days. How long do they take together?

2. A and B have efficiency in ratio 3:1. Together they finish in 9 days. How long does B take alone?

3. A is 4 times as efficient as B. Together they finish in 8 days. How long does A alone take?

4. A does in 6 days what B does in 9 days. Together, how long?

5. A and B have efficiency ratio 5:3. If B alone takes 30 days, how long does A take alone?

6. A is 3 times as efficient as B and C combined. Together A, B, C finish in 5 days. How long does A take alone?

7. The ratio of time taken by A and B is 3:5. Together they finish in 15 days. How long does A take alone?

8. A does work in 10 days that B does in 15 days. In how many days can A do the work that B does in 9 days?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. 8 days
2. 36 days
3. 10 days
4. 18/5 = 3.6 days
5. 18 days
6. 20 days
7. 25 days
8. 6 days
