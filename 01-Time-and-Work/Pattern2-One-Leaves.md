# Pattern 2: One Leaves — Work Together, Then One Person Leaves

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for clues like:
- "A and B work together for T days, then **A leaves**. B finishes alone."
- "They start together, but after some days one person stops working."

---

## 🧠 The Concept (Why This Works)

Imagine two people digging a hole together. After a few days, one person goes home. The other keeps digging alone.

We just need to:
1. Calculate how much they dug **together**
2. See how much is **left**
3. Calculate how long the remaining person takes **alone**

---

## 📐 The 6-Step Method

1. **Total Work = LCM** of individual days
2. **Find each person's efficiency** (Total ÷ their days)
3. **Combined efficiency** = sum of both
4. **Work done together** = Combined × T (days worked together)
5. **Remaining work** = Total − Work done together
6. **Time for remaining person** = Remaining ÷ that person's efficiency

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**Problem:** A can do a job in 12 days, B in 16 days. They work together for 4 days, then A leaves. How many more days does B need to finish?

**Step 1 — Total Work:**
- LCM(12, 16) = 48 units

**Step 2 — Efficiencies:**
- A = 48 ÷ 12 = 4 units/day
- B = 48 ÷ 16 = 3 units/day

**Step 3 — Combined:**
- 4 + 3 = 7 units/day

**Step 4 — Work done in 4 days together:**
- 7 × 4 = 28 units

**Step 5 — Remaining:**
- 48 − 28 = 20 units

**Step 6 — B alone:**
- 20 ÷ 3 = **6.67 days (= 20/3 days)** ✅

---

### Example 2

**Problem:** A can finish in 10 days, B in 15 days. They work together for 3 days, then B leaves. How long does A take to finish?

**Step 1 — Total Work:**
- LCM(10, 15) = 30 units

**Step 2 — Efficiencies:**
- A = 30 ÷ 10 = 3 units/day
- B = 30 ÷ 15 = 2 units/day

**Step 3 — Combined:**
- 3 + 2 = 5 units/day

**Step 4 — Work done in 3 days:**
- 5 × 3 = 15 units

**Step 5 — Remaining:**
- 30 − 15 = 15 units

**Step 6 — A alone:**
- 15 ÷ 3 = **5 more days** ✅

*Total time = 3 + 5 = 8 days*

---

### Example 3 (Finding total time)

**Problem:** A finishes in 20 days, B in 30 days. They work together for 6 days, then A leaves. Find the **total** time to complete the work.

**Step 1:** LCM(20, 30) = 60 units

**Step 2:** A = 3 units/day, B = 2 units/day

**Step 3:** Combined = 5 units/day

**Step 4:** Work in 6 days = 5 × 6 = 30 units

**Step 5:** Remaining = 60 − 30 = 30 units

**Step 6:** B finishes remaining = 30 ÷ 2 = 15 more days

**Total time = 6 + 15 = 21 days** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

The key is: **Work done = (combined rate) × (days together)**

Once you have remaining work, just divide by whoever stays.

Write down:
```
Total = LCM
A eff = __, B eff = __
Together = __ days → Work = __
Remaining = Total − Work
Remaining ÷ (person who stays) = extra days
```

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. A can finish in 12 days, B in 18 days. They work together for 4 days, then A leaves. How many more days for B to finish?

2. A and B can do a job in 10 and 20 days. They work together for 5 days, then B leaves. How long does A take to finish the remaining work?

3. A finishes in 15 days, B in 10 days. They work together for 3 days, then A leaves. How many total days to complete?

4. A can do work in 6 days, B in 9 days. They work together for 2 days. Then A leaves. How many more days does B need?

5. A and B together finish in 8 days. A alone takes 12 days. They work together for 4 days, then A leaves. How many more days does B need to finish?

6. A takes 30 days, B takes 20 days. They work together for 6 days then B leaves. How long does A need to finish?

7. A and B start a task. A can do it in 18 days, B in 24 days. After 6 days of working together, B leaves. How many more days does A need?

8. A does in 8 days, B in 12 days. They work together for 2 days, then B leaves. How long for A to finish alone?

## ✔️ Answers

1. 20/3 ≈ 6.67 days
2. 2.5 days
3. 3 + 7 = 10 days total
4. 10/3 ≈ 3.33 days
5. 8 days
6. 12 days
7. 4.5 days
8. 14/3 ≈ 4.67 days
