# Pattern 1: Basic Together — Two or More People Working Together

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

The question says something like:
- "A can do a job in X days, B can do it in Y days. How long will they take **together**?"
- "A, B, and C together finish a task. How long?"

---

## 🧠 The Concept (Why This Works)

Think of work as filling a bucket.
- If A fills 1/X of the bucket per day, and B fills 1/Y of the bucket per day,
- Together, they fill **(1/X + 1/Y)** of the bucket per day.
- Total time = 1 ÷ (combined rate).

**But fractions are slow.** So we use the **LCM Trick** to avoid fractions entirely.

---

## 📐 Key Formula

### For 2 people:
> Time together = **(X × Y) / (X + Y)**

### For 3+ people (use LCM method):
1. LCM of all days = Total work units
2. Each person's efficiency = LCM ÷ their days
3. Combined efficiency = sum of all efficiencies
4. Time = Total work ÷ Combined efficiency

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**❓ Question:** A can do a job in 6 days. B can do it in 12 days. How long will they take together?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A finishes alone in 6 days, B finishes alone in 12 days
- Find: Time when both work together

**💡 What I'll use:** Together formula for 2 people: (X × Y) / (X + Y)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Plug into formula directly
- (6 × 12) / (6 + 12) = 72 / 18 = 4

**✅ Answer: 4 days**

---

### Example 2

**❓ Question:** A can do a job in 8 days. B can do it in 12 days. How long will they take together?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A takes 8 days alone, B takes 12 days alone
- Find: Time when both work together

**💡 What I'll use:** LCM Method — assign total work as LCM, find each person's efficiency, then divide

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Total Work = LCM(8, 12) = 24 units

Step 2: Find efficiencies
- A = 24 ÷ 8 = 3 units/day
- B = 24 ÷ 12 = 2 units/day

Step 3: Combined efficiency = 3 + 2 = 5 units/day

Step 4: Time = 24 ÷ 5 = 4.8 days

**✅ Answer: 4.8 days**

---

### Example 3

**❓ Question:** A finishes in 10 days, B in 15 days, C in 30 days. All three work together. How long do they take?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A takes 10 days, B takes 15 days, C takes 30 days — all working at the same time
- Find: Time for all three together to finish

**💡 What I'll use:** LCM Method for 3 people — total work = LCM of all three days

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Total Work = LCM(10, 15, 30) = 30 units

Step 2: Find each person's efficiency
- A = 30 ÷ 10 = 3 units/day
- B = 30 ÷ 15 = 2 units/day
- C = 30 ÷ 30 = 1 unit/day

Step 3: Combined efficiency = 3 + 2 + 1 = 6 units/day

Step 4: Time = 30 ÷ 6 = 5 days

**✅ Answer: 5 days**

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

For 2 people only:
> **Time = (X × Y) / (X + Y)**

Just multiply, then divide by sum. Done in 10 seconds!

For 3+ people: quickly find LCM mentally, divide, add, divide again.

---

## 🔑 Key Point

> The LCM you choose does NOT change the final answer. Whether you pick 24 or 48 or 96 as total work, the time will always be the same. Choose the smallest LCM for easy numbers.

---

## 📝 Practice Problems

Try to solve each in under 60 seconds:

1. A can finish a task in 5 days, B in 10 days. How long will they take together?

2. A can do a job in 20 days, B in 30 days. How long if they work together?

3. A finishes in 12 days, B in 18 days. Working together, how long?

4. A, B, and C can finish work in 4, 6, and 12 days respectively. How long together?

5. A does a job in 9 days, B in 18 days, C in 6 days. Together, how long?

6. A takes 15 days alone, B takes 10 days alone. Together?

7. A and B together complete work in 6 days. A alone takes 9 days. How many days does B take alone?
   *(Hint: B's efficiency = Combined − A's efficiency)*

8. Two pipes fill a tank in 20 and 30 minutes. Together, how long to fill the tank?

9. A can build a wall in 8 days, B in 6 days. Together?

10. A takes 40 days, B takes 60 days, C takes 120 days. Together?
