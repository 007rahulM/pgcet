### Q1

**❓ Question:** Pipe A fills in 12 hours, Pipe B in 18 hours. Together, how long?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Pipe A fills the tank in 12 hours, Pipe B in 18 hours — both are inlet pipes
- Find: Time to fill when both are open simultaneously

**💡 What I'll use:** Together formula: (X × Y) / (X + Y)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Plug into formula
- (12 × 18) / (12 + 18) = 216 / 30 = 7.2 hours

**✅ Answer: 7.2 hours**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** Pipe A fills in 10 min, Pipe B empties in 30 min. Net time to fill?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A is an inlet (fills tank in 10 min); B is an outlet (empties tank in 30 min); both are open
- Find: Net time to fill the tank

**💡 What I'll use:** LCM Method — inlet rate is positive, outlet rate is negative; net rate = fill − drain

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Tank capacity = LCM(10, 30) = 30 units

Step 2: Find pipe rates
- A (fills) = 30 ÷ 10 = +3 units/min
- B (empties) = 30 ÷ 30 = −1 unit/min

Step 3: Net rate = 3 − 1 = 2 units/min

Step 4: Time = 30 ÷ 2 = 15 minutes

**✅ Answer: 15 minutes**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** Two pipes fill in 15 and 20 min. A third empties in 10 min. All open. Will tank fill?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Pipe 1 fills in 15 min, Pipe 2 fills in 20 min, Pipe 3 empties in 10 min — all three are open
- Find: Whether the tank fills, and if so, how long

**💡 What I'll use:** LCM Method — add inlet rates, subtract outlet rate; check if net rate is positive

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Tank capacity = LCM(15, 20, 10) = 60 units

Step 2: Find pipe rates
- Pipe 1 (fills) = 60 ÷ 15 = +4 units/min
- Pipe 2 (fills) = 60 ÷ 20 = +3 units/min
- Pipe 3 (empties) = 60 ÷ 10 = −6 units/min

Step 3: Net rate = 4 + 3 − 6 = 1 unit/min

Step 4: Net rate is positive → Yes, the tank will fill!
- Time = 60 ÷ 1 = 60 minutes

**✅ Answer: Yes, the tank fills. Time = 60 minutes**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** A pipe fills in 8 hours. With a leak, takes 10 hours. How long does leak take to empty full tank?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Without leak, pipe fills in 8 hours; with leak open, it takes 10 hours — the leak is draining while the pipe fills
- Find: How long the leak would take to empty a full tank by itself

**💡 What I'll use:** LCM Method — leak rate = fill rate − net rate (with leak)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Tank capacity = LCM(8, 10) = 40 units

Step 2: Find rates
- Fill rate (pipe alone) = 40 ÷ 8 = 5 units/hour
- Net rate (pipe + leak) = 40 ÷ 10 = 4 units/hour

Step 3: Leak rate = Fill rate − Net rate = 5 − 4 = 1 unit/hour

Step 4: Leak empties full tank in 40 ÷ 1 = 40 hours

**✅ Answer: 40 hours**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** Three pipes A, B, C fill a tank in 6, 8, and 12 hours. All open. How long?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A fills in 6 hours, B in 8 hours, C in 12 hours — all three are inlet pipes open simultaneously
- Find: Time to fill the tank with all three open

**💡 What I'll use:** LCM Method for three pipes

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Tank capacity = LCM(6, 8, 12) = 24 units

Step 2: Find pipe rates
- A = 24 ÷ 6 = 4 units/hour
- B = 24 ÷ 8 = 3 units/hour
- C = 24 ÷ 12 = 2 units/hour

Step 3: Net rate = 4 + 3 + 2 = 9 units/hour

Step 4: Time = 24 ÷ 9 = 8/3 hours

**✅ Answer: 8/3 ≈ 2.67 hours**

### Q6

**❓ Question:** Pipe A fills in 5 hours. Pipe B empties in 8 hours. Started together when tank was empty. How long to fill?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A is an inlet (fills in 5 hours); B is an outlet (empties in 8 hours); both start together from empty
- Find: Net time to fill the tank

**💡 What I'll use:** LCM Method — inlet rate positive, outlet rate negative; net rate = fill − drain

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Tank capacity = LCM(5, 8) = 40 units

Step 2: Find pipe rates
- A (fills) = 40 ÷ 5 = +8 units/hour
- B (empties) = 40 ÷ 8 = −5 units/hour

Step 3: Net rate = 8 − 5 = 3 units/hour

Step 4: Time = 40 ÷ 3 = 40/3 hours

**✅ Answer: 40/3 ≈ 13.33 hours**

### Q7

**❓ Question:** A pipe fills 1/3 of tank in 4 hours. How long to fill full tank?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: The pipe fills 1/3 of the tank in 4 hours
- Find: Time to fill the entire tank

**💡 What I'll use:** Direct proportion — if 1/3 of the tank takes 4 hours, the whole tank takes 3× as long

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Rate of pipe = (1/3) ÷ 4 = 1/12 of the tank per hour

Step 2: Full tank = 1 ÷ (1/12) = 12 hours

**✅ Answer: 12 hours**

### Q8

**❓ Question:** Pipe A fills in 20 min, B in 30 min. After 6 min, A is closed. How long for B to fill rest?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A fills in 20 min, B fills in 30 min; both run for 6 minutes, then A is closed and B continues alone
- Find: Total time from start to full tank

**💡 What I'll use:** Calculate how much is filled in the first 6 minutes (both pipes), then find how long B needs to finish the rest alone

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: In 6 minutes with both pipes open:
- A fills = 6/20 = 3/10
- B fills = 6/30 = 2/10
- Together = 3/10 + 2/10 = 5/10 = 1/2 of the tank

Step 2: Remaining = 1 − 1/2 = 1/2 of the tank

Step 3: B alone fills remaining 1/2 at rate 1/30 per minute:
- Time = (1/2) ÷ (1/30) = (1/2) × 30 = 15 more minutes

Step 4: Total time = 6 + 15 = 21 minutes

**✅ Answer: Total time = 21 minutes (6 min together + 15 min for B alone)**
