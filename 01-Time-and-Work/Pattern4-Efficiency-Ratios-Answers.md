# Pattern 4: Efficiency Ratios — "A is Twice as Efficient as B" — Practice Problem Solutions

---

### Q1

**❓ Question:** A is twice as efficient as B. B alone takes 24 days. How long do they take together?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A is 2× as efficient as B; B alone takes 24 days
- Find: Time when both work together

**💡 What I'll use:** Efficiency-to-Time flip (if A is n times efficient, A takes 1/n the time), then Together formula: (X × Y) / (X + Y)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: A is twice as efficient → A takes half the time of B
- B = 24 days → A = 24 ÷ 2 = 12 days

Step 2: Together formula
- (12 × 24) / (12 + 24) = 288 / 36 = 8 days

**✅ Answer: 8 days**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** A and B have efficiency in ratio 3:1. Together they finish in 9 days. How long does B take alone?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A's efficiency to B's efficiency is 3:1; together they finish in 9 days
- Find: Days B would take working alone

**💡 What I'll use:** Assign variable efficiencies using the ratio, find total work from combined rate, then divide by B's rate

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Let A's efficiency = 3k units/day, B's efficiency = 1k units/day
- Combined = 4k units/day

Step 2: Total work = Combined × Days together = 4k × 9 = 36k units

Step 3: B alone = 36k ÷ 1k = 36 days

**✅ Answer: 36 days**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** A is 4 times as efficient as B. Together they finish in 8 days. How long does A alone take?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A is 4× faster than B; they finish together in 8 days
- Find: Time for A to finish alone

**💡 What I'll use:** Set B's efficiency = 1 unit/day, A = 4 units/day, find total work from combined rate, then A's solo time

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Let B's efficiency = 1 unit/day → A's efficiency = 4 units/day
- Combined = 5 units/day

Step 2: Total work = 5 × 8 = 40 units

Step 3: A alone = 40 ÷ 4 = 10 days

**✅ Answer: 10 days**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** A does in 6 days what B does in 9 days. Together, how long?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A completes a full job in 6 days, B completes it in 9 days (their individual times are given directly)
- Find: Time when both work together on the same job

**💡 What I'll use:** Together formula: (X × Y) / (X + Y)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Plug into formula
- (6 × 9) / (6 + 9) = 54 / 15 = 18/5

**✅ Answer: 18/5 = 3.6 days**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** A and B have efficiency ratio 5:3. If B alone takes 30 days, how long does A take alone?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Efficiency ratio A:B = 5:3; B alone takes 30 days
- Find: Time for A to finish alone

**💡 What I'll use:** Use the ratio to set up B's rate, find total work, then compute A's time

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Let B's efficiency = 3k units/day and A's efficiency = 5k units/day

Step 2: B alone takes 30 days → total work = 3k × 30 = 90k units

Step 3: A alone = 90k ÷ 5k = 18 days

**✅ Answer: 18 days**

### Q6

**❓ Question:** A is 3 times as efficient as B and C combined. Together A, B, C finish in 5 days. How long does A take alone?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A's efficiency = 3 × (B's efficiency + C's efficiency); all three together finish in 5 days
- Find: Time for A to finish alone

**💡 What I'll use:** Let combined B+C efficiency = x, so A's efficiency = 3x, then find total work and A's time

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Let B + C combined efficiency = x units/day
- A's efficiency = 3x units/day

Step 2: Total efficiency of A + B + C = 3x + x = 4x units/day

Step 3: Total work = 4x × 5 = 20x units

Step 4: A alone = 20x ÷ 3x = 20/3 days

**✅ Answer: 20/3 ≈ 6.67 days**

### Q7

**❓ Question:** The ratio of time taken by A and B is 3:5. Together they finish in 15 days. How long does A take alone?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Time ratio A:B = 3:5; together they finish in 15 days
- Find: Time for A to finish alone

**💡 What I'll use:** Time ratio → flip to get efficiency ratio → assign efficiencies → find total work → A's time

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Time ratio A:B = 3:5
- Efficiency ratio A:B = 5:3 (inverses of time ratio)

Step 2: Let A's efficiency = 5k units/day, B's efficiency = 3k units/day
- Combined = 8k units/day

Step 3: Total work = 8k × 15 = 120k units

Step 4: A alone = 120k ÷ 5k = 24 days

**✅ Answer: 24 days**

### Q8

**❓ Question:** A does work in 10 days that B does in 15 days. In how many days can A do the work that B does in 9 days?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A's rate is 1/10 per day, B's rate is 1/12 per day; B works for 9 days — find how long A takes to do that same amount
- Find: Days A needs to complete the fraction of work B does in 9 days

**💡 What I'll use:** Find the fraction B completes in 9 days, then divide that fraction by A's daily rate

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: How much does B do in 9 days?
- B's rate = 1/15 per day → B in 9 days = 9/15 = 3/5 of the work

Step 2: How long does A take to do 3/5 of the work?
- A's rate = 1/10 per day
- Time = (3/5) ÷ (1/10) = (3/5) × 10 = 6 days

**✅ Answer: 6 days**
