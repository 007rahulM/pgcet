# Pattern 3: Joining — One Starts, Other Joins Later — Practice Problem Solutions

---

### Q1

**❓ Question:** A can do a task in 8 days, B in 12 days. A works alone for 2 days, B joins. Find total time.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A takes 8 days alone, B takes 12 days alone; A works solo for 2 days, then B joins
- Find: Total time from start to finish

**💡 What I'll use:** 5-Step LCM Method — find work A does solo, then calculate how long A+B take for the rest

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Total Work = LCM(8, 12) = 24 units

Step 2: Find efficiencies
- A = 24 ÷ 8 = 3 units/day
- B = 24 ÷ 12 = 2 units/day

Step 3: Work done by A alone in 2 days = 3 × 2 = 6 units

Step 4: Remaining work = 24 − 6 = 18 units

Step 5: A + B together = 3 + 2 = 5 units/day → 18 ÷ 5 = 3.6 days

**✅ Answer: Total time = 2 + 3.6 = 5.6 days**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** A finishes in 15 days, B in 20 days. A works alone for 4 days, then B joins. How many more days to finish together?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A takes 15 days alone, B takes 20 days alone; A works solo for 4 days, then B joins
- Find: Additional days A and B need together after B joins (not total time)

**💡 What I'll use:** 5-Step LCM Method — find remaining work after A's solo phase, then divide by combined efficiency

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Total Work = LCM(15, 20) = 60 units

Step 2: Find efficiencies
- A = 60 ÷ 15 = 4 units/day
- B = 60 ÷ 20 = 3 units/day

Step 3: Work done by A alone in 4 days = 4 × 4 = 16 units

Step 4: Remaining work = 60 − 16 = 44 units

Step 5: A + B together = 4 + 3 = 7 units/day → 44 ÷ 7 = 44/7 days

**✅ Answer: 44/7 ≈ 6.29 more days together (Total time = 4 + 44/7 ≈ 10.29 days)**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** A takes 20 days alone. A works for 5 days, then B joins. They finish in 4 more days. How long would B take alone?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A takes 20 days alone; A works solo for 5 days, then B joins and together they finish in 4 more days
- Find: How many days B would take working alone

**💡 What I'll use:** Work backwards — calculate remaining work after A's solo phase, find combined efficiency from remaining work and time, then subtract A's efficiency to get B's

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Let total work = 1 (full job)

Step 2: A's rate = 1/20 per day
Work by A alone in 5 days = 5 × (1/20) = 5/20 = 1/4

Step 3: Remaining work = 1 − 1/4 = 3/4

Step 4: A + B together finish 3/4 in 4 days
- Combined rate = (3/4) ÷ 4 = 3/16 per day

Step 5: B's rate = Combined − A's rate = 3/16 − 1/20
- Common denominator 80: 15/80 − 4/80 = 11/80 per day

Step 6: B alone = 1 ÷ (11/80) = 80/11 days

**✅ Answer: 80/11 ≈ 7.27 days**
