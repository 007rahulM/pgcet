---

### Q1

**❓ Question:** A can finish a task in 5 days, B in 10 days. How long will they take together?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A finishes alone in 5 days, B finishes alone in 10 days
- Find: Time when both work together

**💡 What I'll use:** Together formula for 2 people: (X × Y) / (X + Y)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Plug into formula directly
- (5 × 10) / (5 + 10) = 50 / 15 = 10/3

**✅ Answer: 10/3 ≈ 3.33 days**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** A, B, and C can finish work in 4, 6, and 12 days respectively. How long together?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A takes 4 days, B takes 6 days, C takes 12 days — all working at the same time
- Find: Time for all three together to finish

**💡 What I'll use:** LCM Method for 3 people — total work = LCM of all three days

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Total Work = LCM(4, 6, 12) = 12 units

Step 2: Find each person's efficiency
- A = 12 ÷ 4 = 3 units/day
- B = 12 ÷ 6 = 2 units/day
- C = 12 ÷ 12 = 1 unit/day

Step 3: Combined efficiency = 3 + 2 + 1 = 6 units/day

Step 4: Time = 12 ÷ 6 = 2 days

**✅ Answer: 2 days**

### Q7

**❓ Question:** A and B together complete work in 6 days. A alone takes 9 days. How many days does B take alone?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Together they finish in 6 days; A alone takes 9 days
- Find: How many days B would take working alone

**💡 What I'll use:** LCM Method — find combined and A's efficiency, then B's efficiency = Combined − A's. B's days = Total Work ÷ B's efficiency

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Total Work = LCM(6, 9) = 18 units

Step 2: Combined efficiency = 18 ÷ 6 = 3 units/day

Step 3: A's efficiency = 18 ÷ 9 = 2 units/day

Step 4: B's efficiency = 3 − 2 = 1 unit/day

Step 5: B alone = 18 ÷ 1 = 18 days

**✅ Answer: 18 days**
