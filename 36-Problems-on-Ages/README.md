# Problems on Ages — Overview

Age problems in PGCET are solved with linear equations using present age as variables.

## Patterns in This Folder

| File | Pattern | Recognition Clue |
|------|---------|------------------|
| [questions.md](./questions.md) | Practice set | "years ago", "years hence", "sum of ages" |
| [solutions.md](./solutions.md) | Stepwise linear-equation solutions | Present-age variable method |

## Standard Approach

1. Assume present ages as variables (e.g., \(x,y\)).
2. Convert "k years ago" to \(x-k\), "k years hence" to \(x+k\).
3. Build equations from given relations.
4. Solve linear equations and verify non-negative ages.

## Solved Examples

1. Father is 3 times son now. After 10 years, father will be 2 times son. Find present ages.  
   Let son \(=x\), father \(=3x\).  
   \(3x+10=2(x+10)\Rightarrow x=10\).  
   Father \(=30\).  
   **Answer: Son 10, Father 30**

2. (2025-style) Sum of ages of A and B is 50 now. Five years ago, sum was 40? Check.  
   If sum now is 50, five years ago sum should be \(50-10=40\).  
   **Answer: Yes, 40**

3. Mother is 24 years older than daughter. In 6 years, mother’s age will be twice daughter’s.  
   Let daughter \(=d\), mother \(=d+24\).  
   \(d+30=2(d+6)\Rightarrow d=18\), mother \(=42\).  
   **Answer: Daughter 18, Mother 42**
