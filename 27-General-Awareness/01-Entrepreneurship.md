# Entrepreneurship — General Awareness

> **4–6 questions** in PGCET 2024 and 2025 on this topic alone. This is NEW but important!

---

## PART 1 — What Is Entrepreneurship?

**Entrepreneurship** = The ability to turn ideas into action through creativity, innovation, and risk-taking.

**Entrepreneur** = A person who starts and runs a business, taking on financial risks to do so.

---

## PART 2 — Types of Ventures (from 2025 paper)

| Type | Key Characteristic |
|------|-------------------|
| **Scalable Startup** | High growth potential, often technology-driven (e.g., Uber, Airbnb) |
| **Small Business** | Local businesses with limited growth potential (e.g., local bakery) |
| **Social Enterprise** | Aimed at addressing social issues and community needs |
| **Large Company (Intrapreneurship)** | Established firms expanding through innovation |

---

## PART 3 — Characteristics of Entrepreneurial Success (from 2025 paper)

✅ **Essential characteristics:**
- **Innovation and creativity** — finding new solutions
- **Adaptability to market changes** — pivoting when needed
- **Long-term stability** — sustainable growth vision
- **Risk-taking** (calculated, NOT reckless)
- **Persistence** and resilience

❌ **NOT characteristics of successful entrepreneurs:**
- Risk aversion (avoiding all risk)
- Hierarchical decision-making (rigid structure)

---

## PART 4 — Entrepreneurial Ecosystem

The **entrepreneurial ecosystem** is the network of organizations and factors that support entrepreneurship.

**What fosters it (from 2025 paper):**
- ✅ Access to **venture capital**
- ✅ Supportive **mentorship programs**
- ✅ **Technological advancements**
- Government regulations (sometimes supportive, sometimes barrier)
- Competitive market conditions (drives innovation)

---

## PART 5 — Bootstrap vs Venture Capital (from 2024 paper)

**Bootstrap** = Starting a business **without external investment**, relying on personal finances or operating revenues.

**Venture Capital** = External investment from investors in exchange for equity stake.

**Angel Investor** = Individual who provides early-stage funding.

---

## PART 6 — Business Incubators (from 2024 paper)

Business incubators support early-stage companies by providing:
- ✅ Networking opportunities
- ✅ Legal and regulatory assistance
- ✅ Research and Development funding
- ❌ Long-term financial investment (this is venture capital's role, NOT incubators)

## PART 7 — Key Business Concepts

### Porter's Five Forces (from 2024 paper)

A framework for analyzing competitive forces in an industry:

1. **Threat of new entrants** (new competitors entering)
2. **Power of suppliers** (supplier bargaining power)
3. **Competition among existing competitors** (rivalry)
4. **Bargaining power of buyers** (customer power)
5. **Threat of substitute products** (alternatives)

> **All 5 are Porter's Five Forces!** (Answer: a, b, c, d — all of the above)

### SWOT Analysis

| | Internal | External |
|--|---------|---------|
| Positive | **S**trengths | **O**pportunities |
| Negative | **W**eaknesses | **T**hreats |

### Sustainable Competitive Advantage (from 2025 paper)

Critical factors:
- ✅ Exploiting economies of scale
- ✅ Maintaining high customer satisfaction and loyalty
- ✅ Developing unique, valuable resources and capabilities
- ❌ Frequent changes in strategy (this UNDERMINES competitive advantage)

---

## PART 8 — Gantt Chart (from 2024 paper)

**Gantt chart** = A bar chart that displays a **project schedule** showing tasks, timelines, and dependencies.

Used in **project management** to visualize:
- Task durations
- Start and end dates
- Dependencies between tasks

## PART 9 — Important Business Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Strike Price** | The price at which the underlying asset can be bought or sold in options trading |
| **P/E Ratio** | Price-to-Earnings ratio: Market price per share ÷ Earnings per share |
| **Bond** | A debt instrument — borrowing money for a fixed period at fixed interest |
| **Bootstrap** | Start business without external funding |
| **Cabotage** | Transportation of goods/passengers WITHIN a country (domestic route) |
| **Intermodal Transportation** | Using multiple types of transportation for a single shipment |
| **Financial Market** | Markets where financial instruments (stocks, bonds) are traded |

## PART 10 — Industry Sector Types (from 2025 paper)

| Sector | Description | Example |

## PART 11 — Financial Markets (from 2024 paper)

**Types of financial markets:**

- ✅ **Bond market** — trading debt instruments
- ✅ **Real estate market** — property trading
- ❌ **Consumer market** — where goods/services are bought/sold (NOT a financial market)

## 📝 Practice Problems

**Q1.** "Bootstrap" in entrepreneurship means:
- (A) Starting with venture capital  (B) Starting with personal funds, no external investment
- (C) Hiring experienced managers  (D) Acquiring loans from banks

**Q2.** Which of the following is NOT a Porter's Five Force?
- (A) Threat of new entrants  (B) Power of suppliers
- (C) SWOT Analysis  (D) Threat of substitutes

**Q3.** What does a Gantt chart primarily display?
- (A) Financial performance  (B) Project schedule
- (C) Market share  (D) Employee performance

**Q4.** A business incubator provides:
- (A) Long-term financial investment  (B) Networking and mentorship
- (C) Government subsidies  (D) Bank loans

**Q5.** "Cabotage" in transportation means:
- (A) International shipping  (B) Air transport only
- (C) Domestic transport within a country  (D) Cross-border road transport

---

## ✔️ Answers

| Q | A | Reason |
|---|---|--------|
| Q1 | (B) | Bootstrap = personal funds, no outside investment |
| Q2 | (C) | SWOT is a different framework; Porter's = 5 competitive forces |
| Q3 | (B) | Gantt = project schedule/timeline |
| Q4 | (B) | Incubators give networking, mentorship, legal help — not direct investment |
| Q5 | (C) | Cabotage = domestic transport within a country |
