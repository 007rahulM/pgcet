## PART 1 — Health Models

### Health Belief Model (HBM) (from 2025 paper)

The **Health Belief Model** explains why people take (or don't take) health-protective actions.

**Key components:**
- **Perceived Susceptibility** — How likely am I to get this disease?
- **Perceived Severity** — How serious is the disease?
- **Perceived Benefits** — Benefits of taking action
- **Perceived Barriers** — Obstacles to taking action ← HBM DOES consider barriers!
- **Cues to Action** — Triggers that prompt behavior (symptoms, media)

> **From 2025 paper:** Which statement about HBM is wrong?
> - "(b) HBM does not consider perceived barriers to taking health-related actions" → This is **FALSE** (HBM DOES consider barriers)
> - So statement (b) is the incorrect one → Only statement (a) is correct

### LEARN Model (from 2025 paper)

A framework for cross-cultural healthcare communication:
- **L** — **Listen** with empathy
- **E** — **Explain** your perspective
- **A** — **Acknowledge** differences and similarities
- **R** — **Recommend** options ← NOT "collaboratively deciding"
- **N** — **Negotiate** the treatment plan ← This is "collaboratively deciding on treatment"

> **From 2025 paper:** "Which aspect of LEARN is focused on collaboratively deciding on treatment?" → **Negotiate (N)**

## PART 2 — Cultural Dimensions

### Cultural Humility (from 2025 paper)

Cultural humility in healthcare involves:
- ✅ **Understanding personal biases** (self-reflection)
- ✅ Being open to learning from patients
- ❌ NOT just mastery of medical procedures
- ❌ NOT about compliance with hospital regulations
- ❌ NOT about efficient patient scheduling

### Cultural Assimilation (from 2024 paper)

**Cultural assimilation** = process by which a minority group adopts customs/attitudes of the prevailing culture.

**True statements:**
- ✅ "Cultural assimilation is the process by which a minority group adopts the customs and attitudes of the prevailing culture"
- ✅ "Cultural assimilation can result in the blending of cultures, creating a more diverse society"
- ❌ "Cultural assimilation ALWAYS leads to complete loss of the minority group's original culture" → NOT always true

## PART 4 — Governance Concepts (from 2024 paper)

### Key Principles of Good Governance

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **Transparency** | Open, honest, visible decision-making |
| **Accountability** | Those in authority are held responsible |
| **Rule of Law** | Laws apply equally to all |
| **Participation** | All stakeholders have a voice |
| **Effectiveness** | Achieving goals efficiently |
| **Equity** | Fair treatment for all |

> Governance applies to BOTH government AND corporate institutions!

### Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) vs. SDGs (from 2024 paper)

| | MDGs (ended 2015) | SDGs (current, end 2030) |
|--|------------------|------------------------|
| Goals | 8 goals | 17 goals |
| Focus | Developing countries | Universal |
| Child/maternal health | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes (SDG 3) |

> **"Which global health initiative aims to reduce child mortality and improve maternal health?"**
> Answer: **Millennium Development Goals (MDGs)** (Goal 4: child mortality, Goal 5: maternal health)

## 📝 Practice Questions

**Q1.** In the LEARN model, which aspect involves collaboratively deciding on treatment?
- (A) Listen  (B) Explain  (C) Recommend  (D) Negotiate

**Q2.** Deficiency of which vitamin causes Scurvy?
- (A) Vitamin A  (B) Vitamin B  (C) Vitamin C  (D) Vitamin D

**Q3.** Cultural humility in healthcare primarily involves:
- (A) Mastery of medical procedures  (B) Understanding personal biases
- (C) Compliance with hospital regulations  (D) Efficient patient scheduling

**Q4.** Which of the following is a TRUE statement about the Health Belief Model?
- (A) HBM does not consider perceived barriers
- (B) HBM considers perceived benefits but not barriers
- (C) HBM considers both perceived benefits and perceived barriers
- (D) HBM is only for infectious diseases

**Q5.** Theory of General Relativity was given by:
- (A) Isaac Newton  (B) Albert Einstein  (C) Stephen Hawking  (D) Niels Bohr
