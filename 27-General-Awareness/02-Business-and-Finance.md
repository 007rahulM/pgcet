## PART 1 — Stock Market Basics

### Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Share/Stock** | A unit of ownership in a company |
| **P/E Ratio** | Price-to-Earnings ratio: how much investors pay per ₹1 of earnings |
| **Bull Market** | Market prices rising (optimistic) |
| **Bear Market** | Market prices falling (pessimistic) |
| **Dividend** | Share of company profit paid to shareholders |
| **IPO** | Initial Public Offering — when company first lists on stock exchange |
| **Mutual Fund** | Pool of money from many investors, managed professionally |

### P/E Ratio (from 2024 paper)

```
P/E Ratio = Market Price per Share / Earnings per Share
```

**Interpretation:**
- **High P/E** = investors expecting higher future growth (paying premium)
- **Low P/E** = stock may be undervalued OR company has poor growth prospects
- P/E ratio is used to evaluate **company valuation**, NOT liquidity

> ⚠️ **Common wrong answer:** "Low P/E means stock is overvalued" → **WRONG!** Low P/E = possibly undervalued.

## PART 2 — Bonds and Fixed Income

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Bond** | Debt instrument — issuer borrows money, pays back with interest |
| **Face Value** | Original value of bond at issue |
| **Coupon Rate** | Interest rate paid on bond |
| **Maturity** | Date when bond principal is repaid |
| **Credit Rating** | Assessment of issuer's ability to repay |

### What Affects Bond Value? (from 2024 paper)

Bond value is affected by:
- ✅ **Interest rates** — when interest rates rise, bond prices fall (inverse relationship)
- ✅ **Credit rating of issuer** — lower credit rating = higher risk = lower bond price
- ✅ **Inflation rate** — high inflation erodes bond's real return
- ❌ **Company's stock price** — does NOT directly affect bond value

## PART 3 — Options Trading (from 2025 paper)

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Option** | Contract giving the right (but not obligation) to buy/sell asset |
| **Strike Price** | The pre-agreed price at which the option can be exercised |
| **Call Option** | Right to BUY at strike price |
| **Put Option** | Right to SELL at strike price |
| **Premium** | Price paid for the option contract itself |

> **Strike Price ≠ current market price, ≠ option premium**

## PART 4 — Central Bank Functions (from 2024 paper)

**Primary functions of a Central Bank** (e.g., RBI in India):
- ✅ **Regulating money supply** (monetary policy)
- ✅ **Banker to the government**
- ✅ **Banker to banks** (lender of last resort)
- ✅ **Foreign exchange management**
- ✅ **Issuing currency**
- **All of the above** are typically the answer!

## PART 7 — Economic Concepts

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Invisible Hand** | Adam Smith's concept — self-regulating nature of free market forces (NOT government intervention) |
| **GDP** | Gross Domestic Product — total value of goods/services produced in a country |
| **Inflation** | Rise in general price level over time |
| **Recession** | Negative GDP growth for 2+ consecutive quarters |
| **Fiscal Policy** | Government policy on spending and taxation |
| **Monetary Policy** | Central bank's policy on money supply and interest rates |

## PART 8 — Governance (from 2024 paper)

**Key principles of good governance:**
- ✅ **Transparency** — openness about decisions
- ✅ **Accountability** — those in authority responsible for actions
- ✅ **Rule of Law**
- ✅ **Participation** — stakeholders have a voice
- ❌ Governance is NOT only for government institutions (applies to corporate governance too!)

**Tuckman's Model of Group Development (from 2024 paper):**

```
1. Forming   → Group comes together
2. Storming  → Conflicts and disagreements
3. Norming   → Rules and cooperation established
4. Performing → High productivity
(5. Adjourning → Group disbands)
```

> **NOT a stage: "Stabilizing"** — this is NOT in Tuckman's model!

## 📝 Practice Problems

**Q1.** What does "Invisible hand" in economics refer to?
- (A) Government intervention  (B) Self-regulating nature of market forces
- (C) Influence of monopolies  (D) Centralized economic planning

**Q2.** Which statement about P/E ratio is FALSE?
- (A) P/E = Market price ÷ EPS
- (B) High P/E suggests investors expect higher future growth
- (C) Low P/E means stock is overvalued
- (D) P/E is used to evaluate company valuation

**Q3.** When interest rates rise, bond prices:
- (A) Rise  (B) Fall  (C) Stay same  (D) Are unpredictable

**Q4.** The "strike price" in options trading is:
- (A) Current market price  (B) Price of the option contract
- (C) Pre-agreed price at which option can be exercised  (D) Commission fee

**Q5.** Which is NOT a stage in Tuckman's model?
- (A) Forming  (B) Storming  (C) Performing  (D) Stabilizing

---

## ✔️ Answers

| Q | A | Reason |
|---|---|--------|
| Q1 | (B) | Invisible hand = market self-regulation (Adam Smith, Wealth of Nations) |
| Q2 | (C) | Low P/E may indicate UNDERVALUED, not overvalued |
| Q3 | (B) | Bond price moves inverse to interest rates |
| Q4 | (C) | Strike price = exercise price, pre-agreed |
| Q5 | (D) | Tuckman: Forming→Storming→Norming→Performing (no "Stabilizing") |
