# Reverse Percentage & Exam Traps

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for:
- "What additional discount is needed?"
- "Price dropped from X to Y — find % decrease"
- "After increase/decrease, final value is given — find original"
- Questions where the **base changes** and direct subtraction gives the wrong answer

---

## 📐 Core Formulas

### Reverse Percentage
> If final value = Original × `(100 ± p)/100`, then
> **Original = Final × 100 / (100 ± p)**

### Percentage Decrease
> **% decrease = (Decrease / Original) × 100**

### Additional Discount After First Discount
If marked price is MP and first discount is `d₁%`:
1. Find reduced price after first discount
2. Compare that reduced price with target SP
3. Additional discount = `(Difference / Reduced price) × 100`

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**❓ Question:** A book is listed at ₹300 with a discount of 20%. What additional discount must be offered to bring the selling price to ₹216?

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Price after first discount
- 300 × 80 / 100 = ₹240

Step 2: Extra reduction needed
- 240 − 216 = ₹24

Step 3: Additional discount on reduced price
- 24/240 × 100 = **10%**

**✅ Answer: 10%**

---

### Example 2

**❓ Question:** The average attendance at a cricket club dropped from 5640 last year to 3820 this year. What was the percentage decrease?

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Decrease = 5640 − 3820 = 1820

Step 2: Use original year as base
- % decrease = 1820/5640 × 100
- = 91/282 × 100 ≈ **32.27%**

**✅ Answer: 32.27%**

---

### Example 3

**❓ Question:** After a 20% increase, a value becomes 84. Find the original value.

**✏️ My Solution:**
- Original = 84 × 100 / 120 = **70**

**✅ Answer: 70**

---

### Example 4

**❓ Question:** A number is reduced by 25% and becomes 72. Find the original number.

**✏️ My Solution:**
- Original = 72 × 100 / 75 = **96**

**✅ Answer: 96**

---

### Example 5

**❓ Question:** A salary first increases by 20% and then decreases by 20%. Is the final value unchanged?

**✏️ My Solution:**
- Net change = 20 + (−20) + (20×−20)/100
- = 0 − 4 = **−4%**

**✅ Answer: No, final value decreases by 4%**

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

- "Find original" → divide by multiplier
- "Decrease from X to Y" → always divide by **X**, not Y
- "Additional discount" → first work after the first discount, then use that as the base

**Exam trap:** same difference, wrong base = wrong answer.

4. After a 10% decrease, the value becomes 540. Find the original.
