## 📐 Core Formulas

| Find | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Part | (Percentage × Whole) / 100 |
| Percentage | (Part / Whole) × 100 |
| New value after X% increase | Original × (100+X)/100 |
| New value after X% decrease | Original × (100−X)/100 |
| Successive change (a% then b%) | Net = a + b + ab/100 |

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Basic)

**Problem:** What is 30% of 450?

- = 30/100 × 450 = **135** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Find percentage)

**Problem:** 75 is what % of 300?

- = (75/300) × 100 = **25%** ✅

---

### Example 3 (% increase)

**Problem:** A salary of ₹8000 is increased by 15%. New salary?

- = 8000 × 115/100 = **₹9200** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Successive change)

**Problem:** Price increased by 20% then decreased by 10%. Net change?

- Net = 20 + (−10) + (20×(−10))/100
- = 20 − 10 − 2 = **+8% (increase)** ✅

---

### Example 5 (Population growth)

**Problem:** Population = 50,000. Increases 10% in year 1, 20% in year 2. Final population?

- After Y1: 50000 × 1.1 = 55000
- After Y2: 55000 × 1.2 = **66,000** ✅

---

### Example 6 (Finding original value)

**Problem:** After a 20% increase, value is 84. Original?

- 84 = Original × 120/100
- Original = 84 × 100/120 = **70** ✅

---

### Example 7 (% more/less comparison)

**Problem:** A earns ₹500, B earns ₹400. By what % is A's salary more than B's?

- % more = (500−400)/400 × 100 = 100/400 × 100 = **25%** ✅

---

### Example 8 (% less)

**Problem:** B's salary is what % less than A's?

- % less = (500−400)/500 × 100 = 100/500 × 100 = **20%** ✅

> **Important:** "X more than Y" uses Y as base. "Y less than X" uses X as base!

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcuts

1. **For successive changes:** Use a + b + ab/100 (use − for decrease)
2. **To find original:** Divide by the multiplier
3. **% shortcuts using fractions:**
   - 50% of 240 = 120 (divide by 2)
   - 25% of 360 = 90 (divide by 4)
   - 33.33% of 300 = 100 (divide by 3)

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. What is 40% of 250?

2. 90 is what % of 360?

3. A population of 20,000 increases by 5%. New population?

4. Price rises 10% then falls 10%. Net change?

5. A score of 75 out of 150. What %?

6. If A's weight is 20% more than B's, by what % is B's weight less than A's?

7. Price increases by 25%, then by 20%. Net increase?

8. After 2 successive 10% reductions, what is the final value if original is 1000?

9. A is 30% more than B. What % is B less than A?

10. If 60% of a number is 48, find the number.

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. 100
2. 25%
3. 21,000
4. 10 + (−10) + (10×(−10))/100 = 0 − 1 = **−1% (decrease)**
5. 50%
6. A = 1.2B → B = A/1.2. B less than A = (0.2/1.2)×100 = **16.67%**
7. 25 + 20 + 5 = **50%**
8. 1000 × 0.9 × 0.9 = **810**
9. A = 1.3B → B = A/1.3. Diff = 0.3/1.3 × 100 = **23.08%**
10. 48/0.6 = **80**
