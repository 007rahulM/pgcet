## 📐 FORMULA BLOCK 1 — Basic Percentage Calculations

### Question looks like:
- "What is **30% of 450**?"
- "75 is what **% of 300**?"
- "If 20% of a number is 40, find the **number**."
- "What % is 45 out of 180?"

### ALL Formulas:

| Find | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Part from percentage and whole | `(Percentage × Whole) ÷ 100` |
| Percentage from part and whole | `(Part ÷ Whole) × 100` |
| Whole from part and percentage | `(Part × 100) ÷ Percentage` |

### Quick Fraction-Percentage Equivalents (MEMORIZE):

| Fraction | % | Fraction | % |
|----------|---|----------|---|
| 1/2 | 50% | 1/6 | 16.67% |
| 1/3 | 33.33% | 1/7 | 14.29% |
| 2/3 | 66.67% | 1/8 | 12.5% |
| 1/4 | 25% | 1/9 | 11.11% |
| 3/4 | 75% | 1/10 | 10% |
| 1/5 | 20% | 1/12 | 8.33% |
| 2/5 | 40% | 1/20 | 5% |
| 3/5 | 60% | 1/25 | 4% |
| 4/5 | 80% | 1/50 | 2% |

## 📐 FORMULA BLOCK 2 — Increase and Decrease by a Percentage

### Question looks like:
- "Salary ₹8000 **increased by 15%**. New salary?"
- "Price ₹500 **reduced by 20%**. New price?"
- "Population 50,000 **grows 10%** per year. After 2 years?"

### ALL Formulas:

| Find | Formula |
|------|---------|
| New value after X% **increase** | `Original × (100 + X) ÷ 100` |
| New value after X% **decrease** | `Original × (100 − X) ÷ 100` |
| New value after X% increase (multiplier form) | `Original × (1 + X/100)` |
| New value after X% decrease (multiplier form) | `Original × (1 − X/100)` |
| Original before X% increase | `New value × 100 ÷ (100 + X)` |
| Original before X% decrease | `New value × 100 ÷ (100 − X)` |

→ **See examples: [02-Percentage-Change.md](./02-Percentage-Change.md)**

## 📐 FORMULA BLOCK 3 — Successive Percentage Changes

### Question looks like:
- "Price first **increased 20%**, then **decreased 10%**. Net change?"
- "Two consecutive **10% increases**. Final value?"
- "Price went up 30%, then down 20%. Net effect?"
- "Population increases 10% yr1, 20% yr2. Final population?"

### ALL Formulas:

| Find | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Net % for two successive changes a% and b% | `a + b + (a × b) / 100` (use − for decrease) |
| Final value after a% then b% | `Original × (100±a)/100 × (100±b)/100` |
| Net effect of two equal x% increases | `2x + x²/100` |
| Net effect of two equal x% decreases | `−2x + x²/100` (note: this is a net decrease) |
| Net effect of x% increase then x% decrease | `−x²/100` (always a net **loss**) |

→ **See examples: [02-Percentage-Change.md](./02-Percentage-Change.md)**

## 📐 FORMULA BLOCK 4 — Reverse (Find Original)

### Question looks like:
- "After a **20% increase**, value = 84. Original?"
- "After a **10% reduction**, price = ₹450. What was original price?"
- "After two increases of 10% and 20%, final = 1320. Original?"

### ALL Formulas:

| Find | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Original after one increase of x% | `Final ÷ (100+x) × 100` |
| Original after one decrease of x% | `Final ÷ (100−x) × 100` |
| Original after increase x% then increase y% | `Final × 100/(100+x) × 100/(100+y)` |
| Original after increase x% then decrease y% | `Final × 100/(100+x) × 100/(100−y)` |

→ **See examples: [02-Percentage-Change.md](./02-Percentage-Change.md)**

## 📐 FORMULA BLOCK 5 — Comparison (More than / Less than)

### Question looks like:
- "A is **25% more** than B. B is what % less than A?"
- "A earns ₹500, B earns ₹400. A is what % more than B?"
- "B earns ₹400, A earns ₹500. B is what % less than A?"
- "A's weight is 20% more than B's. By how much % is B's weight less?"

### ALL Formulas:

| Find | Formula | Base is |
|------|---------|---------|
| A is X% more than B → B is ?% less than A | `X/(100+X) × 100` | A is the base |
| A is X% less than B → B is ?% more than A | `X/(100−X) × 100` | A is the base |
| % A is more than B | `(A−B)/B × 100` | B is the base |
| % B is less than A | `(A−B)/A × 100` | A is the base |

### ⚠️ Key Rule:
> "X% more than Y" → base is **Y**
> "X% less than Y" → base is **Y**

→ **See examples: [03-Percentage-Applications.md](./03-Percentage-Applications.md)**

## 📐 FORMULA BLOCK 6 — Applications (Election, Income, etc.)

### Question looks like:
- "Candidate A gets **55% of 80,000 votes**. Winning margin?"
- "30% of students are girls. 21 girls. Total students?"
- "Income ₹25,000. Spends 75%. Savings?"
- "A school has 800 students. 60% are boys. 40% of boys play football. How many boys play?"

### Approach:

| Find | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Actual votes from % and total | `(Percentage ÷ 100) × Total` |
| Total from part and % | `Part × 100 ÷ Percentage` |
| Savings | `Income − (Expenditure% × Income / 100)` |
| Multi-step % | Apply each % on the result of the previous step |
| Winning margin | `(Winner% − Loser%) × Total ÷ 100` |

→ **See examples: [03-Percentage-Applications.md](./03-Percentage-Applications.md)**

## 🔑 Master Summary Table

| Formula | Used When |
|---------|-----------|
| `(Part/Whole) × 100` | Finding what percentage |
| `(Percentage × Whole)/100` | Finding part from percentage |
| `(Part × 100)/Percentage` | Finding whole from part and percentage |

| `Original × (100−x)/100` | New value after x% decrease |
| `New × 100/(100+x)` | Finding original before x% increase |
| `New × 100/(100−x)` | Finding original before x% decrease |
| `a + b + ab/100` | Net% for two successive changes |
| `x/(100+x) × 100` | If A is x% more than B, how much % less is B than A |
