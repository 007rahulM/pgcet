# Percentages — Basic Calculations

## 🔍 When This Comes Up

- "What % of X is Y?"
- "X is what % of Y?"
- Basic percentage finding problems

---

## 📐 Core Formulas

| Find | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Part | (Percentage × Whole) / 100 |
| Percentage | (Part / Whole) × 100 |
| Whole (original) | (Part × 100) / Percentage |

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Find Part)

**Problem:** What is 30% of 450?

- = 30/100 × 450 = **135** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Find Percentage)

**Problem:** 75 is what % of 300?

- = (75/300) × 100 = **25%** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Find the Whole)

**Problem:** 40% of a number is 80. Find the number.

- Number = (80 × 100) / 40 = **200** ✅

---

### Example 4 (% more/less comparison)

**Problem:** A earns ₹500, B earns ₹400. By what % is A's salary more than B's?

- % more = (500−400)/400 × 100 = 100/400 × 100 = **25%** ✅

---

### Example 5 (% less)

**Problem:** B's salary is what % less than A's?

- % less = (500−400)/500 × 100 = 100/500 × 100 = **20%** ✅

> **Important:** "X more than Y" uses Y as base. "Y less than X" uses X as base!

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcuts

- **50%** of any number: divide by 2
- **25%** of any number: divide by 4
- **33.33%** of any number: divide by 3
- **10%** of any number: move decimal point one left
- **1%** of any number: move decimal point two left

---

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. What is 40% of 250?

2. 90 is what % of 360?

3. A score of 75 out of 150. What %?

4. If 60% of a number is 48, find the number.

5. A earns ₹600, B earns ₹500. A's salary is what % more than B's?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. 40/100 × 250 = **100**
2. (90/360) × 100 = **25%**
3. (75/150) × 100 = **50%**
4. 48/0.6 = **80**
5. (600−500)/500 × 100 = **20%**
