# Percentages — Applications & Combined Problems

## 🔍 When This Comes Up

- Commonly combined with Profit & Loss, Interest, Data Interpretation
- "A is X% more/less than B" type comparisons
- Election results with vote percentage problems

---

## 📐 Key Relationships

| Problem Type | Approach |
|-------------|---------|
| % more than | Use the base as denominator |
| % less than | Use the larger number as denominator |
| Combined with Profit/Loss | Apply % to CP or SP |
| Election problems | Use total votes as base |

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (% Comparison — Exam Favourite)

**Problem:** If A's weight is 20% more than B's, by what % is B's weight less than A's?

- Let B = 100. Then A = 120.
- B is less than A by: (120−100)/120 × 100 = 20/120 × 100 = **16.67%** ✅

**Quick formula:** If A is x% more than B → B is (x/(100+x)) × 100 % less than A

---

### Example 2 (Election)

**Problem:** A gets 55% votes, B gets 45%. Total votes = 80,000. By how many votes does A win?

- A's votes = 55% × 80000 = 44000
- B's votes = 45% × 80000 = 36000
- Winning margin = 44000 − 36000 = **8000 votes** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Percentage of Percentage)

**Problem:** A school has 800 students. 60% are boys. 40% of boys play football. How many boys play football?

- Boys = 60% × 800 = 480
- Football players = 40% × 480 = **192** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Income and Expenditure)

**Problem:** Income = ₹25,000. Savings = 20% of income. What is expenditure?

- Savings = 20% × 25000 = 5000
- Expenditure = 25000 − 5000 = **₹20,000** ✅

---

### Example 5 (% Change to reach target)

**Problem:** A scored 45 in exam. Pass mark is 60. By what % did he fall short?

- Short = 60 − 45 = 15
- % short = (15/60) × 100 = **25%** ✅

---

### Example 6 (Two-variable problem)

**Problem:** A is 30% more than B. What % is B less than A?

- B less than A = (30/(100+30)) × 100 = 30/130 × 100 ≈ **23.08%** ✅

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. A's income is 25% more than B's. B's income is what % less than A's?

2. In an election, candidate A gets 40% of votes and loses by 1200 votes. Find total votes.

3. 30% of students in a class are girls. If there are 21 girls, how many students in total?

4. A man spends 75% of his salary and saves ₹5000. Find his salary.

5. A number is first multiplied by 1.2 and then divided by 0.8. Net % change?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. B less than A = (25/125) × 100 = **20%**
2. A gets 40%, B gets 60%. Difference = 20%. 20% of total = 1200. Total = **6000 votes**
3. 30% = 21 students. Total = 21/0.3 = **70 students**
4. Savings = 25%. 25% = 5000. Salary = **₹20,000**
5. 1.2/0.8 = 1.5 → **50% increase**
