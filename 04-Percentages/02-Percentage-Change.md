# Percentages — Increase, Decrease & Successive Changes

## 🔍 When This Comes Up

- "X is increased/decreased by Y%"
- "After two increases, final value?"
- Population growth problems
- Price change problems

---

## 📐 Core Formulas

| Need | Formula |
|------|---------|
| New value after X% increase | Original × (100+X)/100 |
| New value after X% decrease | Original × (100−X)/100 |
| Successive change (a% then b%) | Net % = a + b + ab/100 |
| Find original after % change | New value ÷ multiplier |

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (% Increase)

**Problem:** A salary of ₹8000 is increased by 15%. New salary?

- = 8000 × 115/100 = **₹9200** ✅

---

### Example 2 (% Decrease)

**Problem:** Price of ₹500 is reduced by 20%. New price?

- = 500 × 80/100 = **₹400** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Successive Change)

**Problem:** Price increased by 20% then decreased by 10%. Net change?

- Net = 20 + (−10) + (20×(−10))/100
- = 20 − 10 − 2 = **+8% (net increase)** ✅

**Using multiplier:** 100 × 1.20 × 0.90 = 108 → **8% increase** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Population Growth)

**Problem:** Population = 50,000. Increases 10% in year 1, 20% in year 2. Final population?

- After Y1: 50000 × 1.1 = 55000
- After Y2: 55000 × 1.2 = **66,000** ✅

---

### Example 5 (Finding Original Value)

**Problem:** After a 20% increase, value is 84. What was the original?

- 84 = Original × 120/100
- Original = 84 × 100/120 = **70** ✅

---

### Example 6 (Two Successive Increases)

**Problem:** A number is increased by 25%, then by 20%. Net increase %?

- Net = 25 + 20 + (25×20)/100 = 45 + 5 = **50%** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcuts

1. **For successive changes:** Use a + b + ab/100 (use − for decrease)
2. **To find original after increase:** Divide by (1 + rate/100)
3. **To find original after decrease:** Divide by (1 − rate/100)
4. **Two equal successive changes of x%:** Net = 2x + x²/100 (increase) or 2x − x²/100 (decrease)

---

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. A population of 20,000 increases by 5%. New population?

2. Price rises 10% then falls 10%. Net change?

3. Price increases by 25%, then by 20%. Net increase?

4. After 2 successive 10% reductions, what is the final value if original is 1000?

5. A product's price was first increased by 30%, then decreased by 20%. What is the net % change?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. 20000 × 105/100 = **21,000**
2. Net = 10 + (−10) + (10×(−10))/100 = 0 − 1 = **−1% (decrease)**
3. 25 + 20 + 5 = **50%**
4. 1000 × 0.9 × 0.9 = **810**
5. Net = 30 + (−20) + (30×(−20))/100 = 30 − 20 − 6 = **+4% (increase)**
