# Probability: Compound Events — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** Two dice rolled. P(sum ≤ 4)?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Two fair dice are rolled; total outcomes = 36
- Find: Probability that the sum of both dice is 4 or less

**💡 What I'll use:** P(Event) = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes; list all pairs

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Total outcomes when rolling 2 dice = 6 × 6 = 36

Step 2: List all pairs (die1, die2) whose sum ≤ 4:
- Sum = 2: (1,1) → 1 pair
- Sum = 3: (1,2), (2,1) → 2 pairs
- Sum = 4: (1,3), (3,1), (2,2) → 3 pairs
- Total favorable = 1 + 2 + 3 = 6

Step 3: P(sum ≤ 4) = 6/36 = 1/6

**✅ Answer: 1/6**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** P(A) = 1/2, P(B) = 1/3, P(A∩B) = 1/6. Find P(A∪B).

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Individual probabilities of A and B and their intersection
- Find: Probability that A or B (or both) occurs

**💡 What I'll use:** Addition Rule — P(A∪B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A∩B)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Convert to a common denominator (6):
- P(A) = 1/2 = 3/6
- P(B) = 1/3 = 2/6
- P(A∩B) = 1/6

Step 2: P(A∪B) = 3/6 + 2/6 − 1/6 = 4/6 = 2/3

**✅ Answer: 2/3**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** Three coins. P(all heads)?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Three fair coins are tossed simultaneously; total outcomes = 2³ = 8
- Find: Probability of getting heads on all three coins

**💡 What I'll use:** Multiplication rule for independent events — P(H)³ = (1/2)³

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Each coin independently has P(H) = 1/2

Step 2: For independent events: P(all three heads) = P(H) × P(H) × P(H) = (1/2)³ = 1/8

Step 3: Verify — the only favorable outcome is HHH out of 8 total outcomes ✅

**✅ Answer: 1/8**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** Cards: P(face card)?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A standard deck of 52 cards, one card drawn at random
- Find: Probability of drawing a face card (Jack, Queen, or King)

**💡 What I'll use:** P(Event) = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Face cards = J, Q, K across 4 suits = 3 × 4 = 12 face cards

Step 2: Total cards = 52

Step 3: P(face card) = 12/52 = 3/13

**✅ Answer: 3/13**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** Bag: 2 red, 3 green, 5 white. P(not white)?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A bag with 2 red, 3 green, and 5 white balls (10 total), one ball drawn at random
- Find: Probability that the drawn ball is NOT white

**💡 What I'll use:** Count non-white balls directly — P(not white) = non-white / total

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Total balls = 2 + 3 + 5 = 10

Step 2: Non-white balls = 2 red + 3 green = 5

Step 3: P(not white) = 5/10 = 1/2

Step 4: Verify with complement — P(white) = 5/10 = 1/2 → P(not white) = 1 − 1/2 = 1/2 ✅

**✅ Answer: 1/2**
