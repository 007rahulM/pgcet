# Probability: Basic Concepts — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** A die is rolled. P(prime number)?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A single fair die is rolled; faces are {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}
- Find: Probability that the result is a prime number

**💡 What I'll use:** P(Event) = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: List prime numbers on a die → {2, 3, 5} = 3 favorable outcomes

Step 2: Total outcomes = 6

Step 3: P(prime) = 3/6 = 1/2

**✅ Answer: 1/2**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** Two coins tossed. P(at least one tail)?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Two fair coins are tossed simultaneously; sample space = {HH, HT, TH, TT}
- Find: Probability of getting at least one tail

**💡 What I'll use:** Complement rule — P(at least one tail) = 1 − P(no tail at all)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: The only outcome with NO tail is HH → P(no tail) = 1/4

Step 2: P(at least one tail) = 1 − 1/4 = 3/4

Step 3: Verify by direct count — outcomes with at least one tail: {HT, TH, TT} = 3 out of 4 ✅

**✅ Answer: 3/4**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** From a deck of 52 cards, P(an Ace)?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A standard deck of 52 cards, one card drawn at random
- Find: Probability of drawing an Ace

**💡 What I'll use:** P(Event) = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Number of Aces in a deck = 4 (one per suit: ♠ ♥ ♦ ♣)

Step 2: Total cards = 52

Step 3: P(Ace) = 4/52 = 1/13

**✅ Answer: 1/13**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** Bag: 3 red, 4 blue. P(blue)?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A bag containing 3 red and 4 blue balls (7 total), one ball drawn at random
- Find: Probability of drawing a blue ball

**💡 What I'll use:** P(Event) = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Favorable outcomes (blue balls) = 4

Step 2: Total balls = 3 + 4 = 7

Step 3: P(blue) = 4/7

**✅ Answer: 4/7**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** A die rolled. P(number > 4)?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A single fair die is rolled; faces are {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}
- Find: Probability of getting a number greater than 4

**💡 What I'll use:** P(Event) = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Numbers greater than 4 on a die → {5, 6} = 2 favorable outcomes

Step 2: Total outcomes = 6

Step 3: P(number > 4) = 2/6 = 1/3

**✅ Answer: 1/3**
