# CI vs SI — Comparison and Mixed Problems

## 🔍 When This Comes Up

- "Find difference between CI and SI"
- Problems mixing both SI and CI concepts
- "Which gives more interest and by how much?"

---

## 📐 Key Comparison Formulas

### For 2 years:
> **Difference (CI − SI) = P × (R/100)²**

### For 3 years:
> **Difference (CI − SI) = P × (R/100)² × (3 + R/100)**

> **Note:** CI is always ≥ SI. They are equal only for 1 year.

---

## 📐 Quick Reference Table

| Years | SI | CI | Difference |
|-------|----|----|-----------|
| 1 | PR/100 | PR/100 | 0 |
| 2 | 2PR/100 | P(2r + r²) where r = R/100 | Pr² |
| 3 | 3PR/100 | P(3r + 3r² + r³) | P(3r² + r³) |

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Difference for 2 years)

**Problem:** Find difference between CI and SI for ₹5000 at 4% for 2 years.

**Using formula:**
- Difference = P × (R/100)²
- = 5000 × (4/100)² = 5000 × 16/10000 = **₹8** ✅

**Verification:**
- SI = 5000 × 4 × 2 / 100 = 400
- CI = 5000 × 1.04² − 5000 = 5000 × 1.0816 − 5000 = 5408 − 5000 = 408
- Difference = 408 − 400 = **₹8** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Difference for 2 years — larger values)

**Problem:** Find difference between CI and SI for ₹8000 at 5% for 2 years.

- Difference = 8000 × (5/100)² = 8000 × 0.0025 = **₹20** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Difference for 3 years)

**Problem:** If CI on a sum for 3 years at 10% is ₹331 more than SI, find the principal.

- Difference = P × (R/100)² × (3 + R/100)
- 331 = P × (0.1)² × (3 + 0.1)
- 331 = P × 0.01 × 3.1
- 331 = P × 0.031
- P = 331 / 0.031 = **₹10,677** ≈ ₹10,000 ✅

---

### Example 4 (Finding Rate from Difference)

**Problem:** The difference between CI and SI on ₹2000 for 2 years is ₹20. Find rate %.

- Difference = P × (R/100)²
- 20 = 2000 × (R/100)²
- (R/100)² = 20/2000 = 0.01
- R/100 = 0.1
- R = **10%** ✅

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. What is the difference between CI and SI on ₹10000 at 10% for 2 years?

2. The SI on ₹5000 for 3 years is ₹900. Find CI for same principal, rate, and time.

3. If the difference between CI and SI on ₹4000 for 2 years is ₹40, find the rate.

4. In how many years will ₹1000 double at 12.5% SI?

5. At what rate (SI) will ₹3000 become ₹4500 in 5 years?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. 10000 × (10/100)² = 10000 × 0.01 = **₹100**
2. From SI: R = (900×100)/(5000×3) = 6%. CI = 5000×1.06³ − 5000 = 5955.08 − 5000 = **₹955.08**
3. 40 = 4000 × (R/100)² → (R/100)² = 0.01 → R = **10%**
4. T = 100/R = 100/12.5 = **8 years**
5. SI = 1500. R = (1500×100)/(3000×5) = **10%**
