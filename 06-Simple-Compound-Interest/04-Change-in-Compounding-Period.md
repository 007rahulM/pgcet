# Change in Compounding Period — Annual vs Half-Yearly vs Quarterly

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for:
- "compounded half-yearly instead of annually"
- "how much extra interest is paid?"
- "how much money would be saved if compounded annually?"
- comparison between two compounding methods for the same principal, rate, and time

---

## 📐 Core Idea

Smaller compounding periods give **more interest**.
So compare **Amounts**, not just rates.

### Annual Compounding
> `A = P(1 + R/100)^T`

### Half-Yearly Compounding
> `A = P(1 + R/200)^(2T)`

### Quarterly Compounding
> `A = P(1 + R/400)^(4T)`

### Savings by Annual Instead of Half-Yearly
> **Savings = Amount(half-yearly) − Amount(annually)**

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**❓ Question:** A company charges 10% per annum compound interest on a loan of ₹220000 for 2 years. If the company compounds every six months instead of every year, how much extra does the borrower pay? Round to one decimal place.

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Amount if compounded annually
- A₁ = 220000 × (1.10)^2
- A₁ = 220000 × 1.21 = **₹266200**

Step 2: Amount if compounded half-yearly
- Rate per half-year = 5%
- Number of periods = 4
- A₂ = 220000 × (1.05)^4
- (1.05)^4 = 1.21550625
- A₂ = 220000 × 1.21550625 = **₹267411.375**

Step 3: Extra paid / saved by annual compounding
- Difference = 267411.375 − 266200 = **₹1211.375**
- Rounded = **₹1211.4**

**✅ Answer: ₹1211.4**

---

### Example 2

**❓ Question:** ₹50000 is invested for 1 year at 12% p.a. Compare annual compounding and quarterly compounding.

**✏️ My Solution:**
- Annual amount = 50000 × 1.12 = ₹56000
- Quarterly amount = 50000 × (1.03)^4 = 50000 × 1.12550881 = ₹56275.44
- Extra due to quarterly compounding = **₹275.44**

**✅ Answer: Quarterly gives ₹275.44 more**

---

### Example 3

**❓ Question:** ₹80000 is compounded half-yearly at 10% p.a. for 1 year. Find the extra interest over simple interest for 1 year.

**✏️ My Solution:**
- Half-yearly amount = 80000 × (1.05)^2 = 80000 × 1.1025 = ₹88200
- CI = 88200 − 80000 = ₹8200
- SI for 1 year = 80000 × 10 × 1 / 100 = ₹8000
- Extra = **₹200**

**✅ Answer: ₹200**

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

- Annual vs half-yearly → compare `1 + R/100` with `(1 + R/200)^2`
- For 2 years: annual uses power 2, half-yearly uses power 4
- Ask yourself: **"Which method gives larger amount?"** → smaller compounding period wins
