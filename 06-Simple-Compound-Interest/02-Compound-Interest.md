# Compound Interest — Complete Guide

## 🔍 When This Comes Up

- "After how many years will amount double?"
- Half-yearly or quarterly compounding
- Finding CI on a principal

---

## 📐 Core Formula

> **Amount = P × (1 + R/100)^T**
> **CI = Amount − P**

---

## 📅 Special Cases for CI

| Compounding | Replace R with | Replace T with |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| Half-yearly | R/2 | 2T |
| Quarterly | R/4 | 4T |

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Annual CI)

**Problem:** ₹4000 at 10% per year compound interest for 2 years. Find Amount.

- Amount = 4000 × (110/100)² = 4000 × 1.21 = **₹4840**
- CI = 4840 − 4000 = **₹840** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Half-yearly CI)

**Problem:** ₹10000 at 10% per annum compounded half-yearly for 1 year.

- R = 10/2 = 5%, T = 2 (half years)
- Amount = 10000 × (105/100)² = 10000 × 1.1025 = **₹11025** ✅

---

### Example 3 (3 Years CI)

**Problem:** P = ₹10000, R = 10%, T = 3 years. Find CI (compound annually).

- Amount = 10000 × (1.1)³ = 10000 × 1.331 = 13310
- CI = 13310 − 10000 = **₹3310** ✅

---

### Example 4 (When does money double — Rule of 72)

**Shortcut:** Years to double = **72 ÷ Rate%**

- At 8%: doubles in 72/8 = **9 years** (approximate)
- At 12%: doubles in 72/12 = **6 years** ✅

**Exact method:** (1 + R/100)^T = 2. Solve for T.

---

### Example 5 (Quarterly Compounding)

**Problem:** ₹8000 at 12% per annum compounded quarterly for 1 year.

- R = 12/4 = 3%, T = 4 (quarters)
- Amount = 8000 × (1.03)⁴ = 8000 × 1.1255 = **₹9004** ✅

---

## 📐 CI Year-by-Year Method

For ₹1000 at 10% for 3 years:

| Year | Principal | Interest | End Amount |
|------|-----------|---------|------------|
| 1 | 1000 | 100 | 1100 |
| 2 | 1100 | 110 | 1210 |
| 3 | 1210 | 121 | 1331 |

CI = 1331 − 1000 = **₹331** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcuts

| Need | Shortcut |
|------|---------|
| CI for 2 years | P[(1+R/100)² − 1] |
| CI for 2 years (quick) | P[2R/100 + (R/100)²] |
| Years to double (CI) | 72/R (approx) |
| Years to double (exact for 100%) | Solve (1+R/100)^T = 2 |

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. P = ₹6000, R = 10%, T = 2 years. Find CI.

2. ₹5000 at 8% per annum CI, compounded half-yearly for 1 year. Find amount.

3. P = ₹10000, R = 10%, T = 3 years. Find CI (compound annually).

4. CI for ₹2000 at 20% for 2 years (compounded annually)?

5. At what rate (CI) will ₹2000 become ₹2420 in 2 years?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. 6000 × 1.1² − 6000 = 7260 − 6000 = **₹1260**
2. R=4%, T=2. Amount = 5000 × 1.04² = 5000 × 1.0816 = **₹5408**; CI = **₹408**
3. 10000 × 1.1³ − 10000 = 13310 − 10000 = **₹3310**
4. 2000 × 1.2² − 2000 = 2880 − 2000 = **₹880**
5. 2000 × (1 + R/100)² = 2420 → (1 + R/100)² = 1.21 → 1 + R/100 = 1.1 → R = **10%**
