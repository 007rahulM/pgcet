# Compound Interest — Complete Guide

## 🔍 When This Comes Up

- "After how many years will amount double?"
- Half-yearly or quarterly compounding
- Finding CI on a principal

---

## 📐 Core Formula

> **Amount = P × (1 + R/100)^T**
> **CI = Amount − P**

---

## 📅 Special Cases for CI

| Compounding | Replace R with | Replace T with |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| Half-yearly | R/2 | 2T |
| Quarterly | R/4 | 4T |

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**❓ Question:** ₹4000 at 10% per year compound interest for 2 years. Find Amount and CI.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Principal = ₹4000, Rate = 10% per year, Time = 2 years, compounded annually
- Find: Final Amount and Compound Interest

**💡 What I'll use:** Amount = P × (1 + R/100)^T, then CI = Amount − P

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Calculate Amount
Amount = 4000 × (1 + 10/100)² = 4000 × (1.1)² = 4000 × 1.21 = **₹4840**

Step 2: Calculate CI
CI = 4840 − 4000 = **₹840**

**✅ Answer: Amount = ₹4840, CI = ₹840**

---

### Example 2

**❓ Question:** ₹10000 at 10% per annum compounded half-yearly for 1 year. Find Amount.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Principal = ₹10000, Rate = 10% per annum, Time = 1 year, compounded half-yearly
- Find: Final Amount

**💡 What I'll use:** For half-yearly compounding — replace R with R/2 and T with 2T

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Adjust rate and time for half-yearly
Effective R = 10/2 = 5%, Effective T = 2 × 1 = 2 periods

Step 2: Apply formula
Amount = 10000 × (1 + 5/100)² = 10000 × (1.05)² = 10000 × 1.1025 = **₹11025**

**✅ Answer: Amount = ₹11025**

---

### Example 3

**❓ Question:** P = ₹10000, R = 10%, T = 3 years. Find CI (compounded annually).

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Principal = ₹10000, Rate = 10%, Time = 3 years, annual compounding
- Find: Compound Interest

**💡 What I'll use:** Amount = P × (1 + R/100)^T, then CI = Amount − P

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Calculate Amount
Amount = 10000 × (1.1)³ = 10000 × 1.331 = ₹13310

Step 2: Calculate CI
CI = 13310 − 10000 = **₹3310**

**✅ Answer: CI = ₹3310**

---

### Example 4

**❓ Question:** At approximately what rate will money double under compound interest?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: We want to find the time to double money at a given rate
- Find: A quick way to estimate doubling time using compound interest

**💡 What I'll use:** Rule of 72 — Years to double ≈ 72 ÷ Rate%

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Apply Rule of 72
At 8%: doubles in 72 ÷ 8 = **9 years** (approximate)
At 12%: doubles in 72 ÷ 12 = **6 years** (approximate)

Step 2: Exact method (when needed)
Solve (1 + R/100)^T = 2 for T

**✅ Answer: Doubling time ≈ 72 ÷ Rate (e.g., 9 years at 8%)**

---

### Example 5

**❓ Question:** ₹8000 at 12% per annum compounded quarterly for 1 year. Find Amount.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Principal = ₹8000, Rate = 12% per annum, Time = 1 year, compounded quarterly
- Find: Final Amount

**💡 What I'll use:** For quarterly compounding — replace R with R/4 and T with 4T

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Adjust for quarterly compounding
Effective R = 12/4 = 3%, Effective T = 4 × 1 = 4 periods

Step 2: Apply formula
Amount = 8000 × (1.03)⁴ = 8000 × 1.1255 = **₹9004**

**✅ Answer: Amount = ₹9004**

## 📐 CI Year-by-Year Method

For ₹1000 at 10% for 3 years:

| Year | Principal | Interest | End Amount |
|------|-----------|---------|------------|
| 1 | 1000 | 100 | 1100 |
| 2 | 1100 | 110 | 1210 |
| 3 | 1210 | 121 | 1331 |

CI = 1331 − 1000 = **₹331** ✅

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcuts

| Need | Shortcut |
|------|---------|
| CI for 2 years | P[(1+R/100)² − 1] |
| CI for 2 years (quick) | P[2R/100 + (R/100)²] |
| Years to double (CI) | 72/R (approx) |
| Years to double (exact for 100%) | Solve (1+R/100)^T = 2 |
