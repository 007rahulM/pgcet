# CI vs SI Comparison — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** What is the difference between CI and SI on ₹10000 at 10% for 2 years?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: P = ₹10000, R = 10%, T = 2 years
- Find: CI − SI (the extra interest earned with CI)

**💡 What I'll use:** 2-year shortcut formula: Difference = P × (R/100)²

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Apply the formula directly
Difference = 10000 × (10/100)² = 10000 × 0.01 = **₹100**

Step 2: Verify
SI = 10000 × 10 × 2 / 100 = ₹2000
CI = 10000 × 1.1² − 10000 = 12100 − 10000 = ₹2100
Difference = 2100 − 2000 = ₹100 ✓

**✅ Answer: Difference = ₹100**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** The SI on ₹5000 for 3 years is ₹900. Find CI for same principal, rate, and time.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: SI = ₹900 on P = ₹5000 for T = 3 years; use same P, R, T for CI
- Find: First derive R from SI, then compute CI

**💡 What I'll use:** R = (SI × 100) / (P × T), then CI = P × (1 + R/100)³ − P

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find rate from SI
R = (900 × 100) / (5000 × 3) = 90000 / 15000 = **6%**

Step 2: Calculate Amount with CI
Amount = 5000 × (1.06)³ = 5000 × 1.191016 = ₹5955.08

Step 3: CI = Amount − P
CI = 5955.08 − 5000 = **₹955.08**

**✅ Answer: CI = ₹955.08**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** If the difference between CI and SI on ₹4000 for 2 years is ₹40, find the rate.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: P = ₹4000, difference (CI − SI) = ₹40, T = 2 years
- Find: Rate of interest

**💡 What I'll use:** Work backwards: Difference = P × (R/100)² → solve for R

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Set up the equation
40 = 4000 × (R/100)²

Step 2: Isolate (R/100)²
(R/100)² = 40 / 4000 = 0.01

Step 3: Solve for R
R/100 = √0.01 = 0.1
R = **10%**

**✅ Answer: Rate = 10%**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** In how many years will ₹1000 double at 12.5% SI?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: P = ₹1000, final amount = ₹2000 (double), R = 12.5%
- Find: Time in years

**💡 What I'll use:** For doubling: SI = P, so T = 100/R

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Use the doubling shortcut
SI needed = P = ₹1000
T = (SI × 100) / (P × R) = (1000 × 100) / (1000 × 12.5) = 100 / 12.5 = **8 years**

Or using the shortcut: T = 100/R = 100/12.5 = **8 years**

**✅ Answer: 8 years**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** At what rate (SI) will ₹3000 become ₹4500 in 5 years?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: P = ₹3000, Amount = ₹4500, T = 5 years
- Find: Rate of simple interest

**💡 What I'll use:** First find SI, then R = (SI × 100) / (P × T)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find SI
SI = Amount − P = 4500 − 3000 = ₹1500

Step 2: Find Rate
R = (1500 × 100) / (3000 × 5) = 150000 / 15000 = **10%**

**✅ Answer: Rate = 10%**
