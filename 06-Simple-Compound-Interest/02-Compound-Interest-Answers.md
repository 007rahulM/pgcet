# Compound Interest — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** P = ₹6000, R = 10%, T = 2 years. Find CI.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Principal = ₹6000, Rate = 10%, Time = 2 years, compounded annually
- Find: Compound Interest

**💡 What I'll use:** Amount = P × (1 + R/100)^T; CI = Amount − P

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Calculate Amount
Amount = 6000 × (1.10)² = 6000 × 1.21 = ₹7260

Step 2: CI = Amount − P
CI = 7260 − 6000 = **₹1260**

**✅ Answer: CI = ₹1260**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** ₹5000 at 8% per annum CI, compounded half-yearly for 1 year. Find amount.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: P = ₹5000, annual rate = 8%, compounded half-yearly, T = 1 year
- Find: Final Amount

**💡 What I'll use:** For half-yearly: effective R = 8/2 = 4%, effective T = 2 × 1 = 2 periods

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Adjust rate and time
Effective R = 4%, Effective T = 2

Step 2: Apply formula
Amount = 5000 × (1.04)² = 5000 × 1.0816 = **₹5408**

**✅ Answer: Amount = ₹5408**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** At what rate (CI) will ₹2000 become ₹2420 in 2 years?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: P = ₹2000, Amount = ₹2420, T = 2 years, compounded annually
- Find: Rate of interest

**💡 What I'll use:** (1 + R/100)² = Amount/P; solve for R

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Set up the equation
(1 + R/100)² = 2420 / 2000 = 1.21

Step 2: Take the square root
1 + R/100 = √1.21 = 1.1

Step 3: Solve for R
R/100 = 0.1
R = **10%**

**✅ Answer: Rate = 10%**
