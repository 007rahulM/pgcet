# Coincidence, Opposite, and Right Angle Times — Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** Between 6 and 7 o'clock, when do the hands coincide?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 6–7 o'clock range; coincidence = angle 0°
- Find: Minute M

**💡 What I'll use:** M = 60H/11, H = 6

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: M = 60 × 6 / 11 = 360/11 = **32 8/11 minutes**

Step 2: Hands coincide at **6:32 8/11** (approximately 6:33).

**✅ Answer: 32 8/11 minutes past 6**

---

### Q2

**❓ Question:** Between 1 and 2 o'clock, when are the hands at 90°?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 1–2 o'clock; angle = 90°, H = 1
- Find: All valid minute values M

**💡 What I'll use:** |30 × 1 − 5.5M| = 90

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: |30 − 5.5M| = 90

Case A: 30 − 5.5M = 90 → 5.5M = −60 → M = −60/5.5 → negative, **reject**

Case B: 30 − 5.5M = −90 → 5.5M = 120 → M = 120/5.5 = **21 9/11 minutes**

Step 2: Only one valid answer (Case A rejected because M must be 0–59).

**✅ Answer: 21 9/11 minutes past 1 (approximately 1:22)**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** How many times do the hands form a straight line (180°) in 24 hours?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 24-hour period; straight line means 180° (opposite)
- Find: Count

**💡 What I'll use:** In 12 hours, hands are opposite 11 times

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: In 12 hours: the hour hand completes 1 full round, minute hand completes 12.
They are opposite 12 − 1 = **11 times** in 12 hours.

Step 2: In 24 hours: 11 × 2 = **22 times**.

**✅ Answer: 22 times**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** How many times do hands coincide between 11:00 and 1:00?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 2-hour window (11 to 1 = crosses 12)
- Find: Coincidences in this window

**💡 What I'll use:** Coincidences in each hour-block

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: In 11–12 range: M = 60×11/11 = 60 min → that's exactly 12:00, the boundary.
Step 2: In 12–1 range: M = 60×12/11 = 720/11 ≈ 65.5 → outside [0,60), so the next coincidence in 12–1 is at M = 60×0/11 = 0 (i.e., exactly 12:00).
Step 3: The coincidence exactly at 12:00 is counted once.
Step 4: Between 11:00 and 1:00 there are **2 coincidences**: once around 12:00:00 and once more (the 12:00 mark is the coincidence for both 11→12 range end and 12→1 range start — counted once).

Total = **2 coincidences** (at 12:00:00 and approximately 1:05 27/11 — but 1:05 is after 1:00, so strictly between 11:00 and 1:00 there is only **1 coincidence** at exactly 12:00).

**✅ Answer: 1 coincidence (at 12:00 noon/midnight)**

### Q6

**❓ Question:** Between 9 and 10 o'clock, when are the hands at right angles?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 9–10 range; angle = 90°, H = 9
- Find: Both valid minute values

**💡 What I'll use:** |30 × 9 − 5.5M| = 90

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: |270 − 5.5M| = 90

Case A: 270 − 5.5M = 90 → 5.5M = 180 → M = 180/5.5 = **32 8/11 minutes**
Case B: 270 − 5.5M = −90 → 5.5M = 360 → M = 360/5.5 = 65 5/11 → exceeds 60, **reject**

Step 2: Only Case A is valid.

**✅ Answer: 32 8/11 minutes past 9 (approximately 9:33)**
