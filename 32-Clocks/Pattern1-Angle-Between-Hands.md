# Pattern 1: Angle Between Hands

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

- "At 3:20, the angle between the hour and minute hands is…"
- "What is the angle between the hands of a clock at 6:30?"
- "At what time between 4 and 5 o'clock are the hands at right angles?"

---

## 🧠 The Golden Formula

> **Angle = |30H − 5.5M|**
> - **H** = hours (use 1–12; for 12 o'clock use H = 0 or 12 consistently)
> - **M** = minutes (0–59)
> - If result > 180°, the **reflex angle = 360° − result** (use the smaller angle unless asked for reflex)

### Why this formula?

| Hand | Movement per minute |
|------|-------------------|
| Minute hand | 360° ÷ 60 = **6° per minute** |
| Hour hand | 360° ÷ 720 = **0.5° per minute** |

At time H hours and M minutes:
- Minute hand position = 6M degrees from 12
- Hour hand position = 30H + 0.5M degrees from 12
- Difference = |30H + 0.5M − 6M| = **|30H − 5.5M|**

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**❓ Question:** At 3:20 on a clock, the angle between the hour and minute hands is:
(A) 10°  (B) 20°  (C) 30°  (D) 40°

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Time = 3:20 (H = 3, M = 20)
- Find: Angle between the two hands

**💡 What I'll use:** Angle = |30H − 5.5M|

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Plug in H = 3, M = 20
Angle = |30 × 3 − 5.5 × 20|
      = |90 − 110|
      = |−20|
      = **20°**

**✅ Answer: (B) 20°**

---

### Example 2

**❓ Question:** What is the angle between the hands of a clock at 6:00?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Time = 6:00 (H = 6, M = 0)
- Find: Angle

**💡 What I'll use:** Angle = |30H − 5.5M|

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Angle = |30 × 6 − 5.5 × 0| = |180 − 0| = **180°**

(Hands point in exactly opposite directions — a straight line.)

**✅ Answer: 180°**

---

### Example 3

**❓ Question:** Find the angle between the clock hands at 9:00.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: H = 9, M = 0
- Find: Angle

**💡 What I'll use:** Angle = |30H − 5.5M|

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Angle = |30 × 9 − 5.5 × 0| = |270 − 0| = 270°
Step 2: 270° > 180°, so the smaller angle = 360° − 270° = **90°**

**✅ Answer: 90°**

---

### Example 4

**❓ Question:** At 12:00 (noon), the angle between the hands is:

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: H = 12 (use 0 for formula), M = 0
- Find: Angle

**💡 What I'll use:** Angle = |30 × 0 − 5.5 × 0| = 0° (both hands at 12)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: At exactly 12:00, both hands point to 12.
Angle = **0°**

**✅ Answer: 0°**

---

### Example 5

**❓ Question:** At 4:45, what is the angle between the hour and minute hands?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: H = 4, M = 45
- Find: Angle

**💡 What I'll use:** Angle = |30H − 5.5M|

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Angle = |30 × 4 − 5.5 × 45|
              = |120 − 247.5|
              = |−127.5|
              = **127.5°**

**✅ Answer: 127.5°**

---

### Example 6

**❓ Question:** At what time between 2 and 3 o'clock are the hands of the clock at a right angle (90°)?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Time is between 2:00 and 3:00; angle = 90°
- Find: Exact minute M

**💡 What I'll use:** Set |30H − 5.5M| = 90, solve for M with H = 2

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: |30 × 2 − 5.5M| = 90
        |60 − 5.5M| = 90

Case A: 60 − 5.5M = 90 → 5.5M = −30 → M = −30/5.5 (negative, reject)
Case B: 60 − 5.5M = −90 → 5.5M = 150 → M = 150/5.5 = **27 3/11 minutes**

Step 2: At 2 hours 27 3/11 minutes, the hands are at 90°.

**✅ Answer: 2:27 3/11 (approximately 2:27)**

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Strategy

1. Write down H and M.
2. Apply **Angle = |30H − 5.5M|**.
3. If answer > 180°, subtract from 360°.
4. For "find the time" problems: set the formula equal to the target angle and solve for M (two cases: ±).
