# Binomial Expansion — Solutions

1. Coefficient of \(x^4\) in \((1+2x)^6\): \({6\choose4}2^4=15\cdot16=240\).  
   **Answer: 240**

2. \(\frac{{n\choose2}}{{n\choose3}}=\frac{5}{4}\Rightarrow \frac{3}{n-2}=\frac{5}{4}\Rightarrow n=\frac{22}{5}\) not integer, so use 3rd:4th as \({n\choose2}:{n\choose3}=5:4\) impossible for integer \(n\). Check reversed ratio: \({n\choose3}:{n\choose2}=5:4\Rightarrow \frac{n-2}{3}=\frac{5}{4}\Rightarrow n=\frac{23}{4}\) impossible.  
   **Answer: No positive integer \(n\)**

3. 5th term means \(r=4\):  
   \(T_5={7\choose4}(2x)^3(-3)^4=35\cdot8x^3\cdot81=22680x^3\).  
   **Answer: \(22680x^3\)**

4. From solved example in README.

5. General term: \({9\choose r}(x^2)^{9-r}(x^{-1})^r={9\choose r}x^{18-3r}\).  
   For constant term: \(18-3r=0\Rightarrow r=6\).  
   Constant term \(={9\choose6}=84\).  
   **Answer: 84**
