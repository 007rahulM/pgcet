# Profit & Loss: Discount — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** MP = ₹1000, Discount 25%. Find SP.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Marked Price = ₹1000, Discount % = 25%
- Find: Selling Price after discount

**💡 What I'll use:** SP = MP × (100 − D%) / 100

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Apply the discount formula
SP = 1000 × (100 − 25) / 100 = 1000 × 75 / 100 = **₹750**

**✅ Answer: ₹750**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** SP = ₹540 after 10% discount. Find MP.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: SP = ₹540, Discount % = 10%
- Find: Original Marked Price before discount

**💡 What I'll use:** MP = SP × 100 / (100 − D%)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Work backwards from SP
MP = 540 × 100 / (100 − 10) = 540 × 100 / 90 = **₹600**

**✅ Answer: ₹600**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** CP = ₹300. Marked 50% above CP. 20% discount given. Find profit %.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: CP = ₹300, markup = 50%, discount = 20%
- Find: Actual profit percentage

**💡 What I'll use:** MP = CP × 1.5, then SP = MP × 0.8, then Profit % = (SP − CP)/CP × 100

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find Marked Price
MP = 300 × 150 / 100 = ₹450

Step 2: Apply discount to find SP
SP = 450 × 80 / 100 = ₹360

Step 3: Calculate profit %
Profit = 360 − 300 = ₹60
Profit % = (60 / 300) × 100 = **20%**

**✅ Answer: 20% profit**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** A trader wants 25% profit on an item costing ₹400. He gives 20% discount. What should MP be?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: CP = ₹400, desired profit = 25%, discount to give = 20%
- Find: Marked Price to set

**💡 What I'll use:** First find required SP, then work back to find MP that gives this SP after 20% off

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find required SP for 25% profit
SP = 400 × 125 / 100 = ₹500

Step 2: If MP × 0.80 = 500, find MP
MP = 500 / 0.80 = **₹625**

**✅ Answer: MP = ₹625**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** MP = ₹500, SP = ₹425. Find discount %.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Marked Price = ₹500, Selling Price = ₹425
- Find: Discount percentage

**💡 What I'll use:** D% = (Discount / MP) × 100

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Calculate discount amount
Discount = MP − SP = 500 − 425 = ₹75

Step 2: Find discount %
D% = (75 / 500) × 100 = **15%**

**✅ Answer: 15% discount**

### Q6

**❓ Question:** CP = ₹250. MP = ₹300. Shopkeeper gives a 10% discount on MP. Find actual profit %.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: CP = ₹250, MP = ₹300, Discount = 10% on MP
- Find: Actual profit on CP

**💡 What I'll use:** SP = MP × 0.9, then Profit % = (SP − CP)/CP × 100

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find SP after 10% discount on MP
SP = 300 × 90 / 100 = ₹270

Step 2: Calculate profit
Profit = 270 − 250 = ₹20

Step 3: Profit %
Profit % = (20 / 250) × 100 = **8%**

**✅ Answer: 8% profit**

### Q7

**❓ Question:** Two items with MP ₹500 each. 10% discount on first, 20% on second. Total SP?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Both items have MP = ₹500; first gets 10% off, second gets 20% off
- Find: Combined total SP

**💡 What I'll use:** Calculate SP for each item separately, then add

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: SP of first item (10% discount)
SP₁ = 500 × 90 / 100 = ₹450

Step 2: SP of second item (20% discount)
SP₂ = 500 × 80 / 100 = ₹400

Step 3: Total SP
Total = 450 + 400 = **₹850**

**✅ Answer: ₹850**

### Q8

**❓ Question:** A shopkeeper marks price 30% above CP and gives 10% discount. Find profit %.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Markup = 30% above CP, Discount = 10% on MP
- Find: Net profit percentage on CP

**💡 What I'll use:** Combined multiplier: Net = (1 + markup%) × (1 − discount%)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Apply combined multiplier
Net factor = 1.30 × 0.90 = 1.17

Step 2: Interpret result
Net factor = 1.17 means SP = 1.17 × CP

Profit % = (1.17 − 1) × 100 = **17%**

**✅ Answer: 17% profit**
