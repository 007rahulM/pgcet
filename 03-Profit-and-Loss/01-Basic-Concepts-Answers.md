# Profit & Loss: Basic Concepts — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** CP = ₹500, SP = ₹625. Profit %?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Cost Price = ₹500, Selling Price = ₹625
- Find: Profit percentage

**💡 What I'll use:** Profit % = (Profit / CP) × 100

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Calculate profit
Profit = SP − CP = 625 − 500 = ₹125

Step 2: Apply the formula
Profit % = (125 / 500) × 100 = **25%**

**✅ Answer: 25%**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** An item bought for ₹800, sold at 15% profit. SP?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: CP = ₹800, Profit % = 15%
- Find: Selling Price

**💡 What I'll use:** SP = CP × (100 + P%) / 100

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Apply the formula directly
SP = 800 × (100 + 15) / 100 = 800 × 115 / 100 = **₹920**

**✅ Answer: ₹920**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** SP = ₹990, sold at 10% profit. CP?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: SP = ₹990, Profit % = 10%
- Find: Cost Price

**💡 What I'll use:** CP = SP × 100 / (100 + P%)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Plug values into the reverse formula
CP = 990 × 100 / (100 + 10) = 990 × 100 / 110 = **₹900**

**✅ Answer: ₹900**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** SP = ₹630, sold at 10% loss. CP?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: SP = ₹630, Loss % = 10%
- Find: Cost Price

**💡 What I'll use:** CP = SP × 100 / (100 − L%)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Apply the reverse formula for loss
CP = 630 × 100 / (100 − 10) = 630 × 100 / 90 = **₹700**

**✅ Answer: ₹700**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** Two items bought for ₹300 each. One sold at 10% profit, other at 10% loss. Net profit or loss?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: CP of each item = ₹300; one sold at +10%, other at −10%
- Find: Net profit or loss overall

**💡 What I'll use:** Calculate SP of each, find total CP vs total SP

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: SP of item sold at 10% profit
SP₁ = 300 × 110 / 100 = ₹330 → Profit = ₹30

Step 2: SP of item sold at 10% loss
SP₂ = 300 × 90 / 100 = ₹270 → Loss = ₹30

Step 3: Net result
Profit = ₹30, Loss = ₹30 → **Net = 0 (no profit, no loss)**

**✅ Answer: No net profit or loss**

### Q6

**❓ Question:** A trader sells goods at 5% loss on CP but uses 900g instead of 1000g. Find actual profit/loss %.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Nominal loss = 5% on CP, but only gives 900g per claimed 1000g
- Find: Actual profit or loss percentage considering the false weight

**💡 What I'll use:** Find the true SP and true CP for the 900g actually given

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Let CP of 1000g = ₹100
Nominal SP (at 5% loss) = 100 × 95/100 = ₹95

Step 2: He collects ₹95 but only gives 900g
Actual CP of 900g = (900/1000) × 100 = ₹90

Step 3: Calculate actual profit
Profit = 95 − 90 = ₹5
Profit % = (5/90) × 100 ≈ **5.56% profit**

**✅ Answer: ≈ 5.56% profit**

### Q7

**❓ Question:** CP of 20 articles = SP of 25 articles. Profit or loss %?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: The cost to buy 20 articles equals the revenue from selling 25 articles
- Find: Whether this is a profit or loss, and what percentage

**💡 What I'll use:** Express SP per article in terms of CP per article, then compare

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Let CP of 1 article = ₹1
Total CP of 20 articles = ₹20

Step 2: SP of 25 articles = ₹20 (given equal to CP of 20)
SP per article = 20/25 = ₹0.80

Step 3: Compare with CP per article (₹1)
SP < CP → Loss
Loss % = (1 − 0.8) / 1 × 100 = **20% Loss**

**✅ Answer: 20% Loss**

### Q8

**❓ Question:** A man sells an item at 12% profit. Had he sold it for ₹18 more, profit would be 18%. Find CP.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: At current SP → 12% profit; at SP + ₹18 → 18% profit
- Find: Cost Price

**💡 What I'll use:** The ₹18 difference equals the difference between 18% and 12% profit, i.e., 6% of CP

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Set up the equation
SP₁ = CP × 1.12 and SP₂ = CP × 1.18
SP₂ − SP₁ = 18

Step 2: Substitute
CP × 1.18 − CP × 1.12 = 18
CP × 0.06 = 18
CP = 18 / 0.06 = **₹300**

**✅ Answer: CP = ₹300**
