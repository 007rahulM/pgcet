# Profit & Loss — Basic Concepts

## 🧠 The Core Idea

You buy something at a price (CP) and sell it at another price (SP).
- If SP > CP → You made a **Profit**
- If SP < CP → You made a **Loss**

---

## 📐 Key Formulas

| What to Find | Formula |
|-------------|---------|
| Profit | SP − CP |
| Loss | CP − SP |
| Profit % | (Profit / CP) × 100 |
| Loss % | (Loss / CP) × 100 |
| SP (given P%) | CP × (100 + P%) / 100 |
| SP (given L%) | CP × (100 − L%) / 100 |
| CP (given SP and P%) | SP × 100 / (100 + P%) |
| CP (given SP and L%) | SP × 100 / (100 − L%) |

> **Golden Rule: Profit % and Loss % are ALWAYS on CP (not SP)**

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**Problem:** A shopkeeper buys a shirt for ₹400 and sells it for ₹500. Profit %?

- Profit = 500 − 400 = ₹100
- Profit % = (100/400) × 100 = **25%** ✅

---

### Example 2

**Problem:** An article bought for ₹600 is sold at 20% profit. Find SP.

- SP = 600 × (100+20)/100 = 600 × 120/100 = **₹720** ✅

---

### Example 3

**Problem:** A book sold for ₹540 at 10% loss. Find CP.

- CP = 540 × 100 / (100−10) = 540 × 100/90 = **₹600** ✅

---

### Example 4

**Problem:** An article sold for ₹850 at 25% profit. Find CP.

- CP = 850 × 100 / (100+25) = 850 × 100/125 = **₹680** ✅

---

### Example 5 (Selling price to get desired profit)

**Problem:** CP = ₹750. What should be the SP for 16% profit?

- SP = 750 × 116/100 = **₹870** ✅

---

### Example 6 (Two items — one profit, one loss)

**Problem:** Two watches sold for ₹1200 each. One at 20% profit, other at 20% loss. Overall profit or loss?

> **Trick:** When same selling price, same % profit & loss → always a LOSS
> Loss % = (Common %)² / 100 = 20²/100 = 4%

**Verification:**
- Profit item: CP = 1200 × 100/120 = ₹1000
- Loss item: CP = 1200 × 100/80 = ₹1500
- Total CP = 2500, Total SP = 2400
- Loss = ₹100 → Loss% = 100/2500 × 100 = **4%** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

**Memorize these multiplier shortcuts:**

| Profit/Loss | Multiplier for SP | Multiplier for CP |
|-------------|------------------|------------------|
| 20% profit | × 1.2 | ÷ 1.2 = × 5/6 |
| 25% profit | × 1.25 = × 5/4 | ÷ 1.25 = × 4/5 |
| 10% loss | × 0.9 | ÷ 0.9 = × 10/9 |
| 20% loss | × 0.8 | ÷ 0.8 = × 5/4 |

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. CP = ₹500, SP = ₹625. Profit %?

2. An item bought for ₹800, sold at 15% profit. SP?

3. SP = ₹990, sold at 10% profit. CP?

4. SP = ₹630, sold at 10% loss. CP?

5. Two items bought for ₹300 each. One sold at 10% profit, other at 10% loss. Net?

6. A trader sells goods at 5% loss on CP but uses 900g instead of 1000g. Find actual profit/loss%.

7. CP of 20 articles = SP of 25 articles. Profit or loss %?

8. A man sells an item at 12% profit. Had he sold it for ₹18 more, profit would be 18%. Find CP.

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. Profit = 125. P% = 125/500 × 100 = **25%**
2. 800 × 1.15 = **₹920**
3. 990 × 100/110 = **₹900**
4. 630 × 100/90 = **₹700**
5. P = 30, L = 30. **Net = 0 (no profit no loss)**
6. Let CP per 1000g = ₹100. He sells at 5% loss → SP for 1000g = ₹95.
   But he gives only 800g. So he collects ₹95 for 800g.
   CP of 800g = ₹80.
   Profit = 95 − 80 = ₹15. Profit% = 15/80 × 100 = **18.75%**
7. CP of 20 = SP of 25 → 25 items' SP = 20 items' CP. SP per item = 20/25 CP = 0.8 CP → **20% Loss**
8. Let CP = x. 12% profit SP = 1.12x. 18% profit SP = 1.18x. 1.18x − 1.12x = 18 → 0.06x = 18 → x = **₹300**
