# Dishonest Dealer — False Weights Trick

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for:
- "A shopkeeper uses 900g weight instead of 1000g"
- "A dealer cheats by giving less quantity"
- "A shopkeeper earns X% by using a false measure"

---

## 🧠 The Concept (Why This Works)

The dealer **claims** to sell a certain weight but actually gives **less**.

Example:
- Claims to sell 1000g at the price of 1000g
- But actually gives only 900g
- So he's getting paid for 1000g but only giving 900g

He made a "free" profit of (1000 − 900) = 100g!

---

## 📐 Key Formula

> **Profit % = (Error / True Value − Error) × 100**

OR equivalently:
> **Profit % = (True Weight − False Weight) / False Weight × 100**

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**Problem:** A shopkeeper uses a 900g weight instead of 1kg (1000g). Find profit %.

- Error = 1000 − 900 = 100g
- Profit % = (100 / (1000 − 100)) × 100 = (100/900) × 100 = **11.11%** ✅

---

### Example 2 (With own buying price manipulation)

**Problem:** A dishonest dealer professes to sell goods at cost price but uses only 800g weight instead of 1000g. Find profit %.

- Profit % = (200/800) × 100 = **25%** ✅

---

### Example 3 (With both buying and selling cheating)

**Problem:** A dealer buys goods using 900g instead of 1000g, and sells using 1100g instead of 1000g. Find total profit/loss %.

Let true market price = ₹1 per gram.

**Buying side:**
- Dealer uses 900g weight but claims to weigh 1000g
- He pays only for 900g → pays ₹900 for what is truly 1000g of goods
- So CP per 1000g of goods = **₹900**

**Selling side:**
- Dealer uses 1100g weight but charges for 1000g
- He gives out 1100g but collects money for only 1000g → SP for 1100g = ₹1000
- So SP per 1000g of goods sold = 1000 × (1000/1100) = ₹909

**Profit % on 1000g:**
- CP = ₹900 (buying), SP = ₹909 (selling on per-1000g basis)
- Profit % = (909 − 900)/900 × 100 ≈ **1%**

> **Note:** Both cheats partially cancel each other. The net profit is only ~1%. In exams, the exact answer depends on what numbers are given.

---

### Example 4 (Percentage profit with selling discount)

**Problem:** A shopkeeper buys goods at ₹80 per kg, and sells them at ₹100 per kg but uses a weight of 900g instead of 1000g. Find actual profit %.

**Step 1:**
- For 1000g: CP = ₹80, but he only gives 900g
- SP for 900g = ₹100 (customer thinks they're buying 1000g)

**Step 2:**
- CP of 900g = 80 × 900/1000 = ₹72
- SP = ₹100

**Step 3:**
- Profit = 28, Profit % = 28/72 × 100 = **38.9%** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

```
Profit% = (True − False) / False × 100
```

**Example:** 950g instead of 1000g:
→ (1000 − 950) / 950 × 100 = 50/950 × 100 = 5.26%

---

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. A dealer uses 800g instead of 1kg. Profit %?

2. A vendor uses 950g instead of 1kg. He also sells at 10% profit over CP. Total profit %?

3. A milkman mixes 20% water in milk and sells at CP. Profit %?

4. A shopkeeper sells at a 5% loss but uses 800g instead of 1000g. Net profit/loss %?

5. A dealer claims to sell at CP but uses 750g instead of 1000g. Profit %?

6. A shopkeeper uses a faulty balance — one pan weighs 10g more than other. He always puts goods on the heavier side. Profit%?

7. A dealer buys 10 items for ₹100 (₹10 each) and sells 9 items for ₹100. Profit %?

8. A dishonest trader uses false weight. He gets 11.11% profit. What weight does he use for 1 kg?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. (200/800)×100 = **25%**
2. He gets 1000g at 950g cost. Effective gain from weight = 50/950 = 5.26%. Also sells at 10% profit on CP. Total Profit% = (1 + 10/100) × (1000/950) − 1 = 1.1 × 1.0526 − 1 = 1.158 − 1 = **15.8%**
3. For 100 litres sold (80L milk + 20L water), costs of pure milk = 80 units. SP = 100 units. Profit = **25%**
4. He sells 5% loss on CP, but gives only 800g. SP for 800g = 0.95 × CP of 1000g. Actual CP of 800g = 0.8 × CP of 1000g. Profit% = (0.95−0.8)/0.8 × 100 = **18.75%**
5. (250/750)×100 = **33.33%**
6. He weighs with heavy side for goods → gives less. If one pan is 10g more heavy (goods side), he gives 10g less per kg → (10/990) × 100 ≈ **1.01%**
7. Buys 10 for 100, sells 9 for 100 → 10 items cost 100, 10 items sell for 100×10/9 = 111.1. Profit% = **11.1%**
8. Profit% = 11.11% → (1000−x)/x = 1/9 → 9000−9x = x → x = 900g. Uses **900g**
