# Dishonest Dealer — False Weights Trick

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for:
- "A shopkeeper uses 900g weight instead of 1000g"
- "A dealer cheats by giving less quantity"
- "A shopkeeper earns X% by using a false measure"

---

## 🧠 The Concept (Why This Works)

The dealer **claims** to sell a certain weight but actually gives **less**.

Example:
- Claims to sell 1000g at the price of 1000g
- But actually gives only 900g
- So he's getting paid for 1000g but only giving 900g

He made a "free" profit of (1000 − 900) = 100g!

---

## 📐 Key Formula

> **Profit % = (Error / True Value − Error) × 100**

OR equivalently:
> **Profit % = (True Weight − False Weight) / False Weight × 100**

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**❓ Question:** A shopkeeper uses a 900g weight instead of 1 kg (1000g). What is his profit percentage?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: He claims to sell 1000g but actually gives only 900g
- Find: Profit % earned due to false weight

**💡 What I'll use:** Profit % = (Error / True Value − Error) × 100 = (True − False) / False × 100

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Identify the error
Error = 1000 − 900 = 100g

Step 2: Apply the formula
Profit % = (100 / (1000 − 100)) × 100 = (100 / 900) × 100 = **11.11%**

**✅ Answer: 11.11%**

---

### Example 2

**❓ Question:** A dishonest dealer claims to sell goods at cost price but uses only an 800g weight instead of 1000g. What is his profit %?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: He charges for 1000g but gives only 800g, SP = CP per 1000g
- Find: Effective profit %

**💡 What I'll use:** Profit % = (True − False) / False × 100

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Identify the shortfall
Error = 1000 − 800 = 200g

Step 2: Apply the formula
Profit % = (200 / 800) × 100 = **25%**

**✅ Answer: 25%**

---

### Example 3

**❓ Question:** A dealer buys goods using a 900g weight instead of 1000g, and sells using an 1100g weight instead of 1000g. Find the total profit or loss %.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Buys at 900g but pays for 1000g; sells at 1100g but charges for 1000g
- Find: Net profit/loss % when both sides are cheated

**💡 What I'll use:** Work out CP and SP per 1000g of actual goods using true market price as reference

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Set true market price = ₹1 per gram

Step 2: Buying side — he pays for 1000g but gets 1000g of goods using a 900g weight
He pays ₹900 to acquire true 1000g of goods → CP per 1000g = ₹900

Step 3: Selling side — he charges for 1000g but gives 1100g
He collects ₹1000 for giving out 1100g → SP per 1000g = 1000 × (1000/1100) = ₹909.09

Step 4: Calculate profit %
Profit % = (909.09 − 900) / 900 × 100 ≈ **1%**

**✅ Answer: ~1% net profit (both cheats partially cancel each other)**

---

### Example 4

**❓ Question:** A shopkeeper buys goods at ₹80 per kg and sells them at ₹100 per kg, but uses a 900g weight instead of 1000g. Find the actual profit %.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Buys at ₹80/kg, charges ₹100/kg, but gives only 900g per "kg" sold
- Find: Actual profit % considering the false weight

**💡 What I'll use:** Find the actual CP and SP for the 900g he gives, then compute profit %

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find CP of the 900g he actually gives
CP of 900g = 80 × 900 / 1000 = ₹72

Step 2: Find SP — customer pays ₹100 thinking they got 1000g
SP = ₹100

Step 3: Calculate profit %
Profit = 100 − 72 = ₹28
Profit % = (28 / 72) × 100 = **38.9%**

**✅ Answer: 38.9%**

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

```
Profit% = (True − False) / False × 100
```

**Example:** 950g instead of 1000g:
→ (1000 − 950) / 950 × 100 = 50/950 × 100 = 5.26%
