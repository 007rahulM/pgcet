# Discount — Marked Price and Selling Price

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for:
- "Marked price is ₹X with Y% discount"
- "What is the discount given?"
- "What should be the marked price to get Z% profit after Y% discount?"

---

## 🧠 The Concept

Shops label items with a **Marked Price (MP)** — but often offer discounts.

> **SP = MP − Discount**

The discount is always a **% of MP**, NOT of CP!

---

## 📐 Key Formulas

| Find | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Discount | MP × D% / 100 |
| SP after discount | MP × (100 − D%) / 100 |
| MP (given SP and D%) | SP × 100 / (100 − D%) |
| D% | (Discount / MP) × 100 |

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**Problem:** MP = ₹800, Discount = 15%. Find SP.

- SP = 800 × (100−15)/100 = 800 × 85/100 = **₹680** ✅

---

### Example 2

**Problem:** SP = ₹720 after 10% discount. Find MP.

- MP = 720 × 100 / (100−10) = 720 × 100/90 = **₹800** ✅

---

### Example 3 (CP, profit, and discount together)

**Problem:** CP = ₹500. Trader marks 40% above CP. Then gives 10% discount. Find SP and actual profit%.

**Step 1 — MP:**
- MP = 500 × 140/100 = ₹700

**Step 2 — SP:**
- SP = 700 × 90/100 = **₹630**

**Step 3 — Profit %:**
- Profit = 630 − 500 = ₹130
- Profit % = (130/500) × 100 = **26%** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Find mark-up to get desired profit after discount)

**Problem:** CP = ₹400. Shopkeeper wants 20% profit after giving 20% discount. What should MP be?

- SP needed = 400 × 120/100 = ₹480
- SP = MP × 80/100 → 480 = MP × 0.8
- MP = 480/0.8 = **₹600** ✅

---

### Example 5 (Find discount % given CP and profit)

**Problem:** MP = ₹600, CP = ₹400. At what discount % will the profit be 25%?

**Step 1 — SP for 25% profit:**
- SP = 400 × 125/100 = ₹500

**Step 2 — Discount:**
- Discount = 600 − 500 = ₹100
- Discount % = (100/600) × 100 = **16.67%** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

**Key chain:** CP → MP (mark up) → SP (discount down)

```
MP = CP × (100 + markup%) / 100
SP = MP × (100 − discount%) / 100
Profit% = (SP/CP − 1) × 100
```

**Combined formula:**
> Net result = (100 + markup%) × (100 − discount%) / 10000 − 1

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. MP = ₹1000, Discount 25%. Find SP.

2. SP = ₹540 after 10% discount. Find MP.

3. CP = ₹300. Marked 50% above CP. 20% discount given. Find profit %.

4. A trader wants 25% profit on an item costing ₹400. He gives 20% discount. What should MP be?

5. MP = ₹500, SP = ₹425. Find discount %.

6. CP = ₹250. MP = ₹300. Shopkeeper gives a 10% discount on MP. Find actual profit %.

7. Two items with MP ₹500 each. 10% discount on first, 20% on second. Total SP?

8. A shopkeeper marks price 30% above CP and gives 10% discount. Find profit %.

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. 1000 × 0.75 = **₹750**
2. 540 × 100/90 = **₹600**
3. MP = 450. SP = 450 × 0.8 = 360. Profit = 60. P% = 60/300 × 100 = **20%**
4. SP = 500. MP = 500/0.8 = **₹625**
5. Discount = 75. D% = 75/500 × 100 = **15%**
6. SP = 300 × 0.9 = ₹270 (10% discount on MP of ₹300). Profit = 270 − 250 = ₹20. P% = 20/250 × 100 = **8%**
7. 500×0.9 + 500×0.8 = 450+400 = **₹850**
8. Net = 130 × 90 / 100 = 117. Profit = 17%. Or: markup 30%, discount 10% → Net = 1.3 × 0.9 = 1.17 → **17% profit**
