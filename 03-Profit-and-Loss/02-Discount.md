# Discount — Marked Price and Selling Price

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for:
- "Marked price is ₹X with Y% discount"
- "What is the discount given?"
- "What should be the marked price to get Z% profit after Y% discount?"

---

## 🧠 The Concept

Shops label items with a **Marked Price (MP)** — but often offer discounts.

> **SP = MP − Discount**

The discount is always a **% of MP**, NOT of CP!

---

## 📐 Key Formulas

| Find | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Discount | MP × D% / 100 |
| SP after discount | MP × (100 − D%) / 100 |
| MP (given SP and D%) | SP × 100 / (100 − D%) |
| D% | (Discount / MP) × 100 |

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**❓ Question:** MP = ₹800, Discount = 15%. Find the selling price.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Marked Price = ₹800, Discount = 15%
- Find: SP after discount

**💡 What I'll use:** SP = MP × (100 − D%) / 100

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Apply the discount formula
SP = 800 × (100 − 15) / 100 = 800 × 85 / 100 = **₹680**

**✅ Answer: ₹680**

---

### Example 2

**❓ Question:** An item is sold for ₹720 after a 10% discount. Find the marked price.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: SP = ₹720, Discount % = 10%
- Find: MP (original price before discount)

**💡 What I'll use:** MP = SP × 100 / (100 − D%)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Work backwards from SP
MP = 720 × 100 / (100 − 10) = 720 × 100 / 90 = **₹800**

**✅ Answer: ₹800**

---

### Example 3

**❓ Question:** CP = ₹500. A trader marks the price 40% above CP, then gives a 10% discount. Find the selling price and the actual profit %.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: CP = ₹500, mark-up = 40%, discount = 10%
- Find: Final SP and actual profit %

**💡 What I'll use:** MP = CP × (100 + markup%) / 100, then SP = MP × (100 − D%) / 100

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find the marked price
MP = 500 × 140 / 100 = ₹700

Step 2: Apply the discount to get SP
SP = 700 × 90 / 100 = ₹630

Step 3: Calculate actual profit %
Profit = 630 − 500 = ₹130
Profit % = (130 / 500) × 100 = **26%**

**✅ Answer: SP = ₹630, Profit = 26%**

---

### Example 4

**❓ Question:** CP = ₹400. A shopkeeper wants 20% profit after giving a 20% discount. What should the marked price be?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: CP = ₹400, desired profit = 20%, discount to be given = 20%
- Find: MP to set so that after discount, the required profit is still earned

**💡 What I'll use:** First find the required SP from CP, then work backwards to get MP

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find the SP needed for 20% profit
SP = 400 × 120 / 100 = ₹480

Step 2: Work backwards — if 20% discount brings SP to ₹480, what is MP?
SP = MP × 80 / 100 → 480 = MP × 0.8
MP = 480 / 0.8 = **₹600**

**✅ Answer: ₹600**

---

### Example 5

**❓ Question:** MP = ₹600, CP = ₹400. At what discount % will the profit be exactly 25%?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: MP = ₹600, CP = ₹400, desired profit = 25%
- Find: Discount % that still gives 25% profit

**💡 What I'll use:** Find the required SP first, then compute discount from MP

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find SP for 25% profit on CP
SP = 400 × 125 / 100 = ₹500

Step 2: Calculate discount on MP
Discount = MP − SP = 600 − 500 = ₹100
Discount % = (100 / 600) × 100 = **16.67%**

**✅ Answer: 16.67%**

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

**Key chain:** CP → MP (mark up) → SP (discount down)

```
MP = CP × (100 + markup%) / 100
SP = MP × (100 − discount%) / 100
Profit% = (SP/CP − 1) × 100
```

**Combined formula:**
> Net result = (100 + markup%) × (100 − discount%) / 10000 − 1
