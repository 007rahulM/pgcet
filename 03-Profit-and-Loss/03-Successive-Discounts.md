# Successive Discounts — Two or More Discounts Applied One After Another

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for:
- "Two discounts of D1% and D2% given successively"
- "First 20% discount, then another 10% discount"
- "What is the net/equivalent single discount?"

---

## 🧠 The Concept (Why Not Simply Add Discounts)

**Wrong:** 20% + 10% = 30% discount ← **INCORRECT!**

**Why?** The second discount is applied on the **already reduced price**, not the original.

---

## 📐 Key Formula

### Net Single Discount (for two discounts D1 and D2):
> **Net discount = D1 + D2 − (D1 × D2) / 100**

### SP after two successive discounts:
> SP = MP × (100 − D1)/100 × (100 − D2)/100

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**❓ Question:** MP = ₹1000. Two successive discounts of 20% and 10% are given. Find the final SP and the equivalent net single discount.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: MP = ₹1000, two discounts: 20% then 10%
- Find: Final SP and equivalent single discount %

**💡 What I'll use:** Apply discounts step-by-step; also verify using Net discount = D1 + D2 − (D1 × D2) / 100

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Apply first discount
After 20%: SP1 = 1000 × 80 / 100 = ₹800

Step 2: Apply second discount on reduced price
After 10%: SP2 = 800 × 90 / 100 = ₹720

Step 3: Find equivalent net single discount
Net discount = 20 + 10 − (20 × 10) / 100 = 30 − 2 = 28%
Check: 1000 × 72 / 100 = ₹720 ✓

**✅ Answer: SP = ₹720, Net discount = 28%**

---

### Example 2

**❓ Question:** Two discounts of 25% and 20% are available. Does the order matter — is 25% then 20% better than 20% then 25%?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Two discounts of 25% and 20%
- Find: Whether the order of applying discounts changes the final price

**💡 What I'll use:** Net discount formula — it's symmetric, so order doesn't matter

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Calculate net discount using the formula
Net = 25 + 20 − (25 × 20) / 100 = 45 − 5 = 40%

Step 2: The formula is the same regardless of which discount comes first
The result is 40% net discount in both cases — order makes no difference.

**✅ Answer: Both orders give the same 40% net discount**

---

### Example 3

**❓ Question:** MP = ₹2000. Three successive discounts of 10%, 20%, and 25% are applied. Find the final SP.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: MP = ₹2000, three discounts: 10%, then 20%, then 25%
- Find: Final SP after all three discounts

**💡 What I'll use:** SP = MP × (100−D1)/100 × (100−D2)/100 × (100−D3)/100

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: After 10% discount
2000 × 0.9 = ₹1800

Step 2: After 20% discount
1800 × 0.8 = ₹1440

Step 3: After 25% discount
1440 × 0.75 = **₹1080**

**✅ Answer: ₹1080**

---

### Example 4

**❓ Question:** A single discount of 28% is equivalent to two equal successive discounts of D% each. Find D.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Net single discount = 28%, and it equals two identical successive discounts D% and D%
- Find: D

**💡 What I'll use:** Net discount = D + D − D²/100 = 28, then solve for D

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Set up the equation
2D − D²/100 = 28

Step 2: Test values to find D
Try D = 15: 30 − 225/100 = 30 − 2.25 = 27.75 (close)
Try D = 16: 32 − 256/100 = 32 − 2.56 = 29.44 (too high)

Step 3: D ≈ 15.5% (in MCQs, the options will point to an exact value)
For D = 15.5: 31 − 240.25/100 = 31 − 2.40 = **28.6% ≈ 28%**

**✅ Answer: D ≈ 15.5%**

---

### Example 5

**❓ Question:** After two successive discounts of 10% and 20%, an item is sold for ₹720. Find the marked price.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: SP = ₹720 after successive discounts of 10% and 20%
- Find: Original MP before any discounts

**💡 What I'll use:** First find net discount, then work backwards: MP = SP × 100 / (100 − Net%)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find the net equivalent discount
Net = 10 + 20 − (10 × 20) / 100 = 30 − 2 = 28%

Step 2: Work backwards from SP to MP
MP × (100 − 28) / 100 = 720
MP × 72 / 100 = 720
MP = 720 × 100 / 72 = **₹1000**

**✅ Answer: ₹1000**

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

For two successive discounts D1% and D2%:
> **Net = D1 + D2 − (D1 × D2)/100**

For SP directly:
> **SP = MP × (100−D1)/100 × (100−D2)/100**

**Quick Mental Check:** Net discount is ALWAYS less than D1 + D2.
