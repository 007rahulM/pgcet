# Profit & Loss: Dishonest Dealer — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** A dealer uses 800g instead of 1kg. Profit %?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: He claims to sell 1000g but actually gives only 800g; sells at CP (no price markup)
- Find: Profit % earned through false weight

**💡 What I'll use:** Profit % = (True Weight − False Weight) / False Weight × 100

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Identify the shortfall
Error = 1000 − 800 = 200g

Step 2: Apply the formula (false weight = 800g)
Profit % = (200 / 800) × 100 = **25%**

**✅ Answer: 25%**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** A vendor uses 950g instead of 1kg. He also sells at 10% profit over CP. Total profit %?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: False weight = 950g (claims 1000g), plus charges 10% above CP
- Find: Combined total profit %

**💡 What I'll use:** Weight gain factor × price profit factor − 1

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Gain from using false weight
He gets paid for 1000g but gives only 950g → factor = 1000/950

Step 2: Gain from pricing
He also charges 10% above CP → factor = 1.10

Step 3: Combined profit
Total factor = 1.10 × (1000/950) = 1.10 × 1.05263 ≈ 1.1579

Profit % = (1.1579 − 1) × 100 ≈ **15.79%**

**✅ Answer: ≈ 15.79% profit**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** A milkman mixes 20% water in milk and sells at CP. Profit %?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: For every 100L sold, 80L is milk (paid for) and 20L is free water; SP = CP of milk per litre
- Find: Profit % from adulteration

**💡 What I'll use:** Treat water as zero-cost additive; profit comes from selling water at milk price

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: For 100L of mixture sold, cost is only of 80L of milk
Cost = 80 units (milk price)
Revenue = 100 units (selling all at milk price)

Step 2: Calculate profit
Profit = 100 − 80 = 20 units
Profit % = (20 / 80) × 100 = **25%**

**✅ Answer: 25% profit**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** A shopkeeper sells at a 5% loss but uses 800g instead of 1000g. Net profit/loss %?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Nominal loss = 5% (prices are set 5% below CP for 1000g), but only 800g is given
- Find: Whether there is a net profit or loss, and the percentage

**💡 What I'll use:** Compare actual revenue to actual cost of goods given (800g)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Let CP of 1000g = ₹100
SP (at 5% loss) = ₹95 — this is what the customer pays per claimed 1000g

Step 2: Actual CP of the 800g he hands over
CP of 800g = (800/1000) × 100 = ₹80

Step 3: Calculate profit/loss
Profit = 95 − 80 = ₹15
Profit % = (15 / 80) × 100 = **18.75%**

**✅ Answer: 18.75% profit**

### Q7

**❓ Question:** A dealer buys 10 items for ₹100 and sells 9 items for ₹100. Profit %?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Buys 10 items for ₹100 (CP = ₹10 each), sells 9 items for ₹100 (SP = ₹100/9 each)
- Find: Overall profit %

**💡 What I'll use:** Find total CP and total SP for the same 10 items

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Cost of all 10 items = ₹100

Step 2: He sells 9 for ₹100; at that rate, SP of all 10 items = 100 × (10/9) = ₹111.11

Step 3: Profit %
Profit % = (111.11 − 100) / 100 × 100 = **11.11%**

**✅ Answer: 11.11% profit**

### Q8

**❓ Question:** A dishonest trader uses false weight and gets 11.11% profit. What weight does he use for 1 kg?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Profit % = 11.11% = 1/9
- Find: False weight used per claimed 1 kg (1000g)

**💡 What I'll use:** Profit % = Error / False Weight × 100; solve for false weight

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Let false weight = x grams
Error = 1000 − x

Step 2: Set up the equation
(1000 − x) / x × 100 = 11.11 = 100/9

Step 3: Solve
(1000 − x) / x = 1/9
9(1000 − x) = x
9000 − 9x = x
10x = 9000
x = **900g**

**✅ Answer: He uses 900g for 1 kg**
