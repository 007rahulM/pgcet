# Averages — Changed Average (Joining, Leaving, Replacement)

## 🔍 When This Comes Up

- "New member joins — average changes"
- "Replacing a member — average changes"
- "Average changes when a value is added or removed"

---

## 📐 Key Concepts

### When Member Joins:
> New member's value = New average × New count − Old average × Old count

### When Member Leaves:
> Removed value = Old average × Old count − New average × New count

### When Member is Replaced:
> New member = Old member ± (change in average × total count)
> OR: New member = Old member + (new avg − old avg) × count

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (New Member Joins)

**Problem:** Average age of 4 people = 25. A 5th person joins, average becomes 26. Age of 5th person?

- Original sum = 25 × 4 = 100
- New sum = 26 × 5 = 130
- 5th person's age = 130 − 100 = **30** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Member Leaves)

**Problem:** Average of a group of 10 is 20. One person leaves. New average = 19. Age of person who left?

- Original sum = 20 × 10 = 200
- New sum = 19 × 9 = 171
- Person who left = 200 − 171 = **29** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Replacement)

**Problem:** Average weight of 5 people = 60 kg. One person of 80 kg is replaced. Average becomes 58. New person's weight?

- Original sum = 300
- New sum = 58 × 5 = 290
- Change = 300 − 290 = 10 (sum decreased by 10)
- New person = 80 − 10 = **70 kg** ✅

**Quick Method:**
> New member = Outgoing member + (change in avg × n)
> = 80 + (−2 × 5) = 80 − 10 = **70 kg** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Teacher Joins Class)

**Problem:** Average age of class = 20. Teacher (age 40) joins. New average = 21. How many students in class?

- Old sum = 20n. New sum = 21(n+1)
- 20n + 40 = 21n + 21
- n = **19 students** ✅

---

### Example 5 (New Student Joins)

**Problem:** Average weight of 10 students = 50 kg. A new student joins, average becomes 51 kg. What is the new student's weight?

- Old sum = 500
- New sum = 51 × 11 = 561
- New student's weight = 561 − 500 = **61 kg** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcuts

| Situation | Quick Formula |
|-----------|--------------|
| New member added | New member = new avg + (new avg − old avg) × old count |
| Member removed | Old member = old avg + (old avg − new avg) × new count |
| Replacement | New = Old ± (change in avg × n) |

---

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. Average of 5 numbers is 30. If one number (50) is removed, new average?

2. Average age of 8 students = 16. If teacher (age 40) is included, new average?

3. Average weight of 20 persons = 60 kg. If one person of 80 kg is replaced by someone, average drops to 59. New person's weight?

4. Average score of a cricket team of 11 = 40. If the captain's score (80) is excluded, new average?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. Old sum = 150. New sum = 150 − 50 = 100. n = 4. New avg = 100/4 = **25**
2. Old sum = 128. New sum = 128 + 40 = 168. n = 9. New avg = 168/9 ≈ **18.67**
3. Old sum = 1200. New sum = 59 × 20 = 1180. Removed 80, new person = 80 − (1200 − 1180) = 80 − 20 = **60 kg**
4. Old sum = 440. New sum = 440 − 80 = 360. n = 10. New avg = 360/10 = **36**
