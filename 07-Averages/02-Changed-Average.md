# Averages — Changed Average (Joining, Leaving, Replacement)

## 🔍 When This Comes Up

- "New member joins — average changes"
- "Replacing a member — average changes"
- "Average changes when a value is added or removed"

---

## 📐 Key Concepts

### When Member Joins:
> New member's value = New average × New count − Old average × Old count

### When Member Leaves:
> Removed value = Old average × Old count − New average × New count

### When Member is Replaced:
> New member = Old member ± (change in average × total count)
> OR: New member = Old member + (new avg − old avg) × count

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**❓ Question:** Average age of 4 people = 25. A 5th person joins, average becomes 26. What is the age of the 5th person?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 4 people with average age 25; after a 5th person joins, average becomes 26
- Find: Age of the 5th person

**💡 What I'll use:** New member's value = New avg × New count − Old avg × Old count

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find original total
Original sum = 25 × 4 = 100

Step 2: Find new total
New sum = 26 × 5 = 130

Step 3: Subtract to find 5th person's age
5th person's age = 130 − 100 = **30**

**✅ Answer: 5th person's age = 30**

---

### Example 2

**❓ Question:** Average of a group of 10 is 20. One person leaves. New average = 19. What is the age of the person who left?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Group of 10 with average 20; one person leaves, new average drops to 19 for 9 people
- Find: The value of the person who left

**💡 What I'll use:** Removed value = Old avg × Old count − New avg × New count

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find original total
Original sum = 20 × 10 = 200

Step 2: Find new total (after leaving)
New sum = 19 × 9 = 171

Step 3: Difference = the person who left
Person who left = 200 − 171 = **29**

**✅ Answer: The person who left had a value of 29**

---

### Example 3

**❓ Question:** Average weight of 5 people = 60 kg. One person of 80 kg is replaced. Average becomes 58 kg. Find the new person's weight.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 5 people, avg weight 60 kg; person weighing 80 kg is replaced; new avg = 58 kg
- Find: Weight of the new person who replaced them

**💡 What I'll use:** New member = Outgoing member + (change in avg × n)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find original and new totals
Original sum = 60 × 5 = 300
New sum = 58 × 5 = 290

Step 2: Sum decreased by 300 − 290 = 10

Step 3: Find new person's weight
New person = 80 − 10 = **70 kg**

Quick check: New member = 80 + (58 − 60) × 5 = 80 − 10 = **70 kg** ✓

**✅ Answer: New person weighs 70 kg**

---

### Example 4

**❓ Question:** Average age of a class = 20. Teacher (age 40) joins. New average = 21. How many students are in the class?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Class average age = 20; teacher aged 40 joins; new average becomes 21
- Find: Number of students (n) in the class

**💡 What I'll use:** Set up an equation using old sum + new value = new avg × new count

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Express sums algebraically
Old sum = 20n
New sum with teacher = 20n + 40

Step 2: Set equal to new average × new count
20n + 40 = 21 × (n + 1) = 21n + 21

Step 3: Solve for n
40 − 21 = 21n − 20n
19 = n
n = **19 students**

**✅ Answer: 19 students in the class**

---

### Example 5

**❓ Question:** Average weight of 10 students = 50 kg. A new student joins, average becomes 51 kg. What is the new student's weight?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 10 students with average 50 kg; after a new student joins, average becomes 51 kg for 11 students
- Find: The new student's weight

**💡 What I'll use:** New member's weight = New avg × New count − Old avg × Old count

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find original total
Old sum = 50 × 10 = 500

Step 2: Find new total
New sum = 51 × 11 = 561

Step 3: New student's weight = difference
New student's weight = 561 − 500 = **61 kg**

**✅ Answer: New student's weight = 61 kg**

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcuts

| Situation | Quick Formula |
|-----------|--------------|
| New member added | New member = new avg + (new avg − old avg) × old count |
| Member removed | Old member = old avg + (old avg − new avg) × new count |
| Replacement | New = Old ± (change in avg × n) |
