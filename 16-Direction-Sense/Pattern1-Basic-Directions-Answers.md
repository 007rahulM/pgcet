# Basic Directions — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** A person walks 3 km North, then 4 km East. What is the shortest distance from start?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 3 km North, then 4 km East
- Find: Straight-line (shortest) distance from the starting point

**💡 What I'll use:** Pythagorean theorem — movements are perpendicular

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Draw the right triangle
- Vertical leg (North) = 3 km
- Horizontal leg (East) = 4 km

Step 2: Apply Pythagoras
- Distance = √(3² + 4²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5 km

**✅ Answer: 5 km**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** Starting from home, A walks 6 km North, turns right, walks 4 km, turns right, walks 6 km. Distance from home?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 6 km North → turn right (East) → 4 km → turn right (South) → 6 km
- Find: Straight-line distance from starting point

**💡 What I'll use:** Coordinate tracking and cancellation of opposite movements

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Track each movement as coordinates (start at origin)
- 6 km North: position = (0, 6)
- Turn right → now facing East; 4 km East: position = (4, 6)
- Turn right → now facing South; 6 km South: position = (4, 0)

Step 2: Check cancellations
- 6 km North and 6 km South cancel out completely (net Y = 0)
- 4 km East remains

Step 3: Final position is (4, 0) = 4 km East of home
- Distance = 4 km

**✅ Answer: 4 km**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** A faces South, turns right, walks 3 km, turns right, walks 4 km, turns left. Which direction is A facing?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Start facing South, series of turns
- Find: Final facing direction (not position)

**💡 What I'll use:** Turn direction table — Right turn = 90° clockwise, Left turn = 90° counterclockwise

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Start facing South

Step 2: Turn right (clockwise 90°)
- South + right = West → now facing West

Step 3: Walk 3 km West (direction unchanged)

Step 4: Turn right again (clockwise 90°)
- West + right = North → now facing North

Step 5: Walk 4 km North (direction unchanged)

Step 6: Turn left (counterclockwise 90°)
- North + left = West → now facing West

**✅ Answer: West**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** A walks 7 km West, 3 km North, 7 km East. How far from start and in which direction?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 7 km West, then 3 km North, then 7 km East
- Find: Net distance and direction from start

**💡 What I'll use:** Coordinate tracking and cancellation

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Track each movement
- 7 km West: position = (−7, 0)
- 3 km North: position = (−7, 3)
- 7 km East: position = (−7+7, 3) = (0, 3)

Step 2: 7 km West and 7 km East cancel completely (net X = 0)

Step 3: Final position = (0, 3) = 3 km directly North of start
- Distance = 3 km

**✅ Answer: 3 km North**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** A goes 8 km East, 6 km South. Shortest distance from start?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 8 km East, then 6 km South
- Find: Straight-line distance from start

**💡 What I'll use:** Pythagorean theorem

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Identify the right triangle
- Horizontal leg (East) = 8 km
- Vertical leg (South) = 6 km

Step 2: Apply Pythagoras

### Q6

**❓ Question:** From point A: go North 5 km to reach B, then go East 12 km to reach C. Shortest distance from A to C?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A → B (5 km North) → C (12 km East)
- Find: Direct distance A to C

**💡 What I'll use:** Pythagorean theorem — AB and BC are perpendicular legs

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Set up the right triangle
- AB = 5 km (vertical leg)
- BC = 12 km (horizontal leg)
- AC = hypotenuse

Step 2: Apply Pythagoras
- AC = √(5² + 12²) = √(25 + 144) = √169 = 13 km

**✅ Answer: 13 km**

### Q7

**❓ Question:** A man faces North, turns right 90°, turns right again 90°, then turns left 270°. Which direction is he facing?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Start facing North, three successive turns
- Find: Final facing direction

**💡 What I'll use:** Step-by-step rotation tracking; note that left 270° = right 90°

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Start facing North

Step 2: Turn right 90° (clockwise)
- North → East

Step 3: Turn right 90° again
- East → South

Step 4: Turn left 270° (counterclockwise 270°)
- 270° counterclockwise = same as 90° clockwise
- South + 90° clockwise = West

**✅ Answer: West**

### Q8

**❓ Question:** A walks 10m North, 10m East, 10m South, 10m West. Where is he relative to start?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Four equal movements in all four cardinal directions
- Find: Final position relative to starting point

**💡 What I'll use:** Coordinate tracking — opposite movements cancel

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Track each movement
- 10m North: (0, 10)
- 10m East: (10, 10)
- 10m South: (10, 0)
- 10m West: (0, 0)

Step 2: 10m North cancels 10m South (net Y = 0)
Step 3: 10m East cancels 10m West (net X = 0)

Step 4: Final position = (0, 0) = back at start

**✅ Answer: Back at the starting point — distance = 0**
