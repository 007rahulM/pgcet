# Advanced Directions — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** A is 5 km North of B. C is 5 km East of B. In which direction is A from C?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A is 5 km North of B; C is 5 km East of B
- Find: Direction of A as seen from C

**💡 What I'll use:** Coordinate system — place B at origin, find positions of A and C, then determine direction

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Place B at origin (0, 0)
- A is 5 km North of B → A = (0, 5)
- C is 5 km East of B → C = (5, 0)

Step 2: Find A's position relative to C
- Δx = A.x − C.x = 0 − 5 = −5 (A is to the West of C)
- Δy = A.y − C.y = 5 − 0 = +5 (A is to the North of C)

Step 3: Determine direction from quadrant
- Negative X (West) + Positive Y (North) = **Northwest**

**✅ Answer: Northwest**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** Starting at origin facing North: go 4m forward, turn left 90°, go 3m. What is the distance and direction from start?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Start at (0,0) facing North; 4m North, turn left, 3m West
- Find: Straight-line distance from start and the direction

**💡 What I'll use:** Coordinate tracking + Pythagorean theorem

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Track movements
- 4m North: position = (0, 4)
- Turn left (counterclockwise 90°) from North → now facing West
- 3m West: position = (−3, 4)

Step 2: Calculate distance from origin (0, 0) to (−3, 4)
- Distance = √(3² + 4²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = **5m**

Step 3: Determine direction
- Δx = −3 (West), Δy = +4 (North) → **Northwest** direction

**✅ Answer: 5m, Northwest**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** A man faces West. He turns 90° clockwise, then 180° counterclockwise. Which direction is he facing?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Start facing West, two successive turns
- Find: Final facing direction

**💡 What I'll use:** Step-by-step rotation: clockwise = right turn, counterclockwise = left turn

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Start facing West

Step 2: Turn 90° clockwise (right turn)
- West → North

Step 3: Turn 180° counterclockwise
- North + 180° (either direction) = South

**✅ Answer: South**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** X is 8 km East of Y. Z is 6 km North of X. How far is Z from Y?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Y to X is 8 km East; X to Z is 6 km North
- Find: Straight-line distance from Y to Z

**💡 What I'll use:** Coordinate system + Pythagorean theorem

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Place Y at origin (0, 0)
- X is 8 km East of Y → X = (8, 0)
- Z is 6 km North of X → Z = (8, 6)

Step 2: Calculate distance Y to Z
- Δx = 8, Δy = 6
- Distance = √(8² + 6²) = √(64 + 36) = √100 = **10 km**

**✅ Answer: 10 km**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** Sunita starts at home, goes 4 km East, turns North and goes 3 km. What is the shortest distance from home?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 4 km East, then 3 km North
- Find: Straight-line (shortest) distance back to home

**💡 What I'll use:** Pythagorean theorem — movements are perpendicular

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Track position (start at origin)
- 4 km East: (4, 0)
- 3 km North: (4, 3)

Step 2: Apply Pythagoras
- Distance = √(4² + 3²) = √(16 + 9) = √25 = **5 km**

**✅ Answer: 5 km**

### Q6

**❓ Question:** A man is facing Northwest. He turns 90° clockwise. Which direction is he facing?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Facing Northwest, turns 90° clockwise (right)
- Find: New facing direction

**💡 What I'll use:** Compass rotation — 90° clockwise from any intercardinal direction

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Identify Northwest on the compass (between North and West, at 315°)

Step 2: Turn 90° clockwise (add 90° to compass bearing)
- Northwest (315°) + 90° = 405° = 45° = **Northeast**

Step 3: Verify with direction cycle
- N → NE → E → SE → S → SW → W → NW → N
- One step clockwise from NW = **Northeast**

**✅ Answer: Northeast**
