# Blood Relations — Complete Guide

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

- "Pointing to a photo, A says..."
- "B is the son of C's father's brother..."
- "If D is the sister of E who is the son of F..."

---

## 🧠 The Strategy

**ALWAYS draw a family tree!** Never try to solve in your head.

### Symbols to Use:
```
Male:   [Name]
Female: (Name) or Name♀
Marriage:  ═══
Parent-Child:  │
Sibling:  ───
```

---

## 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Key Relationships (Memorize!)

| Relationship | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| Father's or mother's father | Grandfather |
| Father's or mother's mother | Grandmother |
| Father's brother | Uncle (Paternal) |
| Mother's brother | Uncle (Maternal) |
| Father's sister | Aunt (Paternal) |
| Mother's sister | Aunt (Maternal) |
| Uncle's / Aunt's son or daughter | Cousin |
| Husband's or wife's father | Father-in-law |
| Husband's or wife's mother | Mother-in-law |
| Husband's or wife's brother | Brother-in-law |
| Husband's or wife's sister | Sister-in-law |
| Son's wife | Daughter-in-law |
| Daughter's husband | Son-in-law |

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Pointing to photo)

**Problem:** "Pointing to a man in a photograph, a woman says, 'His mother is the only daughter of my father.' How is the woman related to the man?"

**Step 1:** "Only daughter of my father" = the woman herself

**Step 2:** "His mother" = the woman

**Answer:** The woman is the man's **Mother** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Chain)

**Problem:** A is B's father. C is A's sister. D is C's mother. How is D related to B?

**Draw tree:**
```
    D (female)
    │
    ├── A (male)──── C (female, A's sister)
    │
    B (A's child)
```

- D is A's mother, A is B's father
- D is **B's Grandmother** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Coded relationships)

**Problem:** If P $ Q means P is the father of Q, P @ Q means P is the mother of Q, P # Q means P is the sister of Q. Then what does A @ B $ C # D mean?

- A @ B → A is mother of B
- B $ C → B is father of C
- C # D → C is sister of D

So: A is grandmother of C (and D). D is C's sister.

**A is D's grandmother** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Typical KEA format)

**Problem:** Introducing Renu, Suresh says, "She is the only daughter of my father's only son." How is Renu related to Suresh?

- Suresh's father's only son = Suresh himself
- Renu is the only daughter of Suresh
- **Renu is Suresh's daughter** ✅

---

### Example 5

**Problem:** A said to B: "Your mother's husband's sister is my aunt." How is A related to B?

- B's mother's husband = B's father
- B's father's sister = B's aunt
- That aunt is also A's aunt
- So A and B share the same aunt → A is **B's sibling (brother or sister)** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

**Steps for any blood relation problem:**
1. Identify the key person being pointed/referred to
2. Start from a known person and trace step by step
3. Draw the tree (takes 10 seconds but saves mistakes)
4. Find the relationship from the tree

**Common tricks:**
- "Only son/daughter of X" = X himself/herself
- "My mother's only son" = me

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. A is B's brother. C is A's mother. D is C's father. E is D's mother. How is A related to E?

2. Pointing to a lady, a man says "She is the daughter of the woman who is the mother of the husband of my mother." Who is she?

3. X's mother is Y's sister. Z is Y's son. How is X related to Z?

4. A's father is B's son. C is B's father. What is A's relationship to C?

5. P is Q's sister. R is Q's mother. S is R's father. T is S's wife. How is P related to T?

6. Pointing to a photo, a man says "She is the daughter of my grandfather's only son." Who is she?

7. If Ram says "Sita is my father's wife's daughter's son's grandmother," who is Sita to Ram?

8. A is B's father. B is C's brother. D is C's mother. How is A related to D?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. E is A's great-grandmother
2. She is the man's aunt (mother's sister)
3. X is Z's cousin (or maternal aunt's child)
4. A is C's great-grandchild (or C is A's great-grandfather)
5. P is T's granddaughter
6. She is his sister
7. Sita is Ram's mother
8. A is D's husband
