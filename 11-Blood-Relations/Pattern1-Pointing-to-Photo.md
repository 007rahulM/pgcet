# Pattern 1: Pointing to a Photo / Photograph Type

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

The question says:
- "Pointing to a man in a photograph, a woman says..."
- "Introducing a person, X says, 'He/She is...'"
- "Looking at a portrait, A said..."

---

## 🧠 The Strategy

**ALWAYS draw a family tree!** Never try to solve in your head.

### Symbols to Use:
```
Male:   [Name]
Female: (Name) or Name♀
Marriage:  ═══
Parent-Child:  │
Sibling:  ───
```

---

## 🔑 Key Trick for Photo-Type Problems

> **"Only son/daughter of X" = X himself/herself**
> **"My mother's only son" = me**

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Basic Pointing)

**Problem:** "Pointing to a man in a photograph, a woman says, 'His mother is the only daughter of my father.' How is the woman related to the man?"

**Step 1:** "Only daughter of my father" = the woman herself

**Step 2:** "His mother" = the woman

**Answer:** The woman is the man's **Mother** ✅

---

### Example 2

**Problem:** Introducing Renu, Suresh says, "She is the only daughter of my father's only son." How is Renu related to Suresh?

**Step 1:** Suresh's father's only son = Suresh himself

**Step 2:** Renu is the only daughter of Suresh

**Answer:** Renu is Suresh's **Daughter** ✅

---

### Example 3

**Problem:** "Pointing to a photo, a man says, 'She is the daughter of my grandfather's only son.' Who is she?"

**Step 1:** His grandfather's only son = his father

**Step 2:** She is the daughter of his father

**Answer:** She is his **Sister** ✅

---

### Example 4

**Problem:** A man says, "The father of the father of my only son is my grandfather." Is this correct?

- His only son's father = himself
- His father's father = his grandfather ✅

**Answer:** Yes, the statement is correct ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

**Steps for photo-type problems:**
1. Identify **who is speaking** (the narrator)
2. Parse the relationship chain **step by step** from the person being pointed to
3. Work **backward** to the narrator
4. Draw the tree if more than 2 steps

**Golden Phrases:**
- "Only son/daughter of X" → **X himself/herself**
- "My mother's only son" → **me**
- "My father's only daughter" → **me (if I'm female)**

---

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. Pointing to a lady, a man says, "She is the daughter of the woman who is the mother of the husband of my mother." Who is she to the man?

2. A man says, "That boy's mother is the only daughter of my mother." How is the man related to the boy?

3. Pointing to a photograph, a woman says, "He is the son of my father's only son." Who is the person in the photograph to the woman?

4. Introducing a lady, a man says, "She is the wife of the only son of my grandfather." Who is the lady to the man?

5. A says, "The mother of B is the only daughter of my mother." How is A related to B?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. She is the man's **Aunt** (mother's sister) — traced: his mother's husband's mother = his grandmother; grandmother's daughter = his mother's sister (aunt)
2. The man is the boy's **Father** or maternal uncle — "only daughter of my mother" = me (if female) or his sister; her son = the boy → man is the boy's **Maternal Uncle**
3. She is the **Son** of the woman → the person is her **Son** ✅
4. She is his **Mother** (wife of the only son of his grandfather = wife of his father)
5. A is the **Maternal Uncle** of B (A's mother's only daughter = A's sister; B's mother = A's sister → A is B's maternal uncle)
