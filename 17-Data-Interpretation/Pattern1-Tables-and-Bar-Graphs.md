# Pattern 1: Tables and Bar Graphs

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

- Data presented in rows and columns (table)
- Vertical or horizontal bars representing values (bar graph)
- "Find the % change from year X to year Y"
- "In which period was the value highest?"

---

## 🧠 General Strategy

1. **Read the title** — understand what data is about
2. **Note the units** — thousands, lakhs, %, etc.
3. **Read the question first**, then find the data (don't read everything blindly)
4. Use **approximation** for complex calculations

---

## 📐 Key Formulas

| Question Type | Formula |
|--------------|---------|
| % change | (New − Old)/Old × 100 |
| % of total | (Part/Total) × 100 |
| Ratio | Divide two values |
| Average | Sum/Count |

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Table — % change)

| Year | Sales (₹ lakhs) |
|------|----------------|
| 2020 | 150 |
| 2021 | 180 |
| 2022 | 162 |
| 2023 | 198 |

**Q1: What is the % change in sales from 2021 to 2022?**

- Change = 162 − 180 = −18 (decrease)
- % change = (18/180) × 100 = **10% decrease** ✅

**Q2: In which year was the % increase highest?**

- 2020→2021: (180−150)/150 × 100 = 30/150 × 100 = **20%**
- 2021→2022: decrease, so skip
- 2022→2023: (198−162)/162 × 100 = 36/162 × 100 ≈ **22.2%**
- Highest % increase: **2022 to 2023** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Table — multiple products)

| Product | Q1 Sales | Q2 Sales | Q3 Sales | Q4 Sales |
|---------|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| A | 200 | 250 | 220 | 280 |
| B | 150 | 180 | 160 | 200 |
| C | 300 | 280 | 320 | 350 |

**Q1: Total sales of Product A in all 4 quarters?**
- 200+250+220+280 = **950 units** ✅

**Q2: In which quarter was Product C's sales highest?**
- Q4 (350) → **Q4** ✅

**Q3: What % of total Q1 sales is Product B's Q1 sales?**
- Total Q1 = 200+150+300 = 650
- B's share = (150/650) × 100 ≈ **23.08%** ✅

**Q4: Average quarterly sales of Product A?**
- 950/4 = **237.5 units** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Bar Graph reading)

**Given:** A bar graph shows monthly expenses of a family:
- Jan: ₹8000, Feb: ₹7500, Mar: ₹9000, Apr: ₹8500

**Q1: Average monthly expense for these 4 months?**
- Sum = 8000+7500+9000+8500 = 33000
- Average = 33000/4 = **₹8250** ✅

**Q2: In which month was the expense lowest?**
- Feb (₹7500) → **February** ✅

**Q3: What was the % increase from Jan to Mar?**
- (9000−8000)/8000 × 100 = 1000/8000 × 100 = **12.5%** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Tips for Tables/Bar Graphs

1. **For %:** Just multiply quickly: 30% of 180 = 3 × 18 = 54
2. **For large numbers:** Round to nearest 100/1000, calculate, adjust
3. **Approximation is OK** for MCQs — options are usually spaced far apart
4. **Find max/min visually** — scan the column/row for highest/lowest

## 📝 Practice Problems

**Use this table for all problems:**

| Year | Company A | Company B | Company C |
|------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| 2019 | 400 | 300 | 500 |
| 2020 | 450 | 350 | 480 |
| 2021 | 500 | 320 | 550 |
| 2022 | 480 | 400 | 600 |

(Values in ₹ crore)

1. What was the % increase in Company A's revenue from 2019 to 2021?

2. In which year was Company B's revenue lowest?

3. What is the total revenue of all three companies in 2022?

4. What is the ratio of Company A's revenue to Company C's revenue in 2020?

5. What was the average annual revenue of Company B over the 4 years?

6. Which company showed the highest % growth from 2019 to 2022?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. A: 2019=400, 2021=500. % increase = (500−400)/400 × 100 = 100/400 × 100 = **25%**
2. Company B was lowest in **2019** (300)
3. Total 2022 = 480+400+600 = **₹1480 crore**
4. A:C in 2020 = 450:480 = 15:16 → **15:16**
5. B total = 300+350+320+400 = 1370. Average = 1370/4 = **₹342.5 crore**
6. A: (480−400)/400=20%. B: (400−300)/300=33.3%. C: (600−500)/500=20%. **Company B (33.3%)** ✅
