# Data Interpretation — Complete Guide

## 🔍 Types of DI

1. **Tables** — rows and columns of data
2. **Bar graphs** — vertical/horizontal bars
3. **Line graphs** — trend lines
4. **Pie charts** — circular representation
5. **Caselets** — data given as a paragraph

---

## 🧠 General Strategy for Any DI

1. **Read the title** — understand what data is about
2. **Note the units** — thousands, lakhs, %, etc.
3. **Read the question first**, then find the data (don't read everything)
4. Use approximation for complex calculations

---

## 📐 Key Formulas for DI Questions

| Question Type | Formula |
|--------------|---------|
| % change | (New − Old)/Old × 100 |
| % of total | (Part/Total) × 100 |
| Ratio | Divide two values |
| Average | Sum/Count |
| Pie chart angle | (Value/Total) × 360 |

---

## 🥧 Pie Charts

> **Angle for a segment = (Percentage/100) × 360°**

Or: **Angle = (Value/Total) × 360°**

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Table — % change)

| Year | Sales (₹ lakhs) |
|------|----------------|
| 2020 | 150 |
| 2021 | 180 |
| 2022 | 162 |
| 2023 | 198 |

**Q: What is the % change in sales from 2021 to 2022?**

- Change = 162 − 180 = −18 (decrease)
- % change = (18/180) × 100 = **10% decrease** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Bar Graph reading)

**Given:** A bar graph shows monthly expenses of a family.
- Jan: ₹8000, Feb: ₹7500, Mar: ₹9000, Apr: ₹8500

**Q: Average monthly expense for these 4 months?**

- Sum = 33000
- Average = 33000/4 = **₹8250** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Pie Chart)

**Given:** A pie chart shows budget allocation.
- Food: 30%, Rent: 25%, Education: 20%, Transport: 15%, Others: 10%
- Total budget: ₹60,000

**Q1: Amount spent on food?**
- 30% of 60000 = **₹18,000** ✅

**Q2: Angle for Education in pie chart?**
- (20/100) × 360 = **72°** ✅

**Q3: How much more is spent on Rent than Transport?**
- Rent = 25% × 60000 = 15000
- Transport = 15% × 60000 = 9000
- Difference = **₹6000** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Line Graph — trend)

**Given:** Line graph shows temperature over 5 days.
- Mon: 30°C, Tue: 32°C, Wed: 29°C, Thu: 33°C, Fri: 31°C

**Q: On which day was the rise from previous day greatest?**

- Mon to Tue: +2
- Tue to Wed: −3
- Wed to Thu: +4 ← greatest rise
- Thu to Fri: −2

**Answer: Thursday** ✅

---

### Example 5 (Caselet)

**Given:** "A company employs 500 people. 60% are male. Of the males, 40% are engineers. 30% of females are engineers."

**Q: Total engineers?**
- Males = 300, Female = 200
- Male engineers = 40% × 300 = 120
- Female engineers = 30% × 200 = 60
- Total = **180 engineers** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Tips

1. **For %:** Just multiply quickly: 30% of 180 = 3 × 18 = 54
2. **For large numbers:** Round to nearest 100/1000, calculate, adjust
3. **Approximation is OK** for MCQs — options are usually spaced far apart
4. **Pie chart:** If % given, don't convert to angle unless asked

## 📝 Practice Problems

**Use this table for Q1-Q4:**

| Product | Q1 Sales | Q2 Sales | Q3 Sales | Q4 Sales |
|---------|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| A | 200 | 250 | 220 | 280 |
| B | 150 | 180 | 160 | 200 |
| C | 300 | 280 | 320 | 350 |

1. Total sales of Product A in all 4 quarters?

2. In which quarter was Product C's sales highest?

3. What % of total Q1 sales is Product B's Q1 sales?

4. Average quarterly sales of Product A?

**Use this pie chart data for Q5-Q7:**
Total production = 50,000 units.
- Factory A: 35%, Factory B: 25%, Factory C: 20%, Factory D: 20%

5. How many units does Factory B produce?

6. How many MORE units does Factory A produce than Factory C?

7. What is the angle for Factory D in the pie chart?

**Caselet for Q8-Q10:**
"A school has 800 students. 55% are boys. 70% of boys and 60% of girls participate in sports."

8. How many girls are there?

9. How many boys participate in sports?

10. What % of total students participate in sports?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. 200+250+220+280 = **950 units**
2. **Q4** (350 is highest)
3. 150/(200+150+300) × 100 = 150/650 × 100 ≈ **23.08%**
4. 950/4 = **237.5 units**
5. 25% × 50000 = **12,500 units**
6. (35−20)% × 50000 = 15% × 50000 = **7,500 units**
7. (20/100) × 360 = **72°**
8. 45% × 800 = **360 girls**
9. 55% × 800 = 440 boys. 70% of 440 = **308 boys**
10. Boys: 308, Girls: 60% × 360 = 216. Total = 524. % = 524/800 × 100 = **65.5%**
