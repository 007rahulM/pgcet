# Pattern 2: Pie Charts and Line Graphs

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

- **Pie chart:** Circular diagram showing percentages or proportions
- **Line graph:** Points connected by lines showing trends over time
- "Find the angle for sector X"
- "On which day was the rise highest?"

---

## 📐 Key Formulas

| Question Type | Formula |
|--------------|---------|
| Pie chart angle | (Percentage/100) × 360° |
| Pie chart angle from value | (Value/Total) × 360° |
| Amount from % | (Percentage/100) × Total value |
| % from pie chart | (Angle/360) × 100 |

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Pie Chart — basic)

**Given:** A pie chart shows budget allocation.
- Food: 30%, Rent: 25%, Education: 20%, Transport: 15%, Others: 10%
- Total budget: ₹60,000

**Q1: Amount spent on food?**
- 30% of 60000 = 0.30 × 60000 = **₹18,000** ✅

**Q2: Angle for Education in pie chart?**
- (20/100) × 360 = 0.20 × 360 = **72°** ✅

**Q3: How much more is spent on Rent than Transport?**
- Rent = 25% × 60000 = ₹15,000
- Transport = 15% × 60000 = ₹9,000
- Difference = **₹6,000** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Pie Chart — factory production)

**Given:** Total production = 50,000 units.
- Factory A: 35%, Factory B: 25%, Factory C: 20%, Factory D: 20%

**Q1: How many units does Factory B produce?**
- 25% × 50000 = **12,500 units** ✅

**Q2: How many MORE units does Factory A produce than Factory C?**
- A: 35% × 50000 = 17,500
- C: 20% × 50000 = 10,000
- Difference = **7,500 units** ✅

**Q3: What is the angle for Factory D in the pie chart?**
- (20/100) × 360 = **72°** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Line Graph — finding trends)

**Given:** Line graph shows temperature over 5 days.
- Mon: 30°C, Tue: 32°C, Wed: 29°C, Thu: 33°C, Fri: 31°C

**Q1: On which day was the rise from previous day greatest?**
- Mon to Tue: +2
- Tue to Wed: −3 (decrease)
- Wed to Thu: +4 ← greatest rise
- Thu to Fri: −2

**Answer: Thursday** ✅

**Q2: What was the average temperature?**
- Sum = 30+32+29+33+31 = 155
- Average = 155/5 = **31°C** ✅

**Q3: What was the % increase from Monday to Thursday?**
- (33−30)/30 × 100 = 3/30 × 100 = **10%** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Line Graph — multiple series)

**Given:** Line graph shows sales of 2 companies (₹ lakhs):
- Company X: Jan=40, Feb=50, Mar=45, Apr=60, May=55
- Company Y: Jan=30, Feb=35, Mar=40, Apr=35, May=50

**Q1: In how many months was Company X's sales higher than Company Y?**
- Jan: 40>30 ✓
- Feb: 50>35 ✓
- Mar: 45>40 ✓
- Apr: 60>35 ✓
- May: 55>50 ✓
- All 5 months: **5 months** ✅

**Q2: In which month was the difference between X and Y the greatest?**
- Jan: 10, Feb: 15, Mar: 5, Apr: 25, May: 5
- **April (difference = 25)** ✅

## ⚡ 60-Second Tips

**For Pie Charts:**
- If % given: amount = (% / 100) × total
- Angle = % × 3.6 (since 100% = 360°)
- Don't convert to angle unless specifically asked

**For Line Graphs:**
- To find greatest rise/fall: calculate differences between consecutive points
- Look for steepest slope visually
- For "total" questions: add all values shown

## 📝 Practice Problems

**Pie Chart Data:**
Total monthly income = ₹50,000
- House Rent: 40%, Food: 25%, Education: 15%, Transport: 10%, Savings: 10%

1. How much does the person spend on Food per month?

2. What is the angle representing Savings in the pie chart?

3. How much more is spent on House Rent than Education?

4. What fraction of the income is spent on Transport?

**Line Graph Data:**
Monthly rainfall (mm): Jan=80, Feb=60, Mar=40, Apr=20, May=10, Jun=90, Jul=150, Aug=160, Sep=120, Oct=70, Nov=50, Dec=90

5. In which month was rainfall highest?

6. What was the total rainfall in the first quarter (Jan–Mar)?

7. In which month was the drop from previous month highest?

8. What was the average monthly rainfall for the year?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. 25% × 50000 = **₹12,500**
2. 10% → (10/100) × 360 = **36°**
3. Rent: 40% × 50000 = ₹20,000. Education: 15% × 50000 = ₹7,500. Difference = **₹12,500**
4. 10% = **1/10**
5. **August** (160 mm)
6. 80+60+40 = **180 mm**
7. Check drops: Feb(60)−Jan(80)=−20, Mar(40)−Feb(60)=−20, Apr(20)−Mar(40)=−20, May(10)−Apr(20)=−10. Biggest drop = **20 mm** (Feb, Mar, or Apr from previous — tied at 20)
8. Total = 80+60+40+20+10+90+150+160+120+70+50+90 = 940. Average = 940/12 ≈ **78.3 mm**
