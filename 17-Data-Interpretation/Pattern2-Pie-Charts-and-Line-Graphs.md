# Pattern 2: Pie Charts and Line Graphs

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

- **Pie chart:** Circular diagram showing percentages or proportions
- **Line graph:** Points connected by lines showing trends over time
- "Find the angle for sector X"
- "On which day was the rise highest?"

### Quick Visual Templates

**Pie chart (labeled sectors):**
```
           Food 30%
        ___________
      /      |      \
     /  30%  |  20%  \
    |   Food | Rent   |
    |--------|--------|   ← Each sector angle = % × 3.6
    |  25%   |  25%   |
     \ Trans |  Edu  /
      \______|______/
      Transport 25%
```

## 📐 Key Formulas

| Question Type | Formula |
|--------------|---------|
| Pie chart angle | (Percentage/100) × 360° |
| Pie chart angle from value | (Value/Total) × 360° |
| Amount from % | (Percentage/100) × Total value |
| % from pie chart | (Angle/360) × 100 |

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**❓ Question:** A pie chart shows budget allocation (Total = ₹60,000): Food 30%, Rent 25%, Education 20%, Transport 15%, Others 10%. Find: amount spent on food, angle for education sector, and how much more is spent on rent than transport.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Percentage breakdown and total budget of ₹60,000
- Find: Amount for food; pie chart angle for education; rent vs transport difference

**💡 What I'll use:** Amount = (percentage/100) × total; Pie chart angle = (percentage/100) × 360°

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Food amount = 30% × 60000 = 0.30 × 60000 = **₹18,000**
Step 2: Education angle = (20/100) × 360 = **72°**
Step 3: Rent = 25% × 60000 = ₹15,000; Transport = 15% × 60000 = ₹9,000
Step 4: Difference = 15,000 − 9,000 = **₹6,000**

**✅ Answer: ₹18,000; 72°; ₹6,000 more on Rent than Transport**

---

### Example 2

**❓ Question:** Total factory production = 50,000 units distributed as: Factory A=35%, B=25%, C=20%, D=20%. How many units does Factory B produce? How many MORE units does A produce than C? What is the pie chart angle for Factory D?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Total production = 50,000 units with percentage share for 4 factories
- Find: B's units; A minus C units; D's angle in the pie chart

**💡 What I'll use:** Units = (percentage/100) × total; Angle = (percentage/100) × 360°

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: B's production = 25% × 50000 = **12,500 units**
Step 2: A = 35% × 50000 = 17,500; C = 20% × 50000 = 10,000; Difference = **7,500 units**
Step 3: D's angle = (20/100) × 360 = **72°**

**✅ Answer: 12,500 units; 7,500 units more; 72°**

---

### Example 3

**❓ Question:** A line graph shows temperatures: Mon=30°C, Tue=32°C, Wed=29°C, Thu=33°C, Fri=31°C. On which day was the rise from the previous day greatest? What was the average temperature? What was the % increase from Monday to Thursday?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Daily temperature readings for 5 days
- Find: Day with greatest rise; average; % increase Mon→Thu

**💡 What I'll use:** Daily change = Today − Yesterday; Average = Sum/Count; % change formula

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Calculate daily changes:
- Mon→Tue: +2, Tue→Wed: −3 (drop), Wed→Thu: +4, Thu→Fri: −2
- Greatest rise: +4 on **Thursday**
Step 2: Average = (30+32+29+33+31)/5 = 155/5 = **31°C**
Step 3: % increase Mon→Thu = (33−30)/30 × 100 = 3/30 × 100 = **10%**

**✅ Answer: Thursday had the greatest rise; 31°C average; 10% increase Mon to Thu**

### Example 4

**❓ Question:** A line graph shows monthly sales (₹ lakhs): Company X: Jan=40, Feb=50, Mar=45, Apr=60, May=55. Company Y: Jan=30, Feb=35, Mar=40, Apr=35, May=50. In how many months was X's sales higher than Y? In which month was the gap between them greatest?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Monthly sales of two companies over 5 months
- Find: Count of months where X > Y; month with largest difference

**💡 What I'll use:** Direct month-by-month comparison; difference = X − Y for each month

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Compare each month:
- Jan: 40 > 30 ✓, Feb: 50 > 35 ✓, Mar: 45 > 40 ✓, Apr: 60 > 35 ✓, May: 55 > 50 ✓
- X is higher in all **5 months**
Step 2: Differences: Jan=10, Feb=15, Mar=5, Apr=25, May=5
- Largest gap: **April** (difference = 25)

**✅ Answer: 5 months; April had the greatest difference (25 lakhs)**

## ⚡ 60-Second Tips

**For Pie Charts:**
- If % given: amount = (% / 100) × total
- Angle = % × 3.6 (since 100% = 360°)
- Don't convert to angle unless specifically asked

**For Line Graphs:**
- To find greatest rise/fall: calculate differences between consecutive points
- Look for steepest slope visually
- For "total" questions: add all values shown
- For unclear graph print, write points as a mini-table first
