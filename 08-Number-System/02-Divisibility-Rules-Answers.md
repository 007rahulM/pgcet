### Q1

**❓ Question:** Is 348 divisible by 4?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: The number 348
- Find: Whether it is divisible by 4

**💡 What I'll use:** Divisibility rule for 4 — a number is divisible by 4 if its **last two digits** form a number divisible by 4

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Take the last two digits of 348 → **48**

Step 2: Check: 48 ÷ 4 = 12 (exact, no remainder)

Step 3: Since 48 is divisible by 4, so is 348.

**✅ Answer: Yes, 348 is divisible by 4**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** Is 6435 divisible by 9?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: The number 6435
- Find: Whether it is divisible by 9

**💡 What I'll use:** Divisibility rule for 9 — a number is divisible by 9 if the **sum of its digits** is divisible by 9

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Sum of digits = 6 + 4 + 3 + 5 = **18**

Step 2: Check: 18 ÷ 9 = 2 (exact)

Step 3: Since the digit sum 18 is divisible by 9, so is 6435.

**✅ Answer: Yes, 6435 is divisible by 9**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** Is 12345 divisible by 3 and by 5?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: The number 12345
- Find: Divisibility by 3 AND by 5

**💡 What I'll use:**
- Rule for 3: sum of digits divisible by 3
- Rule for 5: last digit is 0 or 5

**✏️ My Solution:**

**Divisibility by 3:**

Step 1: Sum of digits = 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 = **15**

Step 2: 15 ÷ 3 = 5 → Yes, divisible by 3 ✓

**Divisibility by 5:**

Step 3: Last digit = **5** → ends in 0 or 5 → Yes, divisible by 5 ✓

**✅ Answer: Yes, 12345 is divisible by both 3 and 5**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** How many 3-digit numbers are divisible by 7?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 3-digit numbers range from 100 to 999
- Find: Count of numbers in this range divisible by 7

**💡 What I'll use:** Count = (Last term − First term) ÷ 7 + 1

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: First 3-digit multiple of 7 → 100 ÷ 7 = 14.28… → 15 × 7 = **105**

Step 2: Last 3-digit multiple of 7 → 999 ÷ 7 = 142.71… → 142 × 7 = **994**

Step 3: Count = (994 − 105) ÷ 7 + 1 = 889 ÷ 7 + 1 = 127 + 1 = **128**

**✅ Answer: 128 three-digit numbers are divisible by 7**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** What digit should replace the blank in 45_8 to make it divisible by 9?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Number is 45_8 where _ is a single unknown digit
- Find: The digit that makes the number divisible by 9

**💡 What I'll use:** Divisibility rule for 9 — sum of all digits must be divisible by 9

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Known digit sum = 4 + 5 + _ + 8 = 17 + _

Step 2: Need (17 + _) to be divisible by 9

Step 3: Next multiple of 9 after 17 → 18

Step 4: _ = 18 − 17 = **1**

*(Verification: 4 + 5 + 1 + 8 = 18, and 18 ÷ 9 = 2 ✓)*

**✅ Answer: The missing digit is 1 (making the number 4518)**
