## 📐 LCM and HCF

### Definitions:
- **HCF** (Highest Common Factor) = Largest number that divides all given numbers
- **LCM** (Least Common Multiple) = Smallest number divisible by all given numbers

### Key Relationship:
> **Product of two numbers = LCM × HCF**

## 🔢 Divisibility Rules (Must Memorize!)

| Divisible by | Rule |
|-------------|------|
| 2 | Last digit is even (0,2,4,6,8) |
| 3 | Sum of digits divisible by 3 |
| 4 | Last 2 digits divisible by 4 |
| 5 | Ends in 0 or 5 |
| 6 | Divisible by both 2 AND 3 |
| 7 | Double last digit, subtract from rest. Repeat. Result divisible by 7 |
| 8 | Last 3 digits divisible by 8 |
| 9 | Sum of digits divisible by 9 |
| 10 | Ends in 0 |
| 11 | Alternating sum of digits divisible by 11 |

### Divisibility by 11 Example:
- 918082: (9+8+8) − (1+0+2) = 25 − 3 = 22 → **divisible by 11** ✅

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Find HCF)

**Problem:** HCF of 48 and 72.

- 48 = 2⁴ × 3
- 72 = 2³ × 3²
- HCF = 2³ × 3 = **24** ✅

**Short method (division):** 72 = 1×48 + 24 → 48 = 2×24 + 0 → HCF = **24**

---

### Example 2 (Find LCM)

**Problem:** LCM of 12, 18, 24.

- 12 = 2² × 3
- 18 = 2 × 3²
- 24 = 2³ × 3
- LCM = 2³ × 3² = 8 × 9 = **72** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Use LCM × HCF = Product)

**Problem:** LCM of two numbers = 180, HCF = 12. One number = 36. Find other.

- Product = LCM × HCF = 180 × 12 = 2160
- Other = 2160 ÷ 36 = **60** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Largest number to divide all — HCF problem)

**Problem:** Find the largest number that divides 50, 75, 100 leaving remainder 5 each time.

- Subtract remainder: 45, 70, 95
- HCF(45, 70, 95) = **5** ✅

---

### Example 5 (Smallest number — LCM problem)

**Problem:** Smallest number divisible by 4, 6, 9.

- LCM(4, 6, 9) = **36** ✅

---

### Example 6 (Bells ringing)

**Problem:** Three bells ring at intervals of 6, 8, 12 minutes. They ring together at 9 AM. When next together?

- LCM(6, 8, 12) = 24 minutes
- Next together: **9:24 AM** ✅

---

### Example 7 (Divisibility)

**Problem:** Is 456 divisible by 3?

- Sum = 4+5+6 = 15. 15 ÷ 3 = 5. **Yes** ✅

**Problem:** Is 728 divisible by 8?

- Last 3 digits = 728. 728 ÷ 8 = 91. **Yes** ✅

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcuts

- **HCF of fractions:** HCF of numerators / LCM of denominators
- **LCM of fractions:** LCM of numerators / HCF of denominators
- To check if prime: check divisibility by all primes up to √n

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. Find HCF and LCM of 12 and 18.

2. Two numbers have HCF = 8, LCM = 96. One number = 24. Find other.

3. Is 7548 divisible by 9?

4. Find smallest 4-digit number divisible by 8, 12, 15.

5. Find HCF of 36, 48, 60.

6. Bells ring every 15, 20, 25 minutes. Ring together at 10 AM. Next time?

7. How many 3-digit numbers are divisible by 7?

8. Find numbers between 1 and 100 that are divisible by both 4 and 6.

## ✔️ Answers

1. HCF=6, LCM=36
2. Product = 8×96 = 768. Other = 768/24 = **32**
3. 7+5+4+8 = 24. 24/9 = not integer. **No**
4. LCM(8,12,15) = 120. First 4-digit = 1000. 1000/120 = 8.33 → 9×120 = **1080**
5. HCF(36,48,60) = **12**
6. LCM(15,20,25) = 300 min = 5 hours. Next = **3 PM**
7. First = 105, Last = 994. Count = (994−105)/7 + 1 = 889/7 + 1 = 127+1 = **128**
8. LCM(4,6) = 12. Multiples of 12 from 1-100: 12,24,36,48,60,72,84,96 → **8 numbers**
