## 📐 Number Types (Quick Reference)

| Type | Definition | Examples |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Natural | Counting numbers | 1, 2, 3... |
| Whole | Natural + 0 | 0, 1, 2, 3... |
| Integer | +/- whole numbers | ...-2,-1,0,1,2... |
| Prime | Divisible only by 1 and itself | 2,3,5,7,11,13... |
| Composite | More than 2 factors | 4,6,8,9,10... |
| Perfect square | n² | 1,4,9,16,25... |
| Perfect cube | n³ | 1,8,27,64,125... |

**Note:** 1 is neither prime nor composite.

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Check if Prime)

**Problem:** Is 97 a prime number?

- √97 ≈ 9.8, check primes up to 9: 2, 3, 5, 7
- 97 is odd (not ÷2), 9+7=16 (not ÷3), doesn't end in 0/5 (not ÷5), 97÷7=13.8 (not ÷7)
- **97 is prime** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Count of Factors)

**Problem:** How many factors does 36 have?

- 36 = 2² × 3²
- Number of factors = (2+1) × (2+1) = **9** ✅
- They are: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36

---

### Example 3 (Consecutive Integers Sum)

**Problem:** Sum of all integers from 1 to 100.

- Sum = n(n+1)/2 = 100×101/2 = **5050** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Remainder)

**Problem:** What is the remainder when 2^10 is divided by 3?

- 2^1 ÷ 3 = remainder 2
- 2^2 ÷ 3 = 4 ÷ 3 = remainder 1
- 2^3 ÷ 3 = 8 ÷ 3 = remainder 2
- Pattern: 2, 1, 2, 1... (period 2)
- 2^10: 10 is even → remainder = **1** ✅

---

## ⚡ Shortcuts

- **Number of factors of n = a^p × b^q × c^r:** (p+1)(q+1)(r+1)
- **Sum of 1 to n:** n(n+1)/2
- **Sum of squares 1² to n²:** n(n+1)(2n+1)/6
- **Sum of cubes 1³ to n³:** [n(n+1)/2]²

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. List all primes between 40 and 60.

2. How many factors does 72 have?

3. What is the sum of all even numbers from 2 to 50?

4. Is 169 a perfect square? Find its square root.

5. Sum of first 20 natural numbers?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. **41, 43, 47, 53, 59**
2. 72 = 2³ × 3². Factors = (3+1)(2+1) = **12**
3. Sum = 2+4+...+50 = 2(1+2+...+25) = 2×325 = **650**
4. √169 = **13** (yes, perfect square)
5. 20×21/2 = **210**
