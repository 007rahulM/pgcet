### Q1

**❓ Question:** Find the HCF and LCM of 12 and 18.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Two numbers — 12 and 18
- Find: Highest Common Factor (HCF) and Lowest Common Multiple (LCM)

**💡 What I'll use:** Prime factorisation — HCF = product of common factors with lowest powers; LCM = product of all factors with highest powers

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Prime factorise both numbers
- 12 = 2² × 3
- 18 = 2 × 3²

Step 2: HCF = take common primes with **lowest** power → 2¹ × 3¹ = **6**

Step 3: LCM = take all primes with **highest** power → 2² × 3² = 4 × 9 = **36**

*(Verification: 12 × 18 = 216 = 6 × 36 ✓)*

**✅ Answer: HCF = 6, LCM = 36**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** HCF of two numbers is 8, LCM is 96. One number is 24. Find the other.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: HCF = 8, LCM = 96, one number = 24
- Find: The other number

**💡 What I'll use:** Product of two numbers = HCF × LCM

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Product of both numbers = HCF × LCM = 8 × 96 = 768

Step 2: Other number = 768 ÷ 24 = **32**

*(Verification: HCF(24, 32) = 8 ✓, LCM(24, 32) = 96 ✓)*

**✅ Answer: 32**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** Find the HCF of 36, 48, and 60.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Three numbers — 36, 48, 60
- Find: Their HCF

**💡 What I'll use:** Prime factorisation — HCF = product of common prime factors with lowest powers

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Factorise each:
- 36 = 2² × 3²
- 48 = 2⁴ × 3
- 60 = 2² × 3 × 5

Step 2: Common primes: 2 and 3

Step 3: Lowest powers: 2² = 4, 3¹ = 3

Step 4: HCF = 4 × 3 = **12**

**✅ Answer: 12**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** Three bells ring every 15, 20, and 25 minutes respectively. They all ring together at 10:00 AM. When will they next ring together?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Bells ring at intervals of 15, 20, 25 minutes; all ring together at 10:00 AM
- Find: Next time all three ring together

**💡 What I'll use:** LCM of the intervals gives the time until next simultaneous ring

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find LCM(15, 20, 25)
- 15 = 3 × 5
- 20 = 2² × 5
- 25 = 5²
- LCM = 2² × 3 × 5² = 4 × 3 × 25 = **300 minutes**

Step 2: Convert to hours → 300 ÷ 60 = 5 hours

Step 3: Next time = 10:00 AM + 5 hours = **3:00 PM**

**✅ Answer: 3:00 PM**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** Find the smallest 4-digit number divisible by 8, 12, and 15.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Number must be divisible by 8, 12, and 15; must be a 4-digit number
- Find: Smallest such number

**💡 What I'll use:** LCM gives smallest number divisible by all three; then find first multiple ≥ 1000

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find LCM(8, 12, 15)
- 8 = 2³
- 12 = 2² × 3
- 15 = 3 × 5
- LCM = 2³ × 3 × 5 = **120**

Step 2: Smallest 4-digit number ≥ 1000: 1000 ÷ 120 = 8.33…

Step 3: Next whole multiple → 9 × 120 = **1080**

**✅ Answer: 1080**
