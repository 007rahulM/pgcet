## SECTION A: Mathematical Aptitude

**Q1.** A can complete a work in 12 days and B can complete it in 18 days. Working together, how many days will they take?
- (A) 7.2 days  (B) 6 days  (C) 7 days  (D) 8 days

**Answer: (A) 7.2 days**
**Solution:**
- LCM(12, 18) = 36 (total work units)
- A's efficiency = 36/12 = 3 units/day
- B's efficiency = 36/18 = 2 units/day
- Combined = 5 units/day
- Time = 36/5 = **7.2 days**

---

**Q2.** A shopkeeper buys an article for ₹800 and sells it for ₹1000. What is the profit percentage?
- (A) 20%  (B) 25%  (C) 15%  (D) 30%

**Answer: (B) 25%**
**Solution:**
- Profit = 1000 − 800 = ₹200
- Profit% = (200/800) × 100 = **25%**

---

**Q3.** What is the decimal equivalent of binary 10110₂?
- (A) 18  (B) 20  (C) 22  (D) 24

**Answer: (C) 22**
**Solution:**
- 1×16 + 0×8 + 1×4 + 1×2 + 0×1
- = 16 + 0 + 4 + 2 + 0 = **22**

---

**Q4.** In a class of 60 students, 40 play cricket, 30 play football, and 15 play both. How many play neither?
- (A) 5  (B) 10  (C) 15  (D) 20

**Answer: (A) 5**
**Solution:**
- n(C∪F) = 40 + 30 − 15 = 55
- Neither = 60 − 55 = **5**

---

**Q5.** The simple interest on ₹5000 at 8% per annum for 3 years is:
- (A) ₹1000  (B) ₹1200  (C) ₹1500  (D) ₹1800

**Answer: (B) ₹1200**
**Solution:**
- SI = (P × R × T)/100 = (5000 × 8 × 3)/100 = **₹1200**

---

**Q6.** If the mean of 5 numbers is 20 and one number is removed, the new mean is 18. What was the removed number?
- (A) 26  (B) 28  (C) 30  (D) 32

**Answer: (B) 28**
**Solution:**
- Sum of 5 numbers = 5 × 20 = 100
- Sum of 4 numbers = 4 × 18 = 72
- Removed number = 100 − 72 = **28**

---

**Q7.** A train 200m long passes a pole in 10 seconds. What is the speed of the train?
- (A) 54 km/h  (B) 60 km/h  (C) 72 km/h  (D) 80 km/h

**Answer: (C) 72 km/h**
**Solution:**
- Speed = Distance/Time = 200/10 = 20 m/s
- Convert to km/h: 20 × 18/5 = **72 km/h**

---

**Q8.** What is 4P2 (permutation)?
- (A) 4  (B) 8  (C) 12  (D) 16

**Answer: (C) 12**
**Solution:**
- 4P2 = 4!/(4−2)! = 4!/2! = (4×3×2×1)/(2×1) = 24/2 = **12**

---

**Q9.** A coin is tossed twice. What is the probability of getting exactly one head?
- (A) 1/4  (B) 1/2  (C) 3/4  (D) 1

**Answer: (B) 1/2**
**Solution:**
- Total outcomes = {HH, HT, TH, TT} = 4
- Exactly one head = {HT, TH} = 2
- Probability = 2/4 = **1/2**

---

**Q10.** Boats downstream speed = 20 km/h, upstream = 14 km/h. What is the stream speed?
- (A) 2 km/h  (B) 3 km/h  (C) 4 km/h  (D) 5 km/h

**Answer: (B) 3 km/h**
**Solution:**
- Stream speed = (Downstream − Upstream)/2 = (20 − 14)/2 = 6/2 = **3 km/h**

## SECTION B: Computer Awareness

**Q11.** How many bytes are in 1 Kilobyte?
- (A) 1000  (B) 1024  (C) 512  (D) 2048

**Answer: (B) 1024**
**Solution:** 1 KB = 2¹⁰ = 1024 bytes

---

**Q12.** Which data structure uses LIFO principle?
- (A) Queue  (B) Array  (C) Stack  (D) Tree

**Answer: (C) Stack**
**Solution:** Stack = Last In, First Out (LIFO). Queue = FIFO.

---

**Q13.** What is the hexadecimal equivalent of decimal 255?
- (A) EF  (B) FE  (C) FF  (D) F0

**Answer: (C) FF**
**Solution:**
- 255 ÷ 16 = 15 R 15 → F
- 15 ÷ 16 = 0 R 15 → F
- Result = **FF**

---

**Q14.** Which SQL command is used to delete an entire table?
- (A) DELETE  (B) TRUNCATE  (C) REMOVE  (D) DROP

**Answer: (D) DROP**
**Solution:** DROP TABLE removes table structure AND data. DELETE removes only rows. TRUNCATE removes all rows but keeps structure.

---

**Q15.** The OSI model has how many layers?
- (A) 4  (B) 5  (C) 6  (D) 7

**Answer: (D) 7**
**Solution:** OSI = 7 layers: Physical, Data Link, Network, Transport, Session, Presentation, Application.

---

**Q16.** Which of the following is a secondary storage device?
- (A) RAM  (B) ROM  (C) Cache  (D) Hard Disk

**Answer: (D) Hard Disk**
**Solution:** Secondary storage = external, non-volatile, large capacity. RAM/ROM/Cache are primary memory.

---

**Q17.** What is the worst-case time complexity of Quick Sort?
- (A) O(n)  (B) O(n log n)  (C) O(n²)  (D) O(log n)

**Answer: (C) O(n²)**
**Solution:** When the pivot is always minimum/maximum (sorted input), Quick Sort degrades to O(n²).

---

**Q18.** Which memory is lost when power is off?
- (A) ROM  (B) RAM  (C) EEPROM  (D) Flash

**Answer: (B) RAM**
**Solution:** RAM = volatile memory. All data is lost when power is removed. ROM and Flash are non-volatile.

---

**Q19.** In C, which operator gives the address of a variable?
- (A) *  (B) &  (C) #  (D) @

**Answer: (B) &**
**Solution:** `&x` gives the memory address of variable x. `*p` dereferences a pointer (gives value at address p).

---

**Q20.** Which protocol is used for email sending?
- (A) POP3  (B) HTTP  (C) SMTP  (D) FTP

**Answer: (C) SMTP**
**Solution:** SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol) = sending email. POP3/IMAP = receiving email. HTTP = web pages. FTP = file transfer.

## SECTION C: Analytical & Logical Reasoning

**Q21.** Pointing to a woman, Ravi says "She is the daughter of my father's wife's mother." How is she related to Ravi?
- (A) Sister  (B) Aunt  (C) Mother  (D) Cousin

**Answer: (B) Aunt**
**Solution:**
- Ravi's father's wife = Ravi's mother
- Ravi's mother's mother = Ravi's maternal grandmother
- Grandmother's daughter = Ravi's mother's sister = Ravi's **aunt**

---

**Q22.** If BOOK is coded as CPPL, then DOOR is coded as:
- (A) EPPS  (B) EQPT  (C) EQQT  (D) EQPS

**Answer: (A) EPPS**
**Solution:**
- B+1=C, O+1=P, O+1=P, K+1=L → CPPL ✅
- D+1=E, O+1=P, O+1=P, R+1=S → **EPPS**

---

**Q23.** Ramu walks 5 km North, then 3 km East, then 5 km South. How far is he from the start?
- (A) 2 km  (B) 3 km  (C) 5 km  (D) 8 km

**Answer: (B) 3 km**
**Solution:**
- 5 km North, then 5 km South → cancels out. Net N-S = 0
- 3 km East remains
- Distance = 3 km due **East**

---

**Q24.** Find the next number in series: 2, 6, 12, 20, 30, ?
- (A) 40  (B) 42  (C) 44  (D) 46

**Answer: (B) 42**
**Solution:**
- Differences: 4, 6, 8, 10, 12 (increasing by 2)
- Next = 30 + 12 = **42**

---

**Q25.** In a row of 20 students, Priya is 8th from left. What is her position from the right?
- (A) 12th  (B) 13th  (C) 14th  (D) 11th

**Answer: (B) 13th**
**Solution:**
- Position from right = Total + 1 − position from left = 20 + 1 − 8 = **13th**

## SECTION D: General English

**Q26.** Fill in the blank: "She has been working here _____ three years."
- (A) since  (B) for  (C) from  (D) during

**Answer: (B) for**
**Solution:** "for" + duration (three years). "since" + point in time (since 2020).

---

**Q27.** Synonym of "diligent":
- (A) Lazy  (B) Hardworking  (C) Noisy  (D) Careless

**Answer: (B) Hardworking**

---

**Q28.** Find the error: "The committee have taken their decision."
- (A) The  (B) committee  (C) have  (D) decision

**Answer: (C) have**
**Solution:** "Committee" is a collective noun → takes singular verb. Correct: "The committee **has** taken its decision."

---

**Q29.** Antonym of "transparent":
- (A) Clear  (B) Opaque  (C) Bright  (D) Visible

**Answer: (B) Opaque**

---

**Q30.** Choose the grammatically correct sentence:
- (A) Neither of the students have submitted.
- (B) Neither of the students has submitted.
- (C) Neither of the students is submitted.
- (D) Neither of the students was submitted.

**Answer: (B) Neither of the students has submitted.**
**Solution:** "Neither" with a plural noun uses a singular verb → "has" is correct.
