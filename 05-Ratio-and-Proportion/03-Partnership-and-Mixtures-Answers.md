# Ratio & Proportion: Partnership & Mixtures — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** A invests ₹8000 for 6 months, B invests ₹6000 for 8 months. Profit ratio?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A's capital = ₹8000 for 6 months; B's capital = ₹6000 for 8 months
- Find: Ratio in which they share profit

**💡 What I'll use:** Profit ratio = Capital × Time for each partner

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Calculate each partner's contribution
A's contribution = 8000 × 6 = 48,000
B's contribution = 6000 × 8 = 48,000

Step 2: Simplify the ratio
48,000 : 48,000 = **1:1**

**✅ Answer: Profit ratio = 1:1**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** A, B, C invest ₹3000, ₹4000, ₹5000 for 1 year. Profit = ₹3600. B's share?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Investments ₹3000, ₹4000, ₹5000 for equal time; total profit = ₹3600
- Find: B's share

**💡 What I'll use:** Profit is shared in ratio of investments (equal time)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Investment ratio
3000 : 4000 : 5000 = 3:4:5

Step 2: Total parts = 3 + 4 + 5 = 12

Step 3: B's share
B = (4/12) × 3600 = **₹1200**

**✅ Answer: B's share = ₹1200**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** How much water should be added to 20 litres of 80% milk solution to make it 50% milk?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 20 litres of 80% milk solution; need to dilute to 50% milk
- Find: Amount of water to add

**💡 What I'll use:** The amount of pure milk stays constant; find new total volume for 50% concentration

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find the amount of pure milk in the original solution
Milk = 80% × 20 = 16 litres

Step 2: After adding water, milk must still be 50% of new total
16 = 50% × New total
New total = 16 / 0.50 = 32 litres

Step 3: Water to add
Water added = 32 − 20 = **12 litres**

**✅ Answer: Add 12 litres of water**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** A joins a business with ₹12000. After 4 months B joins with ₹16000. After 1 year, profit = ₹11000. Find B's share.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A invests ₹12000 for full 12 months; B joins after 4 months, so invests for 8 months; total profit = ₹11000
- Find: B's share of the profit

**💡 What I'll use:** Profit ratio = Capital × Time; A for 12 months, B for (12 − 4) = 8 months

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Calculate each partner's contribution
A's contribution = 12000 × 12 = 144,000
B's contribution = 16000 × 8 = 128,000

Step 2: Simplify the ratio
144,000 : 128,000 = 144:128 = 9:8

Step 3: Total parts = 9 + 8 = 17

Step 4: B's share
B = (8/17) × 11000 = **₹5176.47 ≈ ₹5177**

**✅ Answer: B's share ≈ ₹5177**
