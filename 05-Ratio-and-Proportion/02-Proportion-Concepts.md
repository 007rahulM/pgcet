# Ratio & Proportion — Proportion Concepts

## 🔍 When This Comes Up

- "Find the fourth proportional"
- "Find mean proportional"
- Cross-multiplication problems
- Direct and inverse proportion

---

## 📐 Core Concepts

### Property of Proportion
> a : b = c : d means a×d = b×c (cross multiply)

### Types of Proportion

| Type | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Fourth Proportional** | a:b = c:? | If 2:3 = 8:? → ? = 12 |
| **Third Proportional** | a:b = b:? | If 2:4 = 4:? → ? = 8 |
| **Mean Proportional** | a:?=?:b → ?=√(ab) | Between 4 and 25 → 10 |
| **Direct Proportion** | x increases, y increases | Speed and Distance |
| **Inverse Proportion** | x increases, y decreases | Speed and Time |

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**❓ Question:** Find x if 3:5 = 12:x.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A proportion where three values are known: 3, 5, and 12
- Find: The fourth proportional x

**💡 What I'll use:** Cross-multiplication property: a × d = b × c

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Cross-multiply
3 × x = 5 × 12
3x = 60
x = **20**

**✅ Answer: x = 20**

---

### Example 2

**❓ Question:** Find the mean proportional between 4 and 25.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Two numbers — 4 and 25
- Find: A number m such that 4:m = m:25

**💡 What I'll use:** Mean proportional = √(product of the two numbers) = √(a × b)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Apply the formula
Mean proportional = √(4 × 25) = √100 = **10**

**✅ Answer: 10**

---

### Example 3

**❓ Question:** Find the third proportional to 3 and 6.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 3 and 6 are the first two terms of a proportion
- Find: x such that 3:6 = 6:x

**💡 What I'll use:** Cross-multiply the proportion to find x

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Set up the proportion
3 : 6 = 6 : x

Step 2: Cross-multiply
3 × x = 6 × 6
3x = 36
x = **12**

**✅ Answer: 12**

---

### Example 4

**❓ Question:** If 5 workers can complete a job in 8 days, how many days will 10 workers take?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 5 workers take 8 days
- Find: Days needed when the number of workers doubles to 10

**💡 What I'll use:** More workers → fewer days, so this is inverse proportion: workers × days = constant

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Set up the inverse proportion
5 × 8 = 10 × x
40 = 10x
x = **4 days**

**✅ Answer: 4 days**

---

### Example 5

**❓ Question:** How much of a 40% acid solution should be mixed with a 60% acid solution to get 30 litres of a 50% acid solution?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Two solutions — 40% acid and 60% acid; want 30 litres of 50% acid
- Find: How many litres of each solution to use

**💡 What I'll use:** Set up a mixture equation — total acid from both solutions = acid needed in final solution

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Let x = litres of 40% solution; then (30 − x) = litres of 60% solution

Step 2: Write the acid balance equation
0.4x + 0.6(30 − x) = 0.5 × 30

Step 3: Solve
0.4x + 18 − 0.6x = 15
−0.2x = −3
x = 15

So 15 litres of 40% solution and (30 − 15) = 15 litres of 60% solution.

**✅ Answer: 15 litres of each**

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcuts

- **Cross multiply to solve proportion:** a:b = c:d → ad = bc
- **Mean proportional** = geometric mean = √(product of extremes)
- **Direct vs Inverse:** more workers → less time (inverse); more speed → more distance (direct)
