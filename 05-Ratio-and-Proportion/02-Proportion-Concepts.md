# Ratio & Proportion — Proportion Concepts

## 🔍 When This Comes Up

- "Find the fourth proportional"
- "Find mean proportional"
- Cross-multiplication problems
- Direct and inverse proportion

---

## 📐 Core Concepts

### Property of Proportion
> a : b = c : d means a×d = b×c (cross multiply)

### Types of Proportion

| Type | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Fourth Proportional** | a:b = c:? | If 2:3 = 8:? → ? = 12 |
| **Third Proportional** | a:b = b:? | If 2:4 = 4:? → ? = 8 |
| **Mean Proportional** | a:?=?:b → ?=√(ab) | Between 4 and 25 → 10 |
| **Direct Proportion** | x increases, y increases | Speed and Distance |
| **Inverse Proportion** | x increases, y decreases | Speed and Time |

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Fourth Proportional)

**Problem:** Find x: 3:5 = 12:x

- 3 × x = 5 × 12 → x = 60/3 = **20** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Mean Proportional)

**Problem:** Find mean proportional between 4 and 25.

- Mean proportional = √(4 × 25) = √100 = **10** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Third Proportional)

**Problem:** Find the third proportional to 3 and 6.

- 3 : 6 = 6 : x
- 3x = 36 → x = **12** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Direct Proportion)

**Problem:** If 5 workers complete a job in 8 days, how many days will 10 workers take?

- More workers → fewer days → **Inverse proportion**
- 5 × 8 = 10 × x → x = **4 days** ✅

---

### Example 5 (Mixing Proportionally)

**Problem:** How much 40% acid solution should be mixed with 60% acid solution to get 30 litres of 50% acid?

- Let x litres of 40% and (30−x) litres of 60%
- 0.4x + 0.6(30−x) = 0.5 × 30
- 0.4x + 18 − 0.6x = 15
- −0.2x = −3 → x = 15
- **15 litres of each** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcuts

- **Cross multiply to solve proportion:** a:b = c:d → ad = bc
- **Mean proportional** = geometric mean = √(product of extremes)
- **Direct vs Inverse:** more workers → less time (inverse); more speed → more distance (direct)

---

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. Find x: 4:x = 8:14

2. Find mean proportional between 9 and 16.

3. Find the fourth proportional to 2, 5, 6.

4. If 8 pens cost ₹120, what do 15 pens cost?

5. A car travels 180 km in 3 hours. How long to travel 300 km at same speed?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. 4×14 = 8x → x = **7**
2. √(9 × 16) = √144 = **12**
3. 2:5 = 6:x → 2x = 30 → x = **15**
4. Direct proportion: 120/8 × 15 = **₹225**
5. Direct proportion: 3/180 × 300 = **5 hours**
