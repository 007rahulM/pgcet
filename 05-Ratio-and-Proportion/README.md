# Ratio & Proportion — Complete Guide

## 🔍 When This Comes Up

- Mixing problems
- Partnership questions
- Comparing quantities
- Often combined with other topics

---

## 📐 Core Concepts

### Basic Ratio
> a : b means a/b

### Property of Proportion
> a : b = c : d means a×d = b×c (cross multiply)

### Combining Ratios
If a:b and b:c are given, find a:b:c → make **b common**

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Basic)

**Problem:** Divide ₹600 in ratio 3:2.

- Total parts = 5
- First part = 3/5 × 600 = **₹360**
- Second part = 2/5 × 600 = **₹240** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Combined ratio)

**Problem:** A:B = 2:3, B:C = 4:5. Find A:B:C.

- Make B common: B is 3 in first, 4 in second
- Multiply first ratio by 4, second by 3:
  - A:B = 8:12, B:C = 12:15
- A:B:C = **8:12:15** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Partnership)

**Problem:** A invests ₹5000 for 12 months, B invests ₹6000 for 8 months. Profit ratio?

- A's contribution = 5000 × 12 = 60000
- B's contribution = 6000 × 8 = 48000
- Ratio = 60000 : 48000 = **5:4** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Proportion — fourth proportional)

**Problem:** Find x: 3:5 = 12:x

- 3 × x = 5 × 12 → x = 60/3 = **20** ✅

---

### Example 5 (Mean proportional)

**Problem:** Find mean proportional between 4 and 25.

- Mean proportional = √(4 × 25) = √100 = **10** ✅

---

### Example 6 (Mixing two solutions)

**Problem:** How much 40% acid solution should be mixed with 60% acid solution to get 30 litres of 50% acid?

- Let x litres of 40% and (30−x) litres of 60%
- 0.4x + 0.6(30−x) = 0.5 × 30
- 0.4x + 18 − 0.6x = 15
- −0.2x = −3 → x = 15
- **15 litres of each** ✅

---

### Example 7 (Ages)

**Problem:** Ratio of ages of A and B is 3:5. After 5 years it becomes 2:3. Find current ages.

- Now: A = 3x, B = 5x
- After 5 years: (3x+5)/(5x+5) = 2/3
- 9x+15 = 10x+10 → x = 5
- A = **15 years**, B = **25 years** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

**For dividing in ratio a:b:**
- Total parts = a + b
- Share = (a or b / total) × whole

**For partnership:** multiply Investment × Time

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. Divide ₹900 in ratio 4:5.

2. A:B = 3:4, B:C = 5:6. Find A:B:C.

3. A invests ₹8000 for 6 months, B invests ₹6000 for 8 months. Profit ratio?

4. If 4:x = 8:14, find x.

5. Ratio of A:B = 2:3 and B:C = 6:7. Find A:C.

6. Ages of A and B are in ratio 5:3. Sum of ages = 56. Find ages.

7. Mixture has milk and water in 3:1 ratio. Add 4 litres of water to 12 litres mixture. New ratio?

8. A, B, C invest ₹2000, ₹3000, ₹4000. Profit is ₹1800. What does B get?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. 400 : 500 → A=**₹400**, B=**₹500**
2. B=4 in first, 5 in second. Multiply: A:B=15:20, B:C=20:24 → **15:20:24**
3. 8000×6 : 6000×8 = 48000:48000 = **1:1**
4. 4×14 = 8x → x = **7**
5. A:B=2:3, B:C=6:7. B=3 and 6 → multiply first by 2: A:B=4:6. A:C = **4:7**
6. A = 5/8 × 56 = 35, B = 3/8 × 56 = **A=35, B=21**
7. Mixture 12L: milk=9, water=3. Add 4L water → milk=9, water=7. Ratio = **9:7**
8. Ratio = 2:3:4. B's share = 3/9 × 1800 = **₹600**
