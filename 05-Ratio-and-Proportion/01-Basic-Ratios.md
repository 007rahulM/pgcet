# Ratio & Proportion — Basic Ratios

## 🔍 When This Comes Up

- Dividing a quantity in a given ratio
- Comparing quantities
- Combining multiple ratios

---

## 📐 Core Concepts

### Basic Ratio
> a : b means a/b

### Properties:
- Ratio a : b = ka : kb (multiplying both by same number)
- a : b = a/k : b/k (dividing both by same number)

### Combining Ratios
If a:b and b:c are given, find a:b:c → make **b common**

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Dividing in Ratio)

**Problem:** Divide ₹600 in ratio 3:2.

- Total parts = 5
- First part = 3/5 × 600 = **₹360**
- Second part = 2/5 × 600 = **₹240** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Combined Ratio)

**Problem:** A:B = 2:3, B:C = 4:5. Find A:B:C.

- Make B common: B is 3 in first, 4 in second
- Multiply first ratio by 4, second by 3:
  - A:B = 8:12, B:C = 12:15
- A:B:C = **8:12:15** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Three-part Ratio)

**Problem:** A, B, C invest ₹2000, ₹3000, ₹4000. Profit is ₹1800. What does B get?

- Ratio = 2:3:4
- B's share = 3/9 × 1800 = **₹600** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Ages in Ratio)

**Problem:** Ratio of ages of A and B is 3:5. After 5 years it becomes 2:3. Find current ages.

- Now: A = 3x, B = 5x
- After 5 years: (3x+5)/(5x+5) = 2/3
- 9x+15 = 10x+10 → x = 5
- A = **15 years**, B = **25 years** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

**For dividing in ratio a:b:**
- Total parts = a + b
- Share = (a or b / total) × whole

---

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. Divide ₹900 in ratio 4:5.

2. A:B = 3:4, B:C = 5:6. Find A:B:C.

3. Ages of A and B are in ratio 5:3. Sum of ages = 56. Find ages.

4. A, B, C share profit in 3:5:2. Total profit = ₹5000. Find B's share.

5. Ratio of A:B = 2:3 and B:C = 6:7. Find A:C.

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. A = 4/9 × 900 = **₹400**, B = 5/9 × 900 = **₹500**
2. B=4 in first, 5 in second. Multiply: A:B=15:20, B:C=20:24 → **15:20:24**
3. A = 5/8 × 56 = **35**, B = 3/8 × 56 = **21**
4. B = 5/10 × 5000 = **₹2500**
5. A:B=2:3, B:C=6:7. B=3 and 6 → multiply first by 2: A:B=4:6. A:C = **4:7**
