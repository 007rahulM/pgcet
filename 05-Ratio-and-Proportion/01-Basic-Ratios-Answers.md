# Ratio & Proportion: Basic Ratios — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** Divide ₹900 in ratio 4:5.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Total amount = ₹900, ratio = 4:5
- Find: Share of each part

**💡 What I'll use:** Share = (Part / Total parts) × Whole

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Total parts = 4 + 5 = 9

Step 2: Calculate each share
A's share = (4/9) × 900 = **₹400**
B's share = (5/9) × 900 = **₹500**

**✅ Answer: ₹400 and ₹500**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** A:B = 3:4, B:C = 5:6. Find A:B:C.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A:B = 3:4 and B:C = 5:6 (B has different values in each ratio)
- Find: Combined ratio A:B:C

**💡 What I'll use:** Make B's value common — find LCM of 4 and 5, then scale both ratios

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: B = 4 in first ratio, B = 5 in second ratio
LCM(4, 5) = 20

Step 2: Scale both ratios so B = 20
A:B = 3:4 → multiply by 5 → **15:20**
B:C = 5:6 → multiply by 4 → **20:24**

Step 3: Combine
A:B:C = **15:20:24**

**✅ Answer: 15:20:24**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** Ages of A and B are in ratio 5:3. Sum of ages = 56. Find ages.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Age ratio A:B = 5:3, A + B = 56
- Find: Individual ages

**💡 What I'll use:** Total parts = 5 + 3 = 8; each part = 56/8

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find value of one part
One part = 56 / 8 = 7

Step 2: Calculate ages
A = 5 × 7 = **35 years**
B = 3 × 7 = **21 years**

**✅ Answer: A = 35 years, B = 21 years**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** A, B, C share profit in 3:5:2. Total profit = ₹5000. Find B's share.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Profit ratio = 3:5:2, total profit = ₹5000
- Find: B's share

**💡 What I'll use:** B's share = (B's parts / Total parts) × Total profit

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Total parts = 3 + 5 + 2 = 10

Step 2: B's share
B = (5/10) × 5000 = **₹2500**

**✅ Answer: B's share = ₹2500**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** Ratio of A:B = 2:3 and B:C = 6:7. Find A:C.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A:B = 2:3 and B:C = 6:7
- Find: The ratio A:C

**💡 What I'll use:** Make B common; B = 3 in first ratio, 6 in second. Multiply first by 2.

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Scale A:B so B matches
A:B = 2:3 → multiply by 2 → A:B = 4:6
B:C = 6:7 (B = 6 already matches)

Step 2: Extract A:C from the combined chain
A:B:C = 4:6:7

A:C = **4:7**

**✅ Answer: A:C = 4:7**
