# Direct and Inverse Variation — Exam-Type Problems

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for:
- "varies directly with"
- "varies inversely with"
- "if x increases, what happens to y?"
- unit-rate questions like weight–volume, workers–days, speed–time

---

## 📐 Core Formulas

### Direct Variation
> If `x ∝ y`, then `x/y = constant`
> or `x₁/y₁ = x₂/y₂`

### Inverse Variation
> If `x ∝ 1/y`, then `xy = constant`
> or `x₁y₁ = x₂y₂`

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**❓ Question:** The weight of a liquid varies directly with its volume. If 12 kg has a volume of 3 litres, what will be the weight of 4 litres?

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Direct variation means weight/volume is constant
- 12/3 = 4 kg per litre

Step 2: For 4 litres
- Weight = 4 × 4 = **16 kg**

**✅ Answer: 16 kg**

---

### Example 2

**❓ Question:** 8 pens cost ₹120. What will 15 pens cost at the same rate?

**✏️ My Solution:**
- Cost per pen = 120/8 = 15
- Cost of 15 pens = 15 × 15 = **₹225**

**✅ Answer: ₹225**

---

### Example 3

**❓ Question:** 5 workers can finish a job in 12 days. In how many days will 8 workers finish it?

**✏️ My Solution:**
- Inverse variation: workers × days = constant
- 5 × 12 = 8 × x
- x = 60/8 = **7.5 days**

**✅ Answer: 7.5 days**

---

### Example 4

**❓ Question:** A car travels 180 km in 3 hours at constant speed. How much distance will it cover in 5 hours?

**✏️ My Solution:**
- Direct variation: distance ∝ time
- Speed = 180/3 = 60 km/h
- Distance in 5 hours = 60 × 5 = **300 km**

**✅ Answer: 300 km**

---

### Example 5

**❓ Question:** If 12 men can build a wall in 15 days, how many men are needed to build it in 9 days?

**✏️ My Solution:**
- Inverse variation: men × days = constant
- 12 × 15 = x × 9
- x = 180/9 = **20 men**

**✅ Answer: 20 men**

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

- Direct variation → find the **unit rate** first
- Inverse variation → multiply the two linked quantities and keep product constant
- If wording says **"varies directly"**, think **divide**
- If wording says **"varies inversely"**, think **multiply**
