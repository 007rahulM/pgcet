### Q1

**❓ Question:** A bicycle covers 60 km in 3 hours. Find the speed.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Distance = 60 km, Time = 3 hours
- Find: Speed

**💡 What I'll use:** Speed = Distance ÷ Time

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Apply the formula directly.
Step 2: Speed = 60 ÷ 3 = **20 km/h**

**✅ Answer: 20 km/h**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** A train runs at 80 km/h. How far does it go in 2.5 hours?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Speed = 80 km/h, Time = 2.5 hours
- Find: Distance

**💡 What I'll use:** Distance = Speed × Time

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Distance = 80 × 2.5
Step 2: Distance = **200 km**

**✅ Answer: 200 km**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** Convert 36 km/h to m/s.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Speed = 36 km/h
- Find: Speed in m/s

**💡 What I'll use:** Multiply by 5/18 to convert km/h → m/s

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: 1 km = 1000 m, 1 hour = 3600 s → 1 km/h = 1000/3600 = 5/18 m/s
Step 2: 36 × 5/18 = 180/18 = **10 m/s**

**✅ Answer: 10 m/s**

### Q4

- Given: Speed = 25 m/s

### Q5

**❓ Question:** A person covers 500 m in 100 seconds. Find speed in km/h.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Distance = 500 m, Time = 100 seconds
- Find: Speed in km/h

**💡 What I'll use:** Speed = Distance/Time (in m/s), then convert to km/h

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Speed = 500 ÷ 100 = 5 m/s
Step 2: Convert to km/h: 5 × 18/5 = **18 km/h**

**✅ Answer: 18 km/h**

### Q6

**❓ Question:** A car travels at 60 km/h for 1.5 hours, then at 40 km/h for 2 hours. Total distance?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Two legs of a journey with different speeds and times
- Find: Total distance

**💡 What I'll use:** Total Distance = D₁ + D₂ = (S₁ × T₁) + (S₂ × T₂)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Distance in first leg = 60 × 1.5 = 90 km
Step 2: Distance in second leg = 40 × 2 = 80 km
Step 3: Total = 90 + 80 = **170 km**

**✅ Answer: 170 km**

### Q7

**❓ Question:** A train covers a distance in 5 hours at 60 km/h. At what speed should it travel to cover the same distance in 4 hours?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Original speed = 60 km/h, original time = 5 hrs; new time = 4 hrs
- Find: New speed

**💡 What I'll use:** First find the distance, then apply Speed = Distance/Time

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Distance = 60 × 5 = 300 km
Step 2: New speed = 300 ÷ 4 = **75 km/h**

**✅ Answer: 75 km/h**

### Q8

**❓ Question:** A person walks at 4 km/h and reaches 10 minutes late. Had he walked at 5 km/h, he'd be on time. Find the distance.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Speed₁ = 4 km/h (10 min late), Speed₂ = 5 km/h (on time)
- Find: Distance to destination

**💡 What I'll use:** Time difference = D/S₁ − D/S₂ = late time (in hours)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Convert 10 minutes to hours → 10/60 = 1/6 hour
Step 2: Set up equation: D/4 − D/5 = 1/6
Step 3: Common denominator for left side: (5D − 4D)/20 = 1/6 → D/20 = 1/6
Step 4: D = 20/6 = **10/3 ≈ 3.33 km**

**✅ Answer: 10/3 km ≈ 3.33 km**
