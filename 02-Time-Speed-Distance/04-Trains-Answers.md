### Q1

**❓ Question:** A 120 m long train crosses a pole in 6 seconds. Find its speed in km/h.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Train length = 120 m, time to cross pole = 6 s
- Find: Speed in km/h

**💡 What I'll use:** Crossing a pole → distance = train length; Speed = Length / Time, then convert m/s → km/h

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Speed = 120 / 6 = 20 m/s
Step 2: Convert to km/h: 20 × 18/5 = **72 km/h**

**✅ Answer: 72 km/h**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** A 200 m long train running at 72 km/h crosses a 300 m bridge. How long does it take?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Train length = 200 m, bridge length = 300 m, speed = 72 km/h
- Find: Time to cross the bridge

**💡 What I'll use:** Crossing a bridge → Distance = Train length + Bridge length; Time = Distance / Speed

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Convert speed: 72 × 5/18 = 20 m/s
Step 2: Total distance = 200 + 300 = 500 m
Step 3: Time = 500 / 20 = **25 seconds**

**✅ Answer: 25 seconds**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** A 150 m long train running at 54 km/h crosses a platform in 20 seconds. Find the platform length.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Train = 150 m, speed = 54 km/h, time = 20 s
- Find: Platform length

**💡 What I'll use:** Distance covered = Train + Platform; Platform = Distance − Train length

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Convert speed: 54 × 5/18 = 15 m/s
Step 2: Total distance covered = 15 × 20 = 300 m
Step 3: Platform length = 300 − 150 = **150 m**

**✅ Answer: 150 m**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** Two trains each 100 m long travel in opposite directions at 50 km/h and 40 km/h. How long do they take to cross each other?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Two trains, each 100 m; opposite directions at 50 and 40 km/h
- Find: Time to completely cross each other

**💡 What I'll use:** Opposite directions → Relative Speed = S₁ + S₂; Distance = sum of lengths

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Relative Speed = 50 + 40 = 90 km/h = 90 × 5/18 = 25 m/s
Step 2: Total distance = 100 + 100 = 200 m
Step 3: Time = 200 / 25 = **8 seconds**

**✅ Answer: 8 seconds**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** A 300 m train overtakes a 200 m train travelling in the same direction at 60 km/h vs 48 km/h. How long does it take?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Faster train = 300 m at 60 km/h; slower = 200 m at 48 km/h; same direction
- Find: Time for the faster train to completely overtake

**💡 What I'll use:** Same direction → Relative Speed = S₁ − S₂; Distance = sum of both lengths

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Relative Speed = 60 − 48 = 12 km/h = 12 × 5/18 = 10/3 m/s
Step 2: Total distance = 300 + 200 = 500 m
Step 3: Time = 500 ÷ (10/3) = 500 × 3/10 = **150 seconds**

**✅ Answer: 150 seconds**

### Q6

**❓ Question:** A 300 m train crosses a man walking at 6 km/h in the same direction in 30 seconds. Find the train's speed.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Train length = 300 m, man speed = 6 km/h (same direction), crossing time = 30 s
- Find: Train speed

**💡 What I'll use:** Relative Speed = Train speed − Man speed = Distance / Time; then solve for train speed

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Relative Speed = 300 / 30 = 10 m/s
Step 2: Man's speed = 6 × 5/18 = 5/3 m/s
Step 3: Train speed = Relative Speed + Man speed = 10 + 5/3 = 30/3 + 5/3 = 35/3 m/s
Step 4: Convert to km/h: (35/3) × 18/5 = 630/15 = **42 km/h**

**✅ Answer: 42 km/h**

### Q8

**❓ Question:** Two trains start from P and Q respectively; the first is 160 m long at 75 km/h, the second 200 m long at 50 km/h travelling toward each other. How long do they take to completely cross each other?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Train 1 = 160 m at 75 km/h; Train 2 = 200 m at 50 km/h; moving toward each other
- Find: Time to completely cross each other

**💡 What I'll use:** Opposite directions → Relative Speed = S₁ + S₂; Distance = sum of lengths

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Relative Speed = 75 + 50 = 125 km/h = 125 × 5/18 = 625/18 m/s
Step 2: Total distance = 160 + 200 = 360 m
Step 3: Time = 360 ÷ (625/18) = 360 × 18/625 = 6480/625 ≈ **10.37 seconds**

**✅ Answer: ≈ 10.37 seconds**
