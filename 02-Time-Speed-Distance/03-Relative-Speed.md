# Relative Speed — Two Objects Moving

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for:
- "Two cars/trains moving toward each other..."
- "Two people walking in the same direction..."
- "They start at the same time and meet after..."

---

## 🧠 The Concept (Why This Works)

Imagine you're on a train. Another train passes you:
- If it goes the **same way**, it feels slow (you subtract speeds)
- If it comes **toward you**, it feels fast (you add speeds)

---

## 📐 The Two Rules

> **Same direction:** Relative Speed = |Speed1 − Speed2|

> **Opposite direction:** Relative Speed = Speed1 + Speed2

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Meeting — opposite direction)

**Problem:** Two trains A and B start from cities 300 km apart, moving toward each other at 60 and 90 km/h. When do they meet?

**Solution:**
- Relative speed = 60 + 90 = 150 km/h (opposite)
- Time = 300 ÷ 150 = **2 hours** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Catching up — same direction)

**Problem:** A thief runs at 10 km/h. Police starts 5 minutes later at 15 km/h. How long for police to catch thief?

**Step 1 — Head start:**
- In 5 min = 5/60 hr, thief covers = 10 × 5/60 = 5/6 km

**Step 2 — Relative speed:**
- Same direction: 15 − 10 = 5 km/h

**Step 3 — Time:**
- (5/6) ÷ 5 = 1/6 hour = **10 minutes** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Meeting point)

**Problem:** A and B walk toward each other from 12 km apart. A walks at 4 km/h, B at 2 km/h. When and where do they meet?

**Step 1:** Relative speed = 4 + 2 = 6 km/h

**Step 2:** Time = 12 ÷ 6 = **2 hours**

**Step 3:** Distance covered by A = 4 × 2 = 8 km from A's start ✅

---

### Example 4 (Same direction, when does faster one lap the slower)

**Problem:** A and B run on a 400m track. A at 8 m/s, B at 6 m/s. Same direction. When does A lap B?

**Solution:**
- Relative speed = 8 − 6 = 2 m/s
- A gains 400m → Time = 400 ÷ 2 = **200 seconds** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

| Situation | Relative Speed |
|-----------|---------------|
| Moving toward each other | Add speeds |
| Moving away from each other | Add speeds |
| Same direction | Subtract speeds |

**Meeting time = Gap ÷ Relative Speed**

---

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. Two cars from cities 450 km apart move toward each other at 70 and 80 km/h. When do they meet?

2. A dog chases a cat. Dog runs at 12 m/s, cat at 8 m/s. Cat has a 20m head start. How long for dog to catch cat?

3. A and B start from the same point and walk in opposite directions at 5 and 3 km/h. How far apart after 4 hours?

4. A and B walk in same direction at 6 and 4 km/h. A is 10 km behind B. When does A catch B?

5. Two trains moving opposite directions at 40 and 60 km/h pass each other in 9 seconds. Sum of their lengths?

6. A starts at 6 AM at 40 km/h. B starts at 8 AM at 60 km/h from same place in same direction. When does B catch A?

7. Two people start from P and Q (120 km apart) at the same time toward each other. A walks at 5 km/h, B at 7 km/h. How far from P do they meet?

8. A thief escapes in a car at 60 km/h. Police car starts 10 min later at 75 km/h. When does police catch the thief?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. 450/150 = **3 hours**
2. 20 ÷ (12−8) = 20/4 = **5 seconds**
3. Total = (5+3)×4 = **32 km**
4. 10 ÷ (6−4) = **5 hours**
5. Length = Relative speed × time = (40+60)×5/18 × 9 = 100×5/18×9 = 250 m
6. A's head start = 2 hrs × 40 = 80 km. Relative speed = 60−40 = 20 km/h. Time = 80/20 = 4 hrs after 8 AM = **12 PM**
7. Time = 120/12 = 10 hrs. A covers 5×10 = **50 km** from P
8. Head start = 10/60 × 60 = 10 km. Relative speed = 15 km/h. Time = 10/15 = 2/3 hr = **40 minutes after police start**
