# Relative Speed — Two Objects Moving

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for:
- "Two cars/trains moving toward each other..."
- "Two people walking in the same direction..."
- "They start at the same time and meet after..."

---

## 🧠 The Concept (Why This Works)

Imagine you're on a train. Another train passes you:
- If it goes the **same way**, it feels slow (you subtract speeds)
- If it comes **toward you**, it feels fast (you add speeds)

---

## 📐 The Two Rules

> **Same direction:** Relative Speed = |Speed1 − Speed2|

> **Opposite direction:** Relative Speed = Speed1 + Speed2

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1

**❓ Question:** Two trains A and B start from cities 300 km apart, moving toward each other at 60 km/h and 90 km/h. When do they meet?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Two trains moving toward each other, 300 km apart; speeds are 60 km/h and 90 km/h
- Find: Time until they meet

**💡 What I'll use:** Opposite direction relative speed = sum of speeds; Time = Gap ÷ Relative Speed

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Relative speed = 60 + 90 = 150 km/h (moving toward each other)

Step 2: Time = 300 ÷ 150 = 2 hours

**✅ Answer: 2 hours**

---

### Example 2

**❓ Question:** A thief runs at 10 km/h. Police start chasing 5 minutes later at 15 km/h. How long does it take the police to catch the thief?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Thief runs at 10 km/h; police start 5 minutes late and run at 15 km/h; both going same direction
- Find: Time for police to catch up (from when police start)

**💡 What I'll use:** Find thief's head start distance, then use same-direction relative speed to find catch-up time

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Head start — in 5 min = 5/60 hour, thief covers = 10 × 5/60 = 5/6 km

Step 2: Relative speed (same direction) = 15 − 10 = 5 km/h

Step 3: Time = (5/6) ÷ 5 = 1/6 hour = 10 minutes

**✅ Answer: 10 minutes after police start**

---

### Example 3

**❓ Question:** A and B walk toward each other from 12 km apart. A walks at 4 km/h, B at 2 km/h. When do they meet, and how far from A's starting point?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A and B are 12 km apart, walking toward each other; A at 4 km/h, B at 2 km/h
- Find: Time to meet, and A's distance from their starting point

**💡 What I'll use:** Opposite direction relative speed = sum of speeds; then Distance = Speed × Time

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Relative speed = 4 + 2 = 6 km/h

Step 2: Time to meet = 12 ÷ 6 = 2 hours

Step 3: Distance covered by A = 4 × 2 = 8 km from A's start

**✅ Answer: They meet after 2 hours, 8 km from A's starting point**

---

### Example 4

**❓ Question:** A and B run on a 400 m circular track. A runs at 8 m/s, B at 6 m/s in the same direction. When does A lap B (complete one full lap ahead)?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 400 m track; A runs at 8 m/s, B at 6 m/s in the same direction
- Find: Time for A to gain a full lap (400 m) on B

**💡 What I'll use:** Same direction relative speed = difference of speeds; Time = Gap ÷ Relative Speed

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Relative speed = 8 − 6 = 2 m/s

Step 2: A needs to gain 400 m on B → Time = 400 ÷ 2 = 200 seconds

**✅ Answer: 200 seconds**

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

| Situation | Relative Speed |
|-----------|---------------|
| Moving toward each other | Add speeds |
| Moving away from each other | Add speeds |
| Same direction | Subtract speeds |

**Meeting time = Gap ÷ Relative Speed**

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. Two cars from cities 450 km apart move toward each other at 70 and 80 km/h. When do they meet?

2. A dog chases a cat. Dog runs at 12 m/s, cat at 8 m/s. Cat has a 20m head start. How long for dog to catch cat?

3. A and B start from the same point and walk in opposite directions at 5 and 3 km/h. How far apart after 4 hours?

4. A and B walk in same direction at 6 and 4 km/h. A is 10 km behind B. When does A catch B?

5. Two trains moving opposite directions at 40 and 60 km/h pass each other in 9 seconds. Sum of their lengths?

6. A starts at 6 AM at 40 km/h. B starts at 8 AM at 60 km/h from same place in same direction. When does B catch A?

7. Two people start from P and Q (120 km apart) at the same time toward each other. A walks at 5 km/h, B at 7 km/h. How far from P do they meet?

8. A thief escapes in a car at 60 km/h. Police car starts 10 min later at 75 km/h. When does police catch the thief?
