# Average Speed — When You Travel at Different Speeds

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for:
- "Goes from A to B at speed X, returns at speed Y. Average speed?"
- "Travels first half at 40 km/h, second half at 60 km/h. Average speed?"

---

## 🧠 The Concept (Why NOT Simple Average)

**Common Mistake:** Students add speeds and divide by 2.

Example: 40 + 60 / 2 = 50 km/h ← **WRONG!**

**Why?** You spend MORE time at the slower speed. So the average speed is always **less than** the simple average.

---

## 📐 The Formula

### Equal Distances (same distance both ways):
> **Average Speed = 2ab / (a + b)**

Where a and b are the two speeds.

### Unequal Distances:
> **Average Speed = Total Distance / Total Time**

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Equal distances — use formula)

**Problem:** A person goes from A to B at 40 km/h and returns at 60 km/h. Average speed?

**Solution:**
- Average = 2 × 40 × 60 / (40 + 60)
- = 4800 / 100
- = **48 km/h** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Three equal distances)

**Problem:** A travels 3 equal distances at 20, 30, 40 km/h. Average speed?

**Formula for 3 equal distances:**
> Average = 3abc / (ab + bc + ca)

- = 3 × 20 × 30 × 40 / (20×30 + 30×40 + 40×20)
- = 72000 / (600 + 1200 + 800)
- = 72000 / 2600
- = **27.69 km/h** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Unequal distances — use Total D / Total T)

**Problem:** A goes 60 km at 30 km/h and 90 km at 45 km/h. Average speed for the whole journey?

**Step 1 — Total Distance:**
- 60 + 90 = 150 km

**Step 2 — Total Time:**
- Time for 60 km = 60/30 = 2 hours
- Time for 90 km = 90/45 = 2 hours
- Total = 4 hours

**Step 3 — Average Speed:**
- 150 ÷ 4 = **37.5 km/h** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Same time, different speeds)

**Problem:** A spends 2 hours at 30 km/h and 2 hours at 50 km/h. Average speed?

**Note:** Same time → use **simple average!**
- Average = (30 + 50) / 2 = **40 km/h** ✅

*(Simple average only works when TIME is equal, not distance!)*

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

| Condition | Formula |
|-----------|---------|
| Same DISTANCE both ways | 2ab/(a+b) |
| Same TIME for each part | (a+b)/2 |
| Different distances and times | Total Distance / Total Time |

---

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. A goes from X to Y at 30 km/h and returns at 20 km/h. Average speed?

2. A car travels first half of a journey at 60 km/h and second half at 40 km/h. Average speed?

3. A bike covers 100 km at 50 km/h and next 100 km at 25 km/h. Average speed?

4. A person travels for 3 hours at 60 km/h and 2 hours at 90 km/h. Average speed?

5. A goes A to B at 50 km/h (takes 3 hours). Returns at 30 km/h. Average speed for whole trip?

6. A train covers first 60 km at 30 km/h, next 40 km at 20 km/h. Average speed?

7. A cyclist rides at 12 km/h for 2 hours, then 18 km/h for 3 hours. Average speed?

8. Going speed is 3x and return speed is 2x. Average speed (equal distances)?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. 2×30×20/(30+20) = 1200/50 = **24 km/h**
2. 2×60×40/(60+40) = 4800/100 = **48 km/h**
3. 2×50×25/(50+25) = 2500/75 = **33.33 km/h**
4. Total D = 180+180 = 360 km, Total T = 5 hrs → **72 km/h**
5. D = 50×3 = 150 km each way. Return time = 150/30 = 5 hrs. Avg = 300/8 = **37.5 km/h**
6. Total D = 100 km, Total T = 60/30 + 40/20 = 2+2 = 4 hrs → **25 km/h**
7. Total D = 24+54 = 78 km, T = 5 hrs → **15.6 km/h**
8. 2×3x×2x/(3x+2x) = 12x²/5x = **12x/5**
