### Q1

**❓ Question:** Two cars start from cities 450 km apart and travel toward each other at 70 km/h and 80 km/h. When do they meet?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Distance = 450 km, speeds moving toward each other = 70 and 80 km/h
- Find: Time to meet

**💡 What I'll use:** Opposite directions → Relative Speed = S₁ + S₂; Time = Distance / Relative Speed

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Relative Speed = 70 + 80 = 150 km/h
Step 2: Time = 450 / 150 = **3 hours**

**✅ Answer: 3 hours**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** A dog runs at 12 m/s and a cat at 8 m/s. The cat has a 20 m head start. How long does the dog take to catch the cat?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Dog speed = 12 m/s, Cat speed = 8 m/s, Head start = 20 m
- Find: Time for dog to catch cat

**💡 What I'll use:** Same direction → Relative Speed = S_dog − S_cat; Time = Head Start / Relative Speed

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Relative Speed = 12 − 8 = 4 m/s
Step 2: Time = 20 / 4 = **5 seconds**

**✅ Answer: 5 seconds**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** A and B start walking in opposite directions at 5 km/h and 3 km/h. How far apart are they after 4 hours?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Speeds = 5 and 3 km/h in opposite directions, Time = 4 hrs
- Find: Distance between them

**💡 What I'll use:** Opposite directions → separation rate = S₁ + S₂; Distance = Rate × Time

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Separation rate = 5 + 3 = 8 km/h
Step 2: Distance after 4 hrs = 8 × 4 = **32 km**

**✅ Answer: 32 km**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** A walks at 6 km/h and B at 4 km/h in the same direction. A is 10 km behind B. When does A catch up with B?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Same direction, A = 6 km/h, B = 4 km/h, A is 10 km behind
- Find: Time for A to catch B

**💡 What I'll use:** Same direction → Relative Speed = S_A − S_B; Time = Gap / Relative Speed

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Relative Speed = 6 − 4 = 2 km/h
Step 2: Time = 10 / 2 = **5 hours**

**✅ Answer: 5 hours**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** Two trains travelling in opposite directions at 40 km/h and 60 km/h completely pass each other in 9 seconds. Find the sum of their lengths.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Opposite directions, speeds = 40 and 60 km/h, crossing time = 9 s
- Find: Combined length of both trains

**💡 What I'll use:** Relative Speed (opposite) = S₁ + S₂; convert to m/s; Length = Speed × Time

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Relative Speed = 40 + 60 = 100 km/h
Step 2: Convert: 100 × 5/18 = 500/18 = 250/9 m/s
Step 3: Combined length = (250/9) × 9 = **250 m**

**✅ Answer: 250 m**

### Q6

**❓ Question:** A starts at 6 AM at 40 km/h. B starts at 8 AM at 60 km/h in the same direction. When does B catch A?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A starts 2 hrs earlier at 40 km/h; B starts at 8 AM at 60 km/h
- Find: Time when B catches A

**💡 What I'll use:** Head start distance = A's speed × head-start time; Catch-up time = Head start / Relative speed

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Head start of A when B departs = 40 × 2 = 80 km
Step 2: Relative Speed (same direction) = 60 − 40 = 20 km/h
Step 3: Time for B to close the gap = 80 / 20 = 4 hours after 8 AM
Step 4: B catches A at **12:00 noon**

**✅ Answer: 12:00 noon (4 hours after B starts)**

### Q7

**❓ Question:** P and Q are 120 km apart. A starts from P at 5 km/h and B starts from Q at 7 km/h toward each other. How far from P do they meet?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Distance PQ = 120 km, A from P at 5 km/h, B from Q at 7 km/h toward each other
- Find: Meeting point's distance from P

**💡 What I'll use:** Time to meet = Total Distance / Relative Speed; Distance from P = A's speed × Time

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Relative Speed = 5 + 7 = 12 km/h
Step 2: Time to meet = 120 / 12 = 10 hours
Step 3: Distance covered by A = 5 × 10 = **50 km from P**

**✅ Answer: 50 km from P**

### Q8

**❓ Question:** A thief runs at 60 km/h. Police start chasing 10 minutes later at 75 km/h. When do the police catch the thief?

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Thief at 60 km/h, Police at 75 km/h starting 10 min later
- Find: Time for police to catch thief (measured from police start)

**💡 What I'll use:** Head start = Thief speed × delay time; Catch-up time = Head start / Relative speed

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Head start = 60 × (10/60) = 10 km
Step 2: Relative Speed = 75 − 60 = 15 km/h
Step 3: Catch-up time = 10 / 15 = 2/3 hour = **40 minutes after police start**

**✅ Answer: 40 minutes after the police start**
