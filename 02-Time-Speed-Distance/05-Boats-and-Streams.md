# Boats & Streams — Upstream and Downstream

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for:
- "A boat travels downstream at X km/h, upstream at Y km/h"
- "Boat speed in still water is X, stream speed is Y"
- "A man rows with/against the current"

---

## 🧠 The Concept (Why This Works)

Imagine a boat in a river:
- **Downstream** (going with current): current helps → add stream speed
- **Upstream** (going against current): current resists → subtract stream speed

---

## 📐 Key Formulas

| Direction | Speed |
|-----------|-------|
| Downstream | Boat speed + Stream speed |
| Upstream | Boat speed − Stream speed |

### Finding Boat and Stream Speed:
> **Boat speed = (Downstream + Upstream) / 2**
> **Stream speed = (Downstream − Upstream) / 2**

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Basic downstream/upstream)

**Problem:** A boat's speed in still water is 12 km/h. Stream speed is 4 km/h.
- Downstream speed?
- Upstream speed?

**Solution:**
- Downstream = 12 + 4 = **16 km/h**
- Upstream = 12 − 4 = **8 km/h** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Find boat and stream speed)

**Problem:** A boat goes 30 km downstream in 2 hours and 20 km upstream in 4 hours. Find boat speed and stream speed.

**Step 1:**
- Downstream speed = 30/2 = 15 km/h
- Upstream speed = 20/4 = 5 km/h

**Step 2:**
- Boat speed = (15 + 5) / 2 = **10 km/h**
- Stream speed = (15 − 5) / 2 = **5 km/h** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Time to travel same distance)

**Problem:** Boat speed = 10 km/h, Stream = 2 km/h. How long to travel 24 km upstream?

**Solution:**
- Upstream speed = 10 − 2 = 8 km/h
- Time = 24 ÷ 8 = **3 hours** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Round trip)

**Problem:** Boat travels 20 km downstream then returns upstream. Boat speed = 10 km/h, stream = 2 km/h. Total time?

**Step 1:**
- Downstream = 10 + 2 = 12 km/h → Time = 20/12 = 5/3 hours
- Upstream = 10 − 2 = 8 km/h → Time = 20/8 = 5/2 hours

**Step 2:** Total = 5/3 + 5/2 = 10/6 + 15/6 = 25/6 ≈ **4.17 hours** ✅

---

### Example 5 (From KEA-style paper)

**Problem:** A man rows 9 km/h in still water. River flows at 3 km/h. How long to row 18 km and return?

- Downstream = 12 km/h → 18/12 = 1.5 hrs
- Upstream = 6 km/h → 18/6 = 3 hrs
- Total = **4.5 hours** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

```
D = Downstream speed, U = Upstream speed

Boat speed = (D + U) / 2
Stream speed = (D - U) / 2

Time = Distance ÷ speed
```

Just remember: **"D + U for Boat, D − U for Stream, divide both by 2"**

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. Boat speed = 15 km/h, stream = 3 km/h. Downstream and upstream speeds?

2. A boat goes 24 km downstream in 3 hours and 12 km upstream in 3 hours. Find boat speed and stream speed.

3. Boat speed = 8 km/h, stream = 2 km/h. Time to travel 30 km upstream?

4. A man rows 16 km downstream in 2 hours and returns upstream in 4 hours. Stream speed?

5. Boat goes 40 km downstream and 20 km upstream in 8 hours. Downstream speed = 2 × upstream speed. Find both speeds.

6. A boat's downstream speed is 18 km/h and upstream is 10 km/h. Boat speed in still water?

7. Stream flows at 4 km/h. Boat goes 32 km upstream in 8 hours. Boat speed in still water?

8. A man can row 8 km in 2 hours with the current and 4 km in 2 hours against it. Find the stream speed.

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. Downstream = 18 km/h, Upstream = 12 km/h
2. D = 8, U = 4. Boat = 6 km/h, Stream = 2 km/h
3. Upstream = 6 km/h. Time = 30/6 = 5 hours
4. D = 8 km/h, U = 4 km/h. Stream = (8-4)/2 = 2 km/h
5. U = x, D = 2x. 40/2x + 20/x = 8 → 20/x + 20/x = 8 → x = 5. U=5, D=10 km/h
6. (18+10)/2 = 14 km/h
7. Upstream = 32/8 = 4 km/h. Boat = 4+4 = 8 km/h
8. D = 4 km/h, U = 2 km/h. Stream = (4-2)/2 = 1 km/h
