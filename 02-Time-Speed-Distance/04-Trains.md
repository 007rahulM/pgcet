# Trains — Crossing Poles, Platforms & Other Trains

## 🔍 How to Recognize This Pattern

Look for:
- "A train crosses a **pole/signal/person** in X seconds"
- "A train crosses a **platform/bridge** in X seconds"
- "Two trains cross each other in X seconds"

---

## 🧠 The Concept (Why Length Matters)

When a train crosses a pole:
- The train must travel its own length past the pole
- Distance = **Length of train**

When a train crosses a platform:
- The engine enters, then the whole train must clear the platform
- Distance = **Length of train + Length of platform**

When two trains cross each other:
- Both trains must fully pass each other
- Distance = **Sum of both lengths**

---

## 📐 Key Formulas

| Situation | Distance | Speed |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Cross a pole/person | Train length | Train speed |
| Cross a platform/bridge | Train length + Platform length | Train speed |
| Two trains opposite direction | Sum of lengths | Sum of speeds |
| Two trains same direction | Sum of lengths | Difference of speeds |

---

## ⚠️ Unit Reminder

Convert speed to m/s when time is in seconds:
> km/h × 5/18 = m/s

---

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Crossing a pole)

**Problem:** A 150m train crosses a pole in 10 seconds. Find its speed in km/h.

**Solution:**
- Speed = 150 ÷ 10 = 15 m/s
- In km/h = 15 × 18/5 = **54 km/h** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Crossing a platform)

**Problem:** A 200m train crosses a 300m platform in 25 seconds. Find speed.

**Solution:**
- Total distance = 200 + 300 = 500 m
- Speed = 500 ÷ 25 = 20 m/s = 20 × 18/5 = **72 km/h** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Find platform length)

**Problem:** A train 180m long runs at 72 km/h. It crosses a platform in 20 seconds. Find platform length.

**Step 1:** 72 km/h = 72 × 5/18 = 20 m/s

**Step 2:** Distance in 20 sec = 20 × 20 = 400 m

**Step 3:** Platform length = 400 − 180 = **220 m** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Two trains opposite direction)

**Problem:** Two trains of lengths 120m and 180m approach each other at 60 and 90 km/h. Time to cross?

**Step 1:** Relative speed = 60 + 90 = 150 km/h = 150 × 5/18 = 125/3 m/s

**Step 2:** Total distance = 120 + 180 = 300 m

**Step 3:** Time = 300 ÷ (125/3) = 300 × 3/125 = **7.2 seconds** ✅

---

### Example 5 (Two trains same direction)

**Problem:** Train A (100m, 60 km/h) overtakes Train B (200m, 40 km/h). Time to overtake?

**Step 1:** Relative speed = 60 − 40 = 20 km/h = 20 × 5/18 = 50/9 m/s

**Step 2:** Total distance = 100 + 200 = 300 m

**Step 3:** Time = 300 ÷ (50/9) = 300 × 9/50 = **54 seconds** ✅

---

### Example 6 (Person standing on platform)

**Problem:** A train 250m long crosses a man standing on a platform in 10 seconds. Speed?

*(Man = point, so just train length)*
- Speed = 250 ÷ 10 = 25 m/s = 25 × 18/5 = **90 km/h** ✅

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Shortcut

```
Pole/person    → Distance = Train length only
Platform/bridge → Distance = Train + Platform
Two trains      → Distance = Sum of lengths
                  Speed = Add (opposite) or Subtract (same)

Time = Distance ÷ Speed
```

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. A 120m train crosses a pole in 6 seconds. Speed in km/h?

2. A train 200m long at 72 km/h crosses a bridge 300m long. Time taken?

3. A 150m train running at 54 km/h crosses a platform in 20 seconds. Platform length?

4. Two trains of 100m each run at 50 and 40 km/h in opposite directions. Time to cross each other?

5. A train 300m long overtakes another 200m long train going in the same direction. Speeds are 60 and 48 km/h. Time to overtake?

6. A train crosses a man walking at 6 km/h (same direction) in 30 sec. Train length 300m. Train speed?

7. A 250m train crosses a 350m platform at 72 km/h. Time taken?

8. Two trains start from P and Q toward each other. Train from P is 160m at 75 km/h, from Q is 200m at 50 km/h. Time to cross?

---

## ✔️ Answers

1. 120/6 = 20 m/s = 72 km/h
2. 500m ÷ 20 m/s = 25 seconds
3. Distance = 15 m/s × 20 = 300m. Platform = 300-150 = 150m
4. Relative speed = 90 km/h = 25 m/s. Distance = 200m. Time = 8 sec
5. Relative = 12 km/h = 10/3 m/s. Distance = 500m. Time = 150 sec
6. Relative speed = 300/30 = 10 m/s. Train speed = 10 + 6×5/18 = 10 + 5/3 ≈ 11.67 m/s = 42 km/h
7. 600m ÷ 20 m/s = 30 seconds
8. Relative = 125 km/h × 5/18 = 625/18 m/s. Distance = 360m. Time ≈ 10.37 sec
