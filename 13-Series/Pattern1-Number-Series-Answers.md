# Number Series — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** Find the missing term: 3, 8, 13, 18, **?**

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: The series 3, 8, 13, 18
- Find: The next term

**💡 What I'll use:** Arithmetic progression — check constant difference

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find differences between consecutive terms
- 8 − 3 = 5
- 13 − 8 = 5
- 18 − 13 = 5

Step 2: Pattern identified — add 5 each time

Step 3: Apply
- 18 + 5 = 23

**✅ Answer: 23**

### Q3

### Q5

**❓ Question:** Find the missing term: 7, 8, 18, 57, **?**

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: The series 7, 8, 18, 57
- Find: The next term

**💡 What I'll use:** Multiply-then-add pattern (×n + n where n increases)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Test ×n + n pattern
- 7 × 1 + 1 = 8 ✅ (n=1)
- 8 × 2 + 2 = 18 ✅ (n=2)
- 18 × 3 + 3 = 57 ✅ (n=3)

Step 2: Apply for n = 4
- 57 × 4 + 4 = 228 + 4 = 232

**✅ Answer: 232**

### Q6

**❓ Question:** Find the missing term: 1, 4, 10, 22, **?**

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: The series 1, 4, 10, 22
- Find: The next term

**💡 What I'll use:** Second-level differences — check if the differences themselves form a pattern

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Find first-level differences
- 4 − 1 = 3
- 10 − 4 = 6
- 22 − 10 = 12

Step 2: Find second-level differences (differences of differences)
- 6 ÷ 3 = 2 (doubling!)
- 12 ÷ 6 = 2 (doubling!)

Step 3: Next difference = 12 × 2 = 24

Step 4: Apply
- 22 + 24 = 46

**✅ Answer: 46**

### Q8

**❓ Question:** Find the missing term: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, **?** (Primes)

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: The series 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13
- Find: The next term

**💡 What I'll use:** Prime number sequence

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Confirm all terms are prime
- 2 ✅, 3 ✅, 5 ✅, 7 ✅, 11 ✅, 13 ✅

Step 2: Find the next prime after 13
- 14 = 2 × 7 (not prime)
- 15 = 3 × 5 (not prime)
- 16 = 2⁴ (not prime)
- 17 — check: not divisible by 2, 3 → **prime** ✅

**✅ Answer: 17**

### Q9

**❓ Question:** Find the missing term: 1, 2, 6, 24, 120, **?** (Factorials!)

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: The series 1, 2, 6, 24, 120
- Find: The next term

**💡 What I'll use:** Factorial pattern — n!

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Identify each term as a factorial
- 1 = 1!
- 2 = 2!
- 6 = 3!
- 24 = 4!
- 120 = 5!

Step 2: The next term is 6!
- 6! = 6 × 5! = 6 × 120 = 720

**✅ Answer: 720**

### Q10

**❓ Question:** Find the wrong term in: 3, 7, 15, 27, 63, 127

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: The series 3, 7, 15, 27, 63, 127, one of which is wrong
- Find: Which term is incorrect and what it should be

**💡 What I'll use:** Test the formula 2ⁿ − 1

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Test 2ⁿ − 1 starting from n = 2
- 2² − 1 = 3 ✅
- 2³ − 1 = 7 ✅
- 2⁴ − 1 = 15 ✅
- 2⁵ − 1 = 31 ✗ (series shows **27**)
- 2⁶ − 1 = 63 ✅
- 2⁷ − 1 = 127 ✅

Step 2: The 4th term should be 31, not 27

**✅ Answer: 27 is the wrong term — it should be 31**
