# Reading Comprehension and Error Detection — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** Find the error: "Neither he nor his friends is coming."

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Sentence with "Neither...nor"
- Find: Grammatical error

**💡 What I'll use:** Neither...nor rule — verb agrees with the subject CLOSEST to the verb

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: "Neither he nor his friends" — two subjects joined by "nor".

Step 2: The subject closest to the verb = "his friends" (plural).

Step 3: Plural subject needs plural verb → "are coming" not "is coming".

Step 4: Error: **"is"** → should be **"are"**

**✅ Answer: Error — "is" should be "are coming"**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** Choose correct sentence: (A) He doesn't knows the answer. (B) He doesn't know the answer. (C) He doesn't knowing the answer. (D) He don't know the answer.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 4 options — find the grammatically correct one
- Find: Correct subject-verb agreement and auxiliary verb usage

**💡 What I'll use:** Auxiliary verb rule — after "does/doesn't", use the base form (infinitive without "to")

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Eliminate wrong options:
- (A) "doesn't knows" — double verb marking ("doesn't" already marks 3rd person) ✗
- (C) "doesn't knowing" — wrong form after auxiliary ✗
- (D) "don't" — "he" is 3rd person singular, needs "doesn't" ✗

Step 2: (B) "doesn't know" — auxiliary "doesn't" + base form "know" ✓

**✅ Answer: (B) He doesn't know the answer**

### Q3

**❓ Question:** Find the error: "Between you and I, this is a mistake."

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A sentence with a pronoun after a preposition
- Find: The grammatical error

**💡 What I'll use:** Pronoun case rule — after a preposition, use OBJECT pronoun (me, him, her, us, them)

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: "Between" is a preposition.

Step 2: After a preposition, use the object form of the pronoun: "I" is subjective, "me" is objective.

Step 3: "Between you and I" → should be "Between you and **me**"

Step 4: Error: **"I"** → should be **"me"**

**✅ Answer: Error — "I" should be "me" → "Between you and me"**

### Q4

**❓ Question:** Choose the correct sentence: (A) The news are disturbing. (B) The news is disturbing. (C) News are disturbing. (D) A news is disturbing.

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: 4 options about "news"
- Find: Correct usage

**💡 What I'll use:** Uncountable noun rule — "news" is always singular and uncountable

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: "News" is an uncountable noun → always takes singular verb.

Step 2: Eliminate:
- (A) "news are" — wrong, singular verb needed ✗
- (C) No article before "news" when used as a specific subject ✗
- (D) "A news" — cannot use indefinite article with uncountable noun ✗

Step 3: (B) "The news is disturbing" ✓

**✅ Answer: (B) The news is disturbing**

### Q5

**❓ Question:** Fill in: "I have been working here ___ 2019."

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: "2019" = a specific year (point in time)
- Find: since or for?

**💡 What I'll use:** Since vs For — "since" with a specific point; "for" with a duration

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: 2019 is a specific year = a point in time.

Step 2: Specific time point → use **since**

**✅ Answer: since** — "I have been working here since 2019"
