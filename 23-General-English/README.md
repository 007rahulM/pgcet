# General English — Grammar, Vocabulary, Comprehension

## 🔑 Overview (10–15 Questions)

General English in MCA PGCET tests:
1. **Grammar** — tenses, articles, prepositions, subject-verb agreement
2. **Vocabulary** — synonyms, antonyms, one-word substitution
3. **Reading Comprehension** — 1 passage with 3–4 questions
4. **Sentence Correction** — find and fix errors
5. **Fill in the Blanks** — choose correct word

---

## 📐 Part 1: Grammar

### Tenses Quick Guide:

| Tense | When | Signal Words | Example |
|-------|------|-------------|---------|
| Simple Present | Habits, facts | always, usually, every day | I eat rice. |
| Present Continuous | Happening now | now, at present, currently | I am eating. |
| Present Perfect | Completed, has effect now | just, already, yet, since, for | I have eaten. |
| Simple Past | Completed action | yesterday, ago, last | I ate rice. |
| Past Continuous | Was happening at a time | while, when | I was eating. |
| Simple Future | Will happen | tomorrow, next | I will eat. |

### Articles (a, an, the):
- **a** — before consonant sounds: a book, a university (y-sound = consonant)
- **an** — before vowel sounds: an apple, an hour (h is silent)
- **the** — specific/known thing: the sun, the first boy
- **No article** — general/abstract: Love is blind. Water is essential.

**Tricky ones:**
- a university ✅ (u sounds like 'yoo' = consonant sound)
- an hour ✅ (h is silent, starts with vowel sound 'ow')
- the United States ✅ (unique entity)

### Prepositions:
| Preposition | Use | Example |
|-------------|-----|---------|
| at | specific time/place | at 5 PM, at school |
| in | enclosed space/periods | in the box, in July |
| on | surface/day | on the table, on Monday |
| since | from a point in time | since 2020 |
| for | duration | for 3 hours |
| by | deadline | by Friday |
| with | accompaniment/instrument | with a pen |
| to | direction/recipient | go to school |

### Subject-Verb Agreement:
- Singular subject → singular verb: **He runs.**
- Plural subject → plural verb: **They run.**
- Uncountable nouns → singular: **The water is clean.**
- Either/Neither + singular noun → singular: **Neither of them is correct.**
- Either/Neither of + plural → plural: **Neither of the students are ready.**

## 📐 Part 3: Common Errors to Avoid

### Error 1: Wrong Article
❌ I have a umbrella.
✅ I have **an** umbrella. (vowel sound)

### Error 2: Wrong Preposition
❌ She is angry on him.
✅ She is angry **with** him.

❌ I am married with her.
✅ I am married **to** her.

### Error 3: Double Negative
❌ I don't know nothing.
✅ I don't know **anything**.

### Error 4: Subject-Verb Agreement
❌ The team are playing well.
✅ The team **is** playing well. (collective noun = singular)

### Error 5: Misuse of Since/For
- **Since** + point in time: since Monday, since 1990
- **For** + duration: for 3 days, for two hours
❌ I have been here since two hours.
✅ I have been here **for** two hours.

## 📐 Part 4: Reading Comprehension Strategy

**60-second approach:**
1. Read the questions FIRST (before the passage)
2. Skim the passage for keywords related to questions
3. Read relevant sentences carefully
4. Choose the most directly supported answer (don't assume)

**Tips:**
- Answers are usually in the passage — don't use outside knowledge
- "Main idea" questions → look at topic sentence of each paragraph
- "The author implies..." → look for indirect statements
- Eliminate obviously wrong answers first

## 📝 Practice Problems

1. Choose the correct form: "She _____ to school every day."
   (A) go  (B) goes  (C) going  (D) gone

2. Fill in the blank with article: "I want to be ___ engineer."
   (A) a  (B) an  (C) the  (D) no article

3. Choose correct preposition: "I have been waiting ___ 3 hours."
   (A) since  (B) for  (C) from  (D) during

4. Synonym of "diligent":
   (A) Lazy  (B) Hardworking  (C) Confused  (D) Silent

5. Antonym of "hostile":
   (A) Unfriendly  (B) Dangerous  (C) Friendly  (D) Angry

6. Find the error: "Neither of the boys were present."
   (A) Neither  (B) of  (C) boys  (D) were

7. One-word substitution for "Fear of heights":
   (A) Hydrophobia  (B) Claustrophobia  (C) Acrophobia  (D) Xenophobia

8. Correct the sentence: "I don't know nothing about this."
   (A) I don't know anything  (B) I know nothing  
   Both (A) and (B) are correct  (D) Sentence is already correct

9. Choose correct tense: "By the time you arrive, I _____ the work."
   (A) finish  (B) finished  (C) will have finished  (D) am finishing

10. "Philatelist" means:
    (A) Coin collector  (B) Stamp collector  (C) Art collector  (D) Book collector

---

## ✔️ Answers with Full Explanation

1. **(B) goes**
   Explanation: Subject is "She" — third person singular. Simple present tense for habits requires third person singular verb → goes. "Go" is for I/you/we/they. "Going" needs "is". "Gone" is past participle.

2. **(B) an**
   Explanation: "Engineer" starts with a vowel sound 'e'. Use "an" before vowel sounds. "A" goes before consonant sounds. "An engineer" ✅.

3. **(B) for**
   Explanation: "For" is used with a DURATION of time (3 hours, 2 days, a year). "Since" is used with a specific POINT in time (since Monday, since 2020). "For 3 hours" is correct.

4. **(B) Hardworking**
   Explanation: "Diligent" means showing careful and persistent work/effort. Synonyms: hardworking, industrious, assiduous. Opposite (antonym): lazy, idle.

5. **(C) Friendly**
   Explanation: "Hostile" means unfriendly, antagonistic, showing opposition. Its antonym is "friendly" or "amicable". "Unfriendly" would be a synonym (same meaning), not antonym.

6. **(D) were**
   Explanation: "Neither" is singular. "Neither of the boys" takes a singular verb → "was" not "were". Rule: Neither/Either + of + noun → singular verb. Correct: "Neither of the boys **was** present."

7. **(C) Acrophobia**
   Explanation: Acro = height, phobia = fear → Acrophobia = fear of heights. Hydrophobia = fear of water. Claustrophobia = fear of closed spaces. Xenophobia = fear of foreigners.

8. **(Both A and B are correct)**
   Explanation: "I don't know nothing" has a double negative (don't + nothing), which is grammatically wrong. Both corrections are valid: "I don't know **anything**" (one negative) OR "I know **nothing**" (one negative).

9. **(C) will have finished**
   Explanation: "By the time" + future reference → Future Perfect tense. Future Perfect = will + have + past participle. It shows an action that will be completed before another future action.

10. **(B) Stamp collector**
    Explanation: A philatelist is someone who collects and studies stamps. "Phil" = love, "atel" = tax/stamp. Numismatist = coin collector. Bibliophile = book lover.
