## Solve Method (Use this in every question)

### A) Synonym question
1. Write a simple meaning of the given word.
2. Remove options with opposite meaning.
3. Remove options from unrelated context.
4. Pick closest meaning, not exact dictionary sentence.

### B) Antonym question
1. Confirm base meaning of the given word.
2. Remove words with same meaning (common trap).
3. Pick strongest opposite in context.

### C) One-word substitution
1. Identify key root in phrase (phobia, -cide, -ology, -phile, etc.).
2. Match root with option.
3. Reject close-but-wrong roots.

## Worked Examples

### Example 1 (Synonym)
**Q:** Synonym of "diligent":
(A) Lazy  (B) Hardworking  (C) Confused  (D) Silent

- "Diligent" = showing careful and persistent work/effort
- **Answer: (B) Hardworking** ✅

### Example 2 (Antonym)
**Q:** Antonym of "hostile":
(A) Unfriendly  (B) Dangerous  (C) Friendly  (D) Angry

- "Hostile" = unfriendly, antagonistic → antonym = friendly
- **Answer: (C) Friendly** ✅
- (Note: Unfriendly is a SYNONYM, not antonym!)

### Example 3 (One-word)
**Q:** "Fear of heights" is:
(A) Hydrophobia  (B) Claustrophobia  (C) Acrophobia  (D) Xenophobia

- Acro = height, phobia = fear → Acrophobia
- **Answer: (C) Acrophobia** ✅

### Example 4 (One-word)
**Q:** "Philatelist" means:
(A) Coin collector  (B) Stamp collector  (C) Art collector  (D) Book collector

- Phil = love, ately/atelic = stamps
- **Answer: (B) Stamp collector** ✅
