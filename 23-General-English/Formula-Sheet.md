## 📐 RULE BLOCK 1 — Fill in the Blank

### Question looks like:
- "She _____ to school every day." (goes / go / going)
- "He has been working _____ 5 PM." (since / for / from)
- "Neither John nor his friends _____ invited." (was / were)

→ Apply rules from blocks below depending on blank type.

## 📐 RULE BLOCK 2 — Subject-Verb Agreement

### ALL Rules:

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Singular subject → singular verb | "He **goes** to school." |
| Plural subject → plural verb | "They **go** to school." |
| "Neither...nor / Either...or" → verb agrees with nearer subject | "Neither he nor they **are** present." |
| "Each / Every / Everyone" → singular verb | "Each of them **is** ready." |
| Collective nouns (team, committee) → singular verb | "The team **has** decided." |
| Two singular subjects joined by "and" → plural verb | "Ram and Shyam **are** friends." |
| Uncountable nouns → singular verb | "The news **is** good." |

→ **See examples: [01-Grammar.md](./01-Grammar.md)**

## 📐 RULE BLOCK 3 — Tenses (Formulas)

### ALL Tense Structures:

| Tense | Positive | Negative | Question |
|-------|----------|---------|---------|
| Simple Present | V₁ / V₁s | do/does + not + V₁ | Do/Does + S + V₁? |
| Present Continuous | am/is/are + V-ing | — + not + V-ing | Am/Is/Are + S + V-ing? |
| Present Perfect | have/has + V₃ | have/has + not + V₃ | Have/Has + S + V₃? |
| Present Perfect Continuous | have/has + been + V-ing | — | — |
| Simple Past | V₂ | did + not + V₁ | Did + S + V₁? |
| Past Continuous | was/were + V-ing | — + not + V-ing | Was/Were + S + V-ing? |
| Past Perfect | had + V₃ | had not + V₃ | Had + S + V₃? |
| Simple Future | will/shall + V₁ | will not + V₁ | Will + S + V₁? |
| Future Perfect | will have + V₃ | — | — |

### Tense Keywords to Identify:
| Keyword | Tense |
|---------|-------|
| every day, always, usually | Simple Present |
| now, at this moment, currently | Present Continuous |
| just, already, yet, recently, ever | Present Perfect |
| yesterday, last, ago, in 1990 | Simple Past |
| when, while (background action) | Past Continuous |
| before, after, by the time (past) | Past Perfect |
| tomorrow, next, will | Future |

## 📐 RULE BLOCK 4 — Active and Passive Voice

### Conversion Formula:

**Active → Passive:**
> Object of Active → Subject of Passive
> Subject of Active → Object of Passive (with "by")
> V₂/V₃ → "is/are/was/were/has been/have been" + V₃

### ALL Tense Passive Forms:

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Simple Present | She **writes** a letter | A letter **is written** by her |
| Simple Past | She **wrote** a letter | A letter **was written** by her |
| Present Continuous | She **is writing** | A letter **is being written** |
| Past Continuous | She **was writing** | A letter **was being written** |
| Present Perfect | She **has written** | A letter **has been written** |
| Past Perfect | She **had written** | A letter **had been written** |
| Future (will) | She **will write** | A letter **will be written** |
| Modal (can, may, must) | She **can write** | A letter **can be written** |

→ **See examples: [02-Active-Passive.md](./02-Active-Passive.md)**

## 📐 RULE BLOCK 5 — Direct to Indirect Speech

### ALL Conversion Rules:

| Element | Direct → Indirect Change |
|---------|--------------------------|
| Reporting verb "said" | stays "said" (no object) |
| Reporting verb "said to X" | changes to "told X" |
| Present tense → | Past tense |
| Past tense → | Past perfect |
| Present perfect → | Past perfect |
| Future (will) → | would |
| "am/is" → | was |
| "are" → | were |
| This/these → | that/those |
| Here → | there |
| Now → | then |
| Today → | that day |
| Yesterday → | the previous day |
| Tomorrow → | the following day |
| Pronouns: I → | he/she; we → they |

### Special Cases:
| Statement Type | Reporting Verb | Conjunction |
|----------------|---------------|------------|
| Statement | said/told | that |
| Yes/No Question | asked | if/whether |
| Wh-Question | asked | same wh-word |
| Command/Request | ordered/requested/advised | to + V₁ |
| Exclamation | exclaimed | that |

→ **See examples: [03-Direct-Indirect.md](./03-Direct-Indirect.md)**

## 📐 RULE BLOCK 6 — Articles (a, an, the)

### ALL Rules:

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| "A" before consonant sound | a book, a university (starts with "yoo" sound) |
| "An" before vowel sound | an apple, an hour (h is silent) |
| "The" for specific / already known | "Pass me **the** book on the table." |
| "The" with superlatives | "**The** tallest man." |
| "The" with unique things | **the** sun, **the** moon |
| "The" with rivers, mountains, deserts | **the** Ganges, **the** Himalayas |
| No article for proper nouns | India (not "the India" unless comparing) |
| No article for general plural | "Dogs are loyal." |
| No article for languages, games, subjects | "I play **cricket**. She studies **physics**." |

→ **See examples: [04-Articles-and-Prepositions.md](./04-Articles-and-Prepositions.md)**

## 📐 RULE BLOCK 7 — Prepositions

### Most Tested Preposition Rules:

| Preposition | Used For | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **at** | point in time; specific place | at 5 PM; at the station |
| **in** | enclosed space; months/years | in the room; in 2025 |
| **on** | surface; days/dates | on the table; on Monday |
| **since** | specific point in past (with perfect tense) | since 2020; since morning |
| **for** | duration | for 3 years; for 2 hours |
| **by** | not later than; agent in passive | by 5 PM; done **by** her |
| **from...to** | range (place or time) | from Monday to Friday |
| **between** | two items | between A and B |
| **among** | more than two | among the students |
| **beside** | next to | beside the tree |
| **besides** | in addition to | besides this |

→ **See examples: [04-Articles-and-Prepositions.md](./04-Articles-and-Prepositions.md)**

## 📐 RULE BLOCK 8 — Vocabulary

### For Synonyms, Antonyms, One-Word Substitutions:
- **Always eliminate** clearly wrong choices first
- Look for **degree** differences (hot vs warm vs scalding)
- For one-word substitution: identify the **category** (person, place, action, fear, study of...)

### Common One-Word Substitutions (Exam Favorites):

| Phrase | One Word |
|--------|---------|
| Fear of water | Hydrophobia |
| One who walks in sleep | Somnambulism / Somnambulist |
| Words written on a tomb | Epitaph |
| Study of stars | Astronomy |
| Study of living organisms | Biology |
| One who knows everything | Omniscient |
| One who is present everywhere | Omnipresent |
| Words that cannot be read | Illegible |
| Murder of one's brother | Fratricide |
| Murder of one's father | Patricide |
| Murder of a king | Regicide |
| One who loves books | Bibliophile |
| Government by one person | Autocracy |
| Government by the people | Democracy |
| Speaks many languages | Polyglot |
| First speech in public | Maiden speech |

## 📐 RULE BLOCK 9 — Reading Comprehension

### Strategy:
1. **Skim** the passage first (30 seconds) for the main idea
2. **Read each question** before reading for the answer
3. **Don't use outside knowledge** — all answers are in the passage
4. For "author's tone" questions: look for positive/negative/neutral language
5. For "central theme" — find what ALL paragraphs have in common
6. For inference questions — the answer is **implied** not stated directly

## 🔑 Master Summary

| Rule | Applied When |
|------|-------------|
| Subject-verb: nearest subject in Neither/or | Agreement with complex subjects |
| Present/Past/Perfect + time keyword | Tense identification |
| Passive = Object becomes Subject + V₃ | Voice conversion |
| Since = point; For = duration | Since vs for |
| An before vowel **sound** not vowel letter | Article a/an |
| Direct→Indirect: tenses shift back one level | Reported speech |
