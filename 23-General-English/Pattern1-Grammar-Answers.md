# Grammar — Practice Problem Solutions

### Q1

**❓ Question:** Choose the correct form: "She _____ to school every day." (go/goes/going/gone)

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: Subject = "She" (3rd person singular), sentence describes a daily habit
- Find: Correct verb form

**💡 What I'll use:** Simple Present tense rule — 3rd person singular (he/she/it) adds "-s" or "-es" to verb

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: Subject is "She" → 3rd person singular.

Step 2: Sentence describes a daily routine → Simple Present tense.

Step 3: Base verb "go" + 3rd person singular rule → **goes**

**✅ Answer: (B) goes**

### Q2

**❓ Question:** Fill in article: "I want to be ___ engineer." (a/an/the/no article)

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: The word that follows is "engineer"
- Find: Correct article (a or an)

**💡 What I'll use:** Article rule — use "an" before words starting with a vowel SOUND

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: "engineer" starts with the vowel sound /e/ (like "en-gi-neer")

Step 2: Vowel sound → use **an**

**✅ Answer: (B) an** — "I want to be an engineer"

### Q3

**❓ Question:** Choose preposition: "I have been here ___ Monday." (since/for/from/during)

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: "Monday" = a specific point in time
- Find: Correct preposition with present perfect

**💡 What I'll use:** Since vs For rule — "since" for a specific point in time; "for" for a duration

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: "Monday" is a specific day (a point in time, not a duration like "3 days").

Step 2: Specific time point → use **since**

**✅ Answer: (A) since** — "I have been here since Monday"

### Q6

**❓ Question:** Find the error: "She is angry on him."

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A sentence with a preposition error
- Find: The incorrect word and the correct one

**💡 What I'll use:** Correct idiom — "angry with" (not "angry on") for being angry at a person

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: "angry on" is incorrect — not standard English.

Step 2: The correct idiom for being angry at a person is "angry **with**"

Step 3: Error word: **"on"** → should be **"with"**

**✅ Answer: Error: "on" → Correct: "angry with him"**

### Q8

**❓ Question:** Choose tense: "By tomorrow, I _____ this book." (will read/will have read/read)

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: "By tomorrow" = an action that will be completed BEFORE a future time
- Find: Correct tense

**💡 What I'll use:** Future Perfect tense — used for actions completed before another future point

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: "By tomorrow" signals that the action will be done and completed before tomorrow arrives.

Step 2: Action completed before a future point → **Future Perfect Tense**

Step 3: Future Perfect = will have + past participle = **will have read**

**✅ Answer: (B) will have read**

### Q9

**❓ Question:** Find the error: "I don't know nothing about this."

**🤔 What I understood:**
- Given: A sentence with two negative words
- Find: The grammatical error

**💡 What I'll use:** Double negative rule — two negatives in one clause cancel out or are grammatically wrong in standard English

**✏️ My Solution:**

Step 1: "don't" = first negative. "nothing" = second negative.

Step 2: Using two negatives = double negative (grammatically incorrect in formal English).

Step 3: Correct form: "I don't know **anything** about this" OR "I know **nothing** about this"

Step 4: Error: **"nothing"** → should be **"anything"**

**✅ Answer: Error: double negative — "nothing" should be "anything"**
