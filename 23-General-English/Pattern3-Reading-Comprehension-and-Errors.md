# Pattern 3: Reading Comprehension and Sentence Correction

## 🔍 When This Comes Up

- A passage is given with 3–5 questions
- "Find the error in the sentence"
- "Which option correctly fills the blank?"
- "Choose the sentence that has no grammatical error"

---

## 📐 Part 1: Common Errors to Avoid

### Error 1: Wrong Article
❌ I have a umbrella.
✅ I have **an** umbrella. (vowel sound)

❌ He is a honest person.
✅ He is **an** honest person. (h is silent)

---

### Error 2: Wrong Preposition
❌ She is angry on him.
✅ She is angry **with** him.

❌ I am married with her.
✅ I am married **to** her.

❌ He is good in English.
✅ He is good **at** English.

❌ Congratulations for your promotion.
✅ Congratulations **on** your promotion.

---

### Error 3: Double Negative
❌ I don't know nothing.
✅ I don't know **anything**. (OR: I know **nothing**.)

---

### Error 4: Subject-Verb Agreement
❌ The team are playing well.
✅ The team **is** playing well. (collective noun = singular)

❌ Either of them are correct.
✅ Either of them **is** correct. (either/neither = singular)

❌ The news are shocking.
✅ The news **is** shocking. (news = uncountable = singular)

---

### Error 5: Misuse of Since/For
- **Since** + point in time: since Monday, since 1990
- **For** + duration: for 3 days, for two hours

❌ I have been here since two hours.
✅ I have been here **for** two hours.

---

### Error 6: Wrong Comparative/Superlative
❌ He is more taller than me.
✅ He is **taller** than me. (not "more taller" — double comparison)

❌ She is the most tallest girl.
✅ She is the **tallest** girl. (not "most tallest")

---

### Error 7: Wrong Pronoun
❌ Between you and I, it is wrong.
✅ Between you and **me**, it is wrong. (object pronoun after preposition)

---

## 📐 Part 2: Reading Comprehension Strategy

### 60-Second Approach:
1. **Read the questions FIRST** (before the passage)
2. **Skim the passage** for keywords related to questions
3. **Read relevant sentences** carefully
4. **Choose** the most directly supported answer (don't assume)

### Tips:
- Answers are usually **directly stated** in the passage
- "Main idea" questions → look at **topic sentence** of each paragraph
- "The author implies..." → look for **indirect statements**
- Eliminate obviously wrong answers first
- Don't use **outside knowledge** — stay with what's in the passage

## ✅ Step-by-Step Examples

### Example 1 (Sentence Correction)

**Q:** Find the error: "Neither of the boys were present."
(A) Neither  (B) of  (C) boys  (D) were

- "Neither" is singular → requires singular verb
- Error: "were" should be "was"
- **Answer: (D) were** ✅

---

### Example 2 (Sentence Correction)

**Q:** Find the error: "I have been living here since three years."
(A) have been  (B) living  (C) here  (D) since

- Three years = duration → needs "for", not "since"
- **Answer: (D) since** ✅

---

### Example 3 (Sentence Correction)

**Q:** Choose the grammatically correct sentence:
(A) He is more smarter than his brother.
(B) He is smarter than his brother.
(C) He is most smart among all.
(D) He is smart more than his brother.

- (A) has "more smarter" = double comparison ✗
- (B) correct comparative ✓
- **Answer: (B)** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Fill in the blank)

**Q:** "She has been learning French ___ six months."
(A) since  (B) for  (C) from  (D) during

- Six months = duration → **for**
- **Answer: (B) for** ✅

---

### Example 5 (Reading Comprehension)

**Passage:** "The Amazon rainforest, often called the 'lungs of the Earth', produces 20% of the world's oxygen. It is home to millions of species. However, deforestation is threatening its existence. If current rates continue, scientists estimate that the Amazon could be completely deforested within a century."

**Q1:** What percentage of world's oxygen does Amazon produce?
→ "produces 20% of the world's oxygen" — **20%** ✅

**Q2:** What does "lungs of the Earth" imply?
→ It produces oxygen (like lungs produce oxygen for body) — **The Amazon produces oxygen for Earth** ✅

**Q3:** According to the passage, what threatens the Amazon?
→ "deforestation is threatening its existence" — **Deforestation** ✅

## ⚡ 60-Second Summary

**For Sentence Correction:**
1. Read the whole sentence for obvious errors
2. Check: articles (a/an/the), prepositions, verb agreement, tense
3. Eliminate options with clear errors
4. Choose the cleanest, most standard English

**Common traps in exams:**
- Double comparatives: "more better" ❌ → "better" ✅
- Double negatives: "don't know nothing" ❌ → "don't know anything" ✅
- Wrong preposition with fixed phrases: angry with (not on), good at (not in)
- Since vs For: since = time point, for = duration

"Exercise is essential for maintaining good health. Regular physical activity reduces the risk of chronic diseases such as diabetes, heart disease, and obesity. Even 30 minutes of moderate exercise daily can make a significant difference. Despite knowing its benefits, many people find it difficult to maintain an exercise routine due to busy schedules."
